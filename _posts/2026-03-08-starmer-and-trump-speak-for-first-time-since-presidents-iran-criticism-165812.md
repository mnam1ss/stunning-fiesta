UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer and former US President Donald Trump held their first conversation since Trump’s recent public comments on Iran. The phone call took place following a period where Trump had voiced significant dissatisfaction with the British leader’s approach to Iranian policy.

A brief statement from the Prime Minister’s office confirmed the discussion occurred, describing it as a standard diplomatic engagement between allies. The call is seen as an initial step in establishing a working relationship between the two figures, given Trump’s potential return to office following the upcoming US election. No specific details of their conversation regarding Iran or other international matters were released by either side.

Key points:
*   The conversation marks the first direct contact between Starmer and Trump since the latter's criticisms.
*   It follows a week of public remarks by Trump expressing frustration over UK policy toward Iran.
*   Official details of the call's substance were not disclosed by either party.
