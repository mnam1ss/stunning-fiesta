Two students have died following a meningitis outbreak at the University of Kent. According to reports, approximately 11 other students from the university are currently receiving hospital treatment for serious illness related to the incident.

University officials and local health authorities have confirmed the deaths and are coordinating a public health response. They are urging students and staff to be vigilant for symptoms associated with the disease, which can include a high fever, severe headache, stiff neck, and a distinctive rash. Health teams are providing information and support on campus.

The university has expressed its condolences to the families and friends of those who have died and is offering support to the wider student community during this time.

Key points:
- Two University of Kent students have died from meningitis.
- Around 11 other students are seriously ill in hospital.
- Health authorities are conducting awareness and support efforts on campus.
