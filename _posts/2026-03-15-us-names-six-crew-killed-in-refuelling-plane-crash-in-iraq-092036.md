The United States military has identified the six service members who died in a recent aircraft crash in Iraq. The incident involved a KC-135 Stratotanker, a refueling plane, which went down in a remote area of western Anbar province. According to official statements, the aircraft was conducting a combat mission in support of ongoing U.S. military operations at the time of the crash on Thursday. An investigation into the cause is underway, though initial reports from the Pentagon indicate no hostile fire was involved. The names of the deceased, all U.S. Air Force personnel, were released by the Department of Defense following notification of their families.

Key points:
• The U.S. has released the identities of six Air Force crew members killed in a KC-135 crash in western Iraq.
• The refueling aircraft was on a combat mission supporting ongoing U.S. operations when it crashed.
• An investigation is ongoing, with officials stating there is no evidence the crash resulted from enemy action.
