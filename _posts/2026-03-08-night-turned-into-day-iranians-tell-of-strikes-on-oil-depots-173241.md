Massive fires erupted at fuel storage facilities in two Iranian cities overnight, with residents reporting the sky was illuminated as if it were daytime. The incidents occurred at depots in Tehran and the nearby city of Karaj. Social media footage from the area showed large explosions followed by towering columns of smoke rising from multiple sites.

Emergency services responded to the blazes, though the full extent of any casualties or damage is not yet clear. Officials have not provided an immediate statement on the cause of the incidents. The events have drawn significant attention online, with local witnesses describing the intense brightness from the fires.

Key points:
- Major fires struck fuel depots in Tehran and Karaj.
- Videos documented large explosions and significant smoke plumes.
- The cause of the incidents is currently unconfirmed by authorities.
