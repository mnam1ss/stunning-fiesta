**Coastguard Team Assists Owner in Cliffside Dog Rescue**

A coastguard team in the United Kingdom recently conducted an unusual rescue operation to retrieve a dog stranded on a cliff ledge. The incident involved a cocker spaniel named Luna, who had become trapped and was reportedly too frightened to approach the emergency responders.

According to reports, the coastguard team determined the safest method was to lower the dog's owner down the cliff face to reach the animal. The owner was then able to secure Luna, and both were safely hauled back to the top. The operation concluded without injury to the dog, its owner, or the rescue personnel. Officials have reiterated standard safety advice for walkers to keep pets on leads near cliff edges.

Key points:
*   A coastguard team rescued a cocker spaniel trapped on a cliff by lowering the dog's owner to the location.
*   The dog was reportedly too fearful to be approached directly by the rescue crew.
*   The operation was completed successfully with no reported injuries.
