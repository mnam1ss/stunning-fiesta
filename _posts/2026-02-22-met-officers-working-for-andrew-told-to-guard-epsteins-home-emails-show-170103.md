**Emails Reveal Met Officers Assigned to Prince Andrew Also Guarded Epstein Property**

Newly disclosed emails indicate that officers from London's Metropolitan Police, assigned to protect Prince Andrew, were also instructed to provide security at a London residence owned by the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The information comes from documents released as part of a U.S. court case. The Metropolitan Police Service has stated that its ongoing review of the matter has not, to date, found evidence of misconduct by its protection officers in relation to these duties. The force has emphasized that its officers' activities were in line with their protective security responsibilities. The disclosure adds further context to the connections between the Duke of York and the convicted sex offender.

Key points:
*   Correspondence shows Met protection officers for Prince Andrew were detailed to guard a London home of Jeffrey Epstein.
*   The Metropolitan Police state their review has not currently identified any wrongdoing by the officers involved.
*   The force maintains the activities were conducted within the scope of official protective security functions.
