A British national has been formally charged in Dubai following allegations that he filmed Iranian missiles in the United Arab Emirates. The individual, whose identity has not been publicly released, is currently detained as legal proceedings move forward.

Officials from the UK's Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office have confirmed they are in contact with local authorities regarding the case. They are also providing consular assistance to the man's relatives.

The specific details of the charges and the circumstances under which the filming allegedly occurred have not been fully disclosed by Emirati officials. The case highlights the strict security laws in the UAE concerning the unauthorized recording of sensitive sites and military assets.

Key points:
*   A British man is facing charges in Dubai for allegedly recording Iranian missiles.
*   UK Foreign Office representatives are engaged and supporting the detainee's family.
*   The incident underscores the stringent local regulations on filming security installations.
