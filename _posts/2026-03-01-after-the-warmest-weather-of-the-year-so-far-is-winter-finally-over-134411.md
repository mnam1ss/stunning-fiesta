**Meteorological Spring Begins Amid Unseasonably Warm Temperatures**

The UK has recorded its highest temperatures of the year so far this week, coinciding with the official start of meteorological spring on March 1st. This recent warm spell has prompted questions about whether the winter season has concluded. Meteorologists note that while the calendar shift to spring is a fixed date used for climatological record-keeping, the actual weather patterns remain variable.

The recent warmth is attributed to a high-pressure system bringing southerly winds. Experts caution that despite the current mild conditions, colder periods and frosts remain possible in the coming weeks, as is typical for early spring. The transition between seasons is often gradual, with fluctuations in temperature expected.

Key points:
*   Meteorological spring began on March 1, distinct from the astronomical spring beginning later in the month.
*   Recent record warmth for the year is linked to a specific high-pressure weather pattern.
*   Forecasters advise that colder spells may still occur, and variable conditions are common in early spring.
