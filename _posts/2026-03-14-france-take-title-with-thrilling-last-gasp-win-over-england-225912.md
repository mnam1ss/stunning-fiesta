France secured the Six Nations rugby championship on Saturday with a narrow victory over England in Paris. The match concluded with a dramatic late penalty kick by French full-back Thomas Ramos, breaking a tie in the final moments. The high-scoring contest saw a combined total of 94 points from both teams.

The win marks France's third Six Nations title in the last five years. The tournament, contested annually by England, France, Ireland, Italy, Scotland, and Wales, concluded with this decisive final fixture. The result in Paris determined the overall champion after a competitive five-weekend schedule.

Key points:
* France won the Six Nations championship following a last-minute victory over England.
* The decisive points came from a penalty kick by Thomas Ramos in the closing stages.
* This victory represents France's third tournament win in the past five editions.
