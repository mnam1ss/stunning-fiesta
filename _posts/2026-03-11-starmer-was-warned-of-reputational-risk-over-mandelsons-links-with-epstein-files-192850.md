Newly released documents indicate that UK Labour Party leader Sir Keir Starmer was previously advised about potential reputational concerns regarding Lord Peter Mandelson's connections to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The files, which have come to light, show that these warnings were part of a broader review of Mandelson's role and history.

The documents further reveal that following his dismissal from a position as the UK's trade envoy to the United States, Lord Mandelson inquired about the possibility of receiving a severance payment reportedly amounting to £500,000. This inquiry was made despite his role being an unpaid appointment. The information adds new details to the public record concerning the peer's departure from the post.

Key points:
*   Files show Sir Keir Starmer received warnings about reputational risks tied to Lord Mandelson's Epstein links.
*   Lord Mandelson explored a potential £500,000 severance payment after being removed as an unpaid US trade envoy.
*   The released documents provide additional context on the circumstances surrounding Mandelson's departure from the role.
