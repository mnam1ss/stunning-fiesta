President Trump's policy toward Iran, which has centered on applying maximum pressure, is increasingly viewed as a high-stakes strategy aimed at prompting a change in the country's leadership. Analysts suggest this approach carries significant risk, potentially leading to broader military conflict in the region. Such a development could present a major political challenge for the administration, requiring it to manage a complex foreign engagement while simultaneously navigating domestic political debates.

The strategy has involved withdrawing from an international nuclear agreement and reinstating severe economic sanctions. Recent escalations, including the killing of a top Iranian general, have heightened tensions. Observers note the administration now faces the possibility of an extended confrontation that would demand substantial diplomatic and military resources.

Key points:
* The U.S. strategy is seen as a deliberate attempt to pressure Iran's government.
* Escalating tensions raise the prospect of a wider regional conflict.
* The administration may need to handle a foreign crisis amid domestic political pressures.
