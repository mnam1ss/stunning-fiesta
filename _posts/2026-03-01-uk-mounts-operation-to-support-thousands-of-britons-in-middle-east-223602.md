UK authorities are preparing contingency plans to assist British citizens currently in the Middle East, according to government sources. The operation is focused on ensuring support and potential evacuation routes for thousands of nationals if regional airspace becomes inaccessible. Officials are assessing various logistical options, though no formal evacuation order has been issued. The planning is described as a precautionary measure in response to the current regional instability. The Foreign Office has not released detailed public statements on the operational specifics but continues to advise travelers to monitor its guidance and register their presence abroad.

Key points:
- UK officials are developing contingency plans for British nationals in the Middle East.
- The focus is on potential evacuation support should air travel be disrupted.
- The Foreign Office advises citizens in the region to stay informed via official travel guidance.
