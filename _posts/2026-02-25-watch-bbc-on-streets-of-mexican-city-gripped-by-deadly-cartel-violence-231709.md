BBC correspondent reports from Mexican city after surge in cartel violence

A BBC news crew has documented conditions in the Mexican city of Culiacán following a significant escalation in local violence. The reporting team, led by an international correspondent, visited the capital of Sinaloa state to assess the situation after recent clashes.

Residents described a climate of fear as armed groups engaged in open confrontations in the streets. The violence is linked to ongoing conflicts between rival drug cartels and security forces. Local authorities have reported numerous fatalities from the recent incidents, which included shootings and blockades of major roads.

The report highlights the challenges faced by civilians caught in the crossfire, with many businesses forced to close and daily life severely disrupted. The city has experienced similar outbreaks of violence in the past, often tied to the powerful Sinaloa Cartel.

Key points:
• A BBC team reported from Culiacán, Mexico, after a major outbreak of violence.
• The clashes involved cartel gunmen and resulted in multiple reported deaths.
• The unrest caused widespread fear and disruption for the city's residents.
