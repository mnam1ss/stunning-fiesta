Scotland secured a decisive 30-21 victory over England in the Six Nations' Calcutta Cup match at Murrayfield Stadium on Saturday. The win marks Scotland's fourth consecutive home triumph in the historic rivalry.

After a surprising loss to Italy in the previous round, Scotland's performance rebounded significantly. The team earned a bonus point by scoring four tries during the match. England, despite a strong start, was unable to maintain its early lead against a determined Scottish side.

The result keeps Scotland in contention in the tournament standings while dealing a setback to England's championship aspirations. The atmosphere at a sold-out Murrayfield was reported as highly energetic throughout the contest.

Key points:
• Scotland defeated England 30-21 to retain the Calcutta Cup.
• The Scottish side scored four tries to secure a tournament bonus point.
• The victory follows Scotland's loss to Italy in the previous round of the Six Nations.
