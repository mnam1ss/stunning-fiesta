Ian Huntley, who was convicted of the murders of two schoolgirls in Soham in 2002, has been removed from life support, according to a BBC report. Huntley had been receiving hospital care since late February following an incident in prison where he sustained a head injury. The Ministry of Justice has not provided an official comment on his current medical status.

The 50-year-old is serving a life sentence for the murders of Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman, a case that profoundly impacted the United Kingdom. Prison authorities are expected to conduct an investigation into the circumstances that led to his hospitalization.

**Key points:**
- Ian Huntley, convicted of the 2002 Soham murders, has been taken off life support after a prison incident.
- He had been hospitalized with a head injury since the end of February.
- An investigation into the circumstances surrounding his injury is anticipated.
