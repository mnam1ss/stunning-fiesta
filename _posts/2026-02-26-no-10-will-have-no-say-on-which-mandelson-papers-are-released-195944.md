A parliamentary committee has announced that the government will not intervene in decisions regarding the release of official papers from Lord Peter Mandelson's time in office. The committee, composed of MPs and peers, stated it received confirmation that Downing Street will not overrule any determinations made about disclosing the files. This process falls under the standard 20-year rule for releasing historical government records.

The papers in question relate to Lord Mandelson's tenure as a minister under former Prime Ministers Tony Blair and Gordon Brown. The independent advisory committee responsible for reviewing such releases will now assess the documents without political interference from the Prime Minister's office. This ensures the decision rests solely on the standard criteria for withholding or making public historical government information.

Key points:
*   The UK government has confirmed it will not overrule decisions on releasing Lord Mandelson's ministerial papers.
*   An independent parliamentary committee will handle the review under standard historical record protocols.
*   The papers concern Mandelson's service in the Blair and Brown governments from 1997 to 2010.
