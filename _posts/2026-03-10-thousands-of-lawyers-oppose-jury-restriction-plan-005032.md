A significant number of legal professionals in the UK have publicly called on the government to reconsider a proposed change to the justice system. The reform would see jury trials eliminated for certain types of criminal cases. A letter expressing opposition has been signed by thousands of lawyers, among them prominent courtroom advocates and former members of the judiciary.

The signatories argue that the right to trial by jury is a fundamental principle. They contend that removing this right for specific offenses would undermine public confidence in the fairness of the legal process. The government has indicated the measure is intended to improve efficiency and reduce costs within the court system, but critics maintain it represents a substantial erosion of a long-standing legal safeguard.

Key points:
*   Thousands of legal professionals, including senior barristers and retired judges, oppose the government's plan.
*   The proposed reform would remove the right to a jury trial for some criminal offenses.
*   Opponents argue the change threatens a cornerstone of the justice system, while the government cites efficiency goals.
