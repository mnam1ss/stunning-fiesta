**Woman Reunited with Dog Eight Years After Theft**

A woman in the United Kingdom has been reunited with her pet dog eight years after the animal was stolen. Rebecca Harvey was contacted after a microchip scan identified the dog, named Flora, during a routine veterinary check. The dog had been missing since 2016.

Authorities facilitated the return after confirming the microchip registration. Harvey described the reunion as a highly emotional experience. The circumstances under which Flora went missing and where she has been for the past eight years were not detailed in initial reports.

The case highlights the importance of microchipping pets for permanent identification. Local police have acknowledged the incident but have not indicated if any charges are pending related to the original disappearance.

Key points:
*   A microchip scan led to the identification and return of a dog stolen in 2016.
*   Owner Rebecca Harvey was reunited with her pet, Flora, after eight years.
*   The incident underscores the role of microchipping in recovering lost or stolen animals.
