**Spain to Implement New Gibraltar Border Checks Under Post-Brexit Agreement**

Spain is set to introduce new border control procedures for travelers entering Gibraltar, following the finalization of a new treaty between the United Kingdom and the European Union. The agreement, which addresses Gibraltar's status after Brexit, will require individuals crossing from Spain into the British Overseas Territory to undergo passport inspections twice.

Under the forthcoming system, Spanish authorities will conduct checks at a new facility located at the port and airport in Gibraltar. This arrangement is part of a broader framework intended to facilitate fluidity of movement while maintaining security controls for entry into the EU's Schengen Area. The deal, which has been under negotiation since the UK's departure from the EU, aims to establish a stable long-term operational model for the Gibraltar-Spain frontier.

Key points:
*   A new UK-EU treaty mandates dual passport checks for entry into Gibraltar from Spain.
*   Spanish officials will operate the controls from facilities within Gibraltar's port and airport.
*   The agreement seeks to balance border security with maintaining cross-border movement.
