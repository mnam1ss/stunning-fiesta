American alpine skier Lindsey Vonn underwent surgery for a fracture in her left leg following a crash during a World Cup event in Cortina d'Ampezzo, Italy. The incident occurred during the women's downhill competition, an event for which Vonn had been preparing extensively. Medical personnel attended to her on the slope before she was transported for further evaluation, which confirmed the injury necessitated an operation.

The crash represents a significant setback for the veteran skier, who has previously discussed the possibility of retiring from competitive skiing. While not officially confirmed as her final race, the timing of the injury during a major competition has led to widespread speculation about the future of her career. Vonn's team has stated that updates on her condition and recovery timeline will be provided following the surgical procedure.

Key points:
• Lindsey Vonn sustained a left-leg fracture in a crash during a World Cup downhill race in Cortina.
• The injury required surgical intervention, with her recovery timeline pending further updates.
• The crash has prompted questions about the potential end of the 39-year-old skier's competitive career.
