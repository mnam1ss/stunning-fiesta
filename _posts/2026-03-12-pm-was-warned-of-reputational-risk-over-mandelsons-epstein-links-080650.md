Newly released documents indicate the Prime Minister was previously advised about potential reputational damage concerning Lord Mandelson’s connections to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The records form part of a broader disclosure of official papers.

The documents further reveal that following his dismissal from a role as the UK's trade envoy to the United States, Lord Mandelson inquired about the possibility of receiving a severance payment reportedly valued at half a million pounds. The released information does not confirm whether such a payment was ever made.

These disclosures contribute to ongoing public scrutiny of associations with Epstein, who was a convicted sex offender and died in prison while awaiting trial on further charges.

Key points:
• The Prime Minister received warnings about reputational risks linked to Lord Mandelson’s Epstein ties.
• Lord Mandelson explored a potential £500,000 severance payment after his removal as a US trade envoy.
• The information emerges from official documents released to the public.
