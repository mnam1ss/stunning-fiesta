A newspaper editor has testified in a high-profile privacy trial that she kept knowledge of a royal pregnancy confidential for several days. Charlotte Griffiths, the former Mail on Sunday editor, made the statement while giving evidence in a case brought by a group of high-profile individuals, including Prince Harry and Sir Elton John.

The claimants allege that journalists and private investigators working for Associated Newspapers, the publisher of the Daily Mail and Mail on Sunday, engaged in unlawful information gathering. During her testimony, Griffiths denied personally engaging in phone hacking or commissioning the work of a private investigator. The trial is examining allegations of widespread privacy breaches spanning several decades.

Key points:
* An editor testified to withholding news of Catherine, Princess of Wales's pregnancy while at the Mail on Sunday.
* The privacy case is being brought by several high-profile figures against Associated Newspapers.
* The editor denied any personal involvement in phone hacking or hiring private investigators.
