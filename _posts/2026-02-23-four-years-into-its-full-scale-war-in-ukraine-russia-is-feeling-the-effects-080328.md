Four years after launching its full-scale invasion of Ukraine, Russia is experiencing significant domestic economic strain. International sanctions and the substantial costs of the military campaign have impacted the national economy and the daily lives of citizens. Reports indicate that while official statistics show some resilience, many ordinary Russians are facing higher prices for goods and services, contributing to a noticeable decline in living standards for a portion of the population.

The situation has prompted various coping strategies among residents, including seeking additional employment and adjusting household budgets. The long-term economic outlook remains a subject of analysis, with the ongoing conflict continuing to shape fiscal and trade policies.

Key points:
• The prolonged war has imposed considerable economic costs on Russia, exacerbated by international sanctions.
• Many citizens report a decrease in purchasing power and are adapting their financial habits.
• The full-scale invasion, now in its fourth year, continues to influence both national policy and individual livelihoods.
