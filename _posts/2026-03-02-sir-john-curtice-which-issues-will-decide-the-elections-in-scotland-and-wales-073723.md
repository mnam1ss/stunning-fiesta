A new poll commissioned by the BBC has identified the primary concerns likely to influence voters in Scotland and Wales ahead of the 7 May elections. The analysis, provided by polling expert Sir John Curtice, highlights the distinct political landscapes and policy priorities in each nation as voters prepare to cast their ballots.

In Scotland, constitutional questions continue to dominate the political debate, with the issue of independence remaining a significant factor for many voters. The poll suggests this theme is central to how parties are being evaluated. Meanwhile, in Wales, the survey indicates a greater focus on domestic matters such as public services and the management of the National Health Service. The findings provide a snapshot of the electorate's key considerations as campaigning intensifies.

Key points:
*   A new BBC-commissioned poll examines voter priorities ahead of the 7 May elections in Scotland and Wales.
*   Constitutional issues, including independence, are highlighted as major factors in Scotland.
*   Voters in Wales appear more focused on domestic concerns like healthcare and public services.
