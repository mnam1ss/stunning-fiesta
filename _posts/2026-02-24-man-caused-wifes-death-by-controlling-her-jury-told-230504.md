A man is on trial for the manslaughter of his wife, who died by suicide in 2017. The prosecution alleges that Christopher Trybus caused the death of Tarryn Baird through a pattern of controlling and coercive behavior. Trybus has pleaded not guilty to the charge.

The court heard that the alleged controlling conduct occurred over a period of time prior to Ms. Baird's death. The prosecution's case centers on the argument that this behavior was a significant factor in the circumstances leading to her death. The defense is contesting these allegations as the trial proceeds.

Key points:
- Christopher Trybus denies the manslaughter of his wife, Tarryn Baird.
- The prosecution claims coercive control was a factor in her 2017 death.
- The trial is ongoing to determine the facts of the case.
