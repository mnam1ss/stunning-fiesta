Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has been killed, according to a report from Iranian state television. The announcement followed the first day of extensive aerial bombardments by the United States and Israel targeting locations within Iran. Khamenei, who served as the country's ultimate political and religious authority, had maintained leadership for nearly four decades.

The confirmation of his death marks a significant moment for the Islamic Republic, which he led since 1989. The reported airstrikes represent a major escalation in regional tensions. Further details regarding the circumstances of his death or the full scope of the military operations have not been immediately provided by authorities.

Key points:
- Iranian state media reports Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has been killed.
- The announcement followed large-scale US and Israeli airstrikes on Iran.
- Khamenei had been the nation's paramount leader for almost 40 years.
