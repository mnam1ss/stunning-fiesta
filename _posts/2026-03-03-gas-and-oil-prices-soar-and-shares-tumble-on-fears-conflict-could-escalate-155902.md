Global energy markets experienced significant volatility on Monday following heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. A senior Iranian official issued a warning regarding maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a critical waterway for global oil shipments. This statement contributed to investor concerns that regional conflict could expand, impacting supply routes.

The immediate reaction saw a sharp increase in the price of crude oil and natural gas. Concurrently, major stock indices in Europe and Asia declined as market participants shifted towards safer assets. Analysts note that the Strait of Hormuz is a vital chokepoint, with approximately one-fifth of the world's oil supply passing through it. Any disruption to shipping in the area has historically led to market instability.

Key points:
*   Energy prices rose sharply due to supply disruption fears.
*   Stock markets fell as investors sought safer holdings.
*   The focus is on the Strait of Hormuz, a crucial passage for global oil exports.
