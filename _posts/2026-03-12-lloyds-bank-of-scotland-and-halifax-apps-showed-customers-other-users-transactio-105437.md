**Banking Apps Displayed Other Customers' Transaction Information**

Customers of several major UK banks experienced a significant data privacy issue on Thursday when their mobile banking applications erroneously displayed financial information belonging to other users. The incident affected digital services operated by Lloyds Bank, Bank of Scotland, and Halifax, all of which are part of Lloyds Banking Group.

Numerous account holders reported logging into their banking apps only to find they could view detailed transaction histories, including payments and charges, from accounts that were not their own. The banking group has confirmed it is investigating the technical fault and stated that the problem has now been resolved. The company apologized for the disruption and emphasized that no personal data was compromised beyond the temporary display of incorrect information. Financial regulators have been notified of the breach.

**Key points:**
*   A technical fault in apps for Lloyds, Halifax, and Bank of Scotland showed customers other users' transactions.
*   The issue has been resolved, with the bank stating no personal data was compromised beyond the visible error.
*   The banking group has apologized and notified the appropriate financial regulators of the incident.
