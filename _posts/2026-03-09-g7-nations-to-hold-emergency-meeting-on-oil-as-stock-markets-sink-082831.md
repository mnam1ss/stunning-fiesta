Finance ministers from the Group of Seven nations are convening an emergency meeting to address concerns over global oil markets and financial instability. The talks, scheduled for this week, will include UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves. The meeting follows a significant downturn in global stock markets and rising pressure on energy supplies.

Discussions are expected to focus on potential coordinated actions to stabilize markets. Among the options reportedly under consideration is a strategic release of national oil reserves, a measure aimed at increasing supply to help lower soaring fuel prices. The move comes as economic uncertainty and inflation continue to weigh on major economies.

Key points:
* G7 finance ministers are holding emergency talks amid falling stock markets and high oil prices.
* Coordinated action, including a possible release of strategic oil reserves, is on the agenda.
* UK Chancellor Rachel Reeves will participate in the discussions.
