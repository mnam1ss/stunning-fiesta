Cuban authorities have reported the fatal shooting of four individuals aboard a speedboat registered in the United States. According to an official government statement, the incident occurred in Cuban territorial waters. The statement alleges the passengers were Cuban nationals residing in the U.S. and that the vessel was attempting an unauthorized entry with the intent to commit acts of terrorism.

The event has drawn attention amid ongoing tensions between the two nations. U.S. officials have not yet issued a formal response to the specific allegations made by the Cuban government. The incident is under investigation by Cuban authorities, who have provided no further details on the alleged terrorist objectives.

Key points:
• Cuban forces shot and killed four people on a U.S.-registered speedboat in its waters.
• The Cuban government alleges the individuals were U.S.-based nationals planning a terrorist infiltration.
• The U.S. has not yet publicly responded to these specific claims from Havana.
