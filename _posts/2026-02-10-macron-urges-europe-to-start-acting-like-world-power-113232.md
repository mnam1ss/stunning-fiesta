French President Emmanuel Macron has called for a more assertive and unified European foreign policy, stating the continent must begin to operate as a major global power. He delivered these remarks in a recent address, highlighting a series of external challenges.

President Macron identified strategic and economic pressures from multiple directions as a primary concern. He specifically pointed to the actions of China and Russia, and also noted recent policy shifts from the United States. According to Macron, these combined factors represent a significant challenge that requires a fundamental change in Europe's approach to its own defense and diplomatic strategy. He described the current situation as a critical moment for European leaders and institutions.

Key points:
*   President Macron advocates for Europe to adopt a more cohesive and powerful role in world affairs.
*   He cited challenges posed by China, Russia, and recent U.S. policies as reasons for this shift.
*   The French leader characterized the geopolitical climate as a pivotal moment for European strategic autonomy.
