UK Economic Growth Stalls in January

The UK economy showed no growth in January, according to official figures released by the Office for National Statistics. The data indicates a pause in economic activity at the start of the year, following a slight expansion in the previous month. A significant factor in the flat performance was a notable decline in consumer spending within the services sector, particularly in restaurants and other food service establishments. This contraction offset gains observed in other areas, such as retail sales. The reported stagnation fell short of the modest growth that many financial analysts had projected for the period.

Key points:
* Official statistics show the UK economy recorded zero growth in January.
* Reduced consumer spending on dining out contributed to the overall flat performance.
* The outcome was below the growth expectations of many economic forecasters.
