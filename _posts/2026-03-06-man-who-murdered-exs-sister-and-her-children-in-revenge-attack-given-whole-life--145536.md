A man has been sentenced to life in prison without the possibility of parole for a fatal arson attack that killed a woman and her two children. The court heard that Sharaz Ali deliberately set fire to the family's home in an act of revenge after his former partner, the victim's sister, ended their relationship. The fire resulted in the deaths of Bryonie Gawith and her children.

The judge imposed a whole-life order, meaning the defendant will never be considered for release. During the sentencing, the judge described the crime as a premeditated act of exceptional cruelty that devastated an entire family. Emergency services responded to the incident, but were unable to save the victims.

Key points:
- Sharaz Ali received a whole-life sentence for murder via arson.
- The attack targeted his ex-partner's sister and her two children.
- The judge cited the crime's premeditation and devastating impact.
