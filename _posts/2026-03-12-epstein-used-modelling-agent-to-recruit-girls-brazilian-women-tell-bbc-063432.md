Two Brazilian women have told the BBC that a modeling agent was used to recruit them and other young women for the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. According to their account, the agent leveraged his businesses to identify potential recruits and also assisted in arranging U.S. visas for their travel.

The women allege the process involved promises of modeling opportunities. Their statements form part of ongoing reporting into the methods used to facilitate access to Epstein, who died in jail in 2019 while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges. The BBC report cites these new firsthand allegations from individuals in Brazil.

Key points:
- Two Brazilian women allege a modeling agent recruited for Epstein.
- The agent reportedly used his businesses to find women and secure visas.
- These claims add to ongoing investigations into Epstein's network.
