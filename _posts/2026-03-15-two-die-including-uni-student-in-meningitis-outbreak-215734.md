Two people have died and eleven others are seriously ill in a meningitis outbreak in the Canterbury area, according to reports. One of the deceased has been identified as a university student.

Local health authorities have confirmed the presence of the disease and are conducting an urgent investigation to identify the source and contain its spread. Public health officials are contacting individuals who may have been in close contact with those infected.

The outbreak has prompted heightened awareness at local universities and student accommodations, where health information is being distributed. Medical professionals are urging anyone experiencing symptoms such as a high fever, severe headache, stiff neck, or a rash to seek immediate medical attention.

Key points:
- Two fatalities, including a student, have been confirmed.
- Eleven additional cases are being treated in hospital.
- Health officials are conducting contact tracing and urging public vigilance.
