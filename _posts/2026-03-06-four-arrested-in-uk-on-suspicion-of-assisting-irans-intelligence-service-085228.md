Four individuals have been detained in the United Kingdom following a counter-terrorism investigation. The arrests, which occurred on Friday, were made on suspicion of offenses related to assisting a foreign state's intelligence service. According to official statements, the activity in question is believed to be connected to Iran. The operation was conducted by officers from the Metropolitan Police's Counter Terrorism Command across multiple locations in London and the surrounding area. All four men remain in police custody for questioning. Authorities have stated that the investigation is ongoing and that there is no immediate threat to the wider public in connection with this specific operation.

Key points:
- Four men were arrested in the UK on Friday in a counter-terrorism operation.
- They are suspected of assisting Iran's intelligence service.
- Police state there is no immediate wider threat to public safety.
