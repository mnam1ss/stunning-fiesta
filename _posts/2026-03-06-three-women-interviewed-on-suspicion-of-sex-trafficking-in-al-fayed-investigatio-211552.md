Three women have been questioned by London's Metropolitan Police as part of an ongoing investigation into allegations of serious sexual offenses and human trafficking. The interviews were conducted on suspicion that the individuals aided and abetted these crimes. This development is connected to a wider inquiry that has drawn significant public attention in recent weeks.

Authorities have not released the identities of those interviewed, and no formal charges have been announced at this stage. The police emphasized that the investigation remains active and ongoing. The case is being handled by specialist detectives from the Met's dedicated crime command.

Key points:
- Three women were interviewed by police on suspicion of assisting rape and human trafficking.
- The interviews are part of a broader, active Metropolitan Police investigation.
- No charges have been filed, and the identities of those questioned have not been disclosed.
