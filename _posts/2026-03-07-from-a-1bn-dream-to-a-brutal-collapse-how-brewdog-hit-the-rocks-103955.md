BrewDog, the Scottish craft beer company founded in 2007, has reported a significant pre-tax loss of £24 million for 2023. This financial result marks a stark reversal for the brand, which had once aimed for a £1 billion valuation and sought to disrupt the traditional brewing sector. The company, which began as a small start-up, expanded rapidly through aggressive marketing and equity crowdfunding campaigns involving thousands of investors.

The recent figures highlight ongoing challenges, including a slowdown in growth and increased operational costs. Company statements have cited difficult market conditions and necessary investments for future strategy. The report follows a period of public scrutiny over the firm's workplace culture and business practices in recent years.

Key points:
• BrewDog recorded a £24 million pre-tax loss in 2023, contrasting with earlier growth ambitions.
• The company experienced rapid expansion from its 2007 founding but now faces a market slowdown.
• Its performance follows previous controversies regarding its internal culture and business operations.
