Labour MP Joani Reid has announced she is voluntarily stepping back from her parliamentary duties following the arrest of her husband in connection with an investigation into suspected espionage for China.

Reid stated she made the decision to temporarily suspend herself from the party to avoid any distraction from her colleagues' work. The arrest was made by authorities as part of an ongoing national security probe. No further details about the specific allegations have been made public at this stage.

The MP represents a constituency in London and has served since the last general election. Parliamentary officials have acknowledged her decision, stating it is a matter for the Labour Party's internal processes. The investigation into her husband remains active.

Key points:
* Labour MP Joani Reid has voluntarily suspended herself from party duties.
* The move follows her husband's arrest in a national security investigation related to suspected spying for China.
* The investigation is ongoing, and no further case details have been released.
