BBC journalists have spent a day embedded with emergency response teams in southern Lebanon, documenting the ongoing cross-border hostilities. Their reporting focused on the city of Nabatieh, where they observed the intensity of Israeli airstrikes. The coverage highlighted the operational challenges for civil defense crews as they respond to incidents. The conflict between Israel and the Lebanese armed group Hezbollah has seen a significant increase in military exchanges in recent weeks. The report provided a ground-level view of the situation as tensions continue to rise along the border.

Key points:
- BBC crews accompanied emergency teams in Nabatieh, southern Lebanon.
- Reporting detailed the frequency and impact of recent Israeli airstrikes.
- The conflict between Israel and Hezbollah has escalated in recent weeks.
