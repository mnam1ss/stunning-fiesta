A fire has severely damaged a historic building on Union Street in Glasgow, Scotland. This incident is part of a concerning pattern of fires affecting the city's older architectural structures in recent years. Emergency services responded to the blaze, which caused significant damage to the property. No injuries have been reported in connection with this latest fire. Local authorities and fire investigators are examining the site to determine the cause. The building is one of many in Glasgow that contribute to its distinctive historic character, raising questions about the frequency of such incidents.

Key points:
• A historic building on Glasgow's Union Street was recently damaged in a fire.
• This fire is the latest in a series of blazes impacting the city's older properties.
• Investigators are working to determine the cause, with no injuries reported.
