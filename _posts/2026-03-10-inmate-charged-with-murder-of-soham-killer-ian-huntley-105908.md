**Inmate Charged in Prison Death of Ian Huntley**

A prisoner has been formally charged with murder following the death of inmate Ian Huntley, according to police. Huntley, who was serving a life sentence for the 2002 murders of two schoolgirls in Soham, died after an incident at HM Prison Frankland in County Durham earlier this year. The suspect, another inmate, is scheduled to appear before magistrates. Authorities have not released further details regarding the circumstances of the alleged attack. The Ministry of Justice has confirmed an investigation into the death is ongoing, which is standard procedure following a fatality in custody. The case has drawn significant public attention due to the notoriety of Huntley's original crimes.

Key points:
*   A prisoner has been charged with murder in connection with the death of inmate Ian Huntley.
*   Huntley was convicted in 2003 for the murders of Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman in Soham.
*   The incident occurred at HM Prison Frankland, and an investigation is proceeding.
