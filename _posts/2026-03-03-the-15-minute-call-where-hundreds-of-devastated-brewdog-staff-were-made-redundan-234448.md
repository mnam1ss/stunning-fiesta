Hundreds of employees at the brewery and pub chain BrewDog were informed of redundancies during a brief company-wide call this week. The announcement follows the recent acquisition of the independent brewer by the American beverage and cannabis company Tilray in a deal valued at approximately £33 million. Staff members have described the notification process as abrupt and expressed feelings of devastation at the sudden job losses.

The acquisition by Tilray marks a significant shift for the Scottish-based company, which had grown from a craft beer startup into an international brand with numerous bars. Company leadership stated the restructuring was a difficult but necessary step following the change in ownership. Further details regarding the number of positions affected and severance packages have not been publicly disclosed.

Key points:
- BrewDog staff were notified of widespread layoffs during a 15-minute call.
- The redundancies follow Tilray's acquisition of the company for about £33 million.
- Employees have characterized the announcement as sudden and distressing.
