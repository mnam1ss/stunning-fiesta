Foreign Secretary David Cooper has stated that the United Kingdom will continue to act in its own national interests, following recent public criticism from US President Donald Trump. The comments come after the US leader questioned the UK Prime Minister's policy positions.

Speaking to reporters, Cooper acknowledged the close and historic alliance between the two nations but emphasized that agreement on every single issue is not guaranteed. He affirmed that the UK government's primary responsibility is to its own citizens and strategic goals.

The diplomatic exchange highlights ongoing discussions between the two governments, with Cooper reiterating the UK's commitment to its independent foreign policy stance while maintaining its strong transatlantic partnership.

Key points:
*   The UK Foreign Secretary has responded to criticism from the US President.
*   Officials state the UK will prioritize its own national interests in policy decisions.
*   The government emphasizes its enduring alliance with the US despite occasional disagreements.
