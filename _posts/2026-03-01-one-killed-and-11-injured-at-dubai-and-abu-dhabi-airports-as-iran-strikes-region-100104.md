**Debris from Intercepted Strikes Causes Casualties at UAE Airports**

One person was killed and at least eleven others were injured at airports in Dubai and Abu Dhabi early Sunday morning. The casualties and damage were caused by falling debris from unmanned aerial vehicles and missiles that were intercepted by regional defense systems. The projectiles were part of a larger aerial attack launched by Iran targeting locations across the Gulf region.

The incident caused significant disruption to air travel, with flights temporarily diverted or delayed at major international hubs. Emergency services responded to the scenes to treat the injured and assess structural damage to airport facilities. Authorities have launched investigations into the precise origins and flight paths of the intercepted weapons.

Key points:
*   One fatality and eleven injuries reported at Dubai and Abu Dhabi airports.
*   Damage caused by falling debris from downed drones and missiles.
*   The projectiles were part of a wider Iranian aerial assault on the Gulf region.
