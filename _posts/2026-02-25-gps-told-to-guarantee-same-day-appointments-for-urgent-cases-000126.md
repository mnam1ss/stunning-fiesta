New contractual terms for general practitioners in England will mandate that patients with urgent medical needs be offered an appointment on the same day they request one. The updated agreement, set to be implemented, aims to improve access to primary care by ensuring timely responses for pressing health concerns.

Under the forthcoming requirements, GP practices must assess patients seeking help and provide an immediate access option, such as a face-to-face consultation or a phone call, when a condition is deemed urgent. The change is part of a broader effort to address patient backlogs and streamline how primary care services handle demand. Health officials state the measure is intended to provide clarity and consistency for patients across the country.

Key points:
*   GP practices in England must offer same-day appointments for patients assessed as having urgent needs.
*   The requirement is part of a new national contract for general practitioners.
*   The policy aims to standardize and improve access to immediate primary care.
