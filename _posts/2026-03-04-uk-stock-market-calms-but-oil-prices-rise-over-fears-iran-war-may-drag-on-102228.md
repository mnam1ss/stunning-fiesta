UK financial markets showed signs of stabilization on Wednesday, opening slightly higher after a period of volatility. The FTSE 100 and other major European indices edged upward as investor anxiety over escalating Middle East tensions appeared to temper. However, energy markets told a different story, with crude oil prices continuing to climb. The increase is attributed to persistent concerns that the conflict involving Iran could prolong, potentially disrupting supply flows from a key oil-producing region. Natural gas prices also remained unstable, reflecting the broader uncertainty in commodity markets driven by geopolitical risk. Analysts note that while equities have found some temporary footing, the trajectory for energy costs remains highly sensitive to developments in the Middle East.

Key points:
- UK and European stock markets opened moderately higher, indicating a pause in recent selling pressure.
- Crude oil prices rose further due to fears that the Iran-Israel conflict may be protracted, threatening supply.
- Natural gas prices stayed volatile, underscoring ongoing market uncertainty linked to geopolitical instability.
