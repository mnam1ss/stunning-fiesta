A former nursery worker has been sentenced to 18 years in prison for a series of serious offenses against children. The sentencing took place at a court in England, where the judge described the defendant's actions as profoundly corrupt and indicative of a complete abandonment of ethical principles.

The individual, Vincent Chan, was convicted on multiple counts related to the abuse of young children at the nursery where he was employed. During the proceedings, the presiding judge stated that the crimes represented a severe breach of trust and were of a particularly disturbing nature.

The case has prompted a review of safeguarding procedures at the facility. Authorities have emphasized that the welfare of children remains their utmost priority.

Key points:
*   Vincent Chan received an 18-year prison sentence for crimes against children at a nursery.
*   The presiding judge characterized the offenses as deeply corrupt and a total breach of trust.
*   The case has led to a review of child protection measures at the involved institution.
