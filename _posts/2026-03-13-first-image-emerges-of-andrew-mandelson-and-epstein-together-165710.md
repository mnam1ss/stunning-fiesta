A previously unpublished photograph showing the late financier Jeffrey Epstein with Britain's Prince Andrew and former cabinet minister Lord Peter Mandelson has been made public. The image, reportedly taken in 1999, depicts the three men seated together on the deck of Epstein's private island in the U.S. Virgin Islands. Both Prince Andrew and Lord Mandelson are shown wearing bathrobes.

The emergence of the picture provides a new visual connection between the two British figures and Epstein, who died in jail in 2019 while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges. Prince Andrew previously settled a civil sexual assault case brought by one of Epstein's accusers, while Lord Mandelson has stated his visits to Epstein were for professional discussions and that he was unaware of any criminal activity.

Key points:
*   A new photograph shows Prince Andrew and Lord Mandelson with Jeffrey Epstein on his private island in 1999.
*   The image shows the two British men wearing bathrobes while seated with the financier.
*   The picture surfaces years after Epstein's death and following legal cases related to his conduct.
