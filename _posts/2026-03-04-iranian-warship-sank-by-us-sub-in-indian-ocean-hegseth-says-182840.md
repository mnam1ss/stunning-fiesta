A military vessel has sunk off the southern coast of Sri Lanka, according to the country's navy. Officials report that approximately 140 personnel are currently unaccounted for following the incident. The event has prompted a significant search and rescue operation in the area.

Separately, a claim has been made regarding a different naval incident. A commentator has stated that an Iranian warship was sunk by a U.S. submarine in the Indian Ocean. This assertion has not been independently verified by official military sources.

Authorities are focusing on the confirmed sinking near Sri Lanka, where efforts to locate the missing individuals are ongoing. The cause of that vessel's sinking has not been publicly disclosed.

Key points:
• A Sri Lankan naval vessel sank, with about 140 people feared missing.
• An unverified claim suggests an Iranian warship was sunk by a U.S. submarine elsewhere.
• Search operations are underway for the Sri Lankan incident.
