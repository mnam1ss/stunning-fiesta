Veteran alpine skier Lindsey Vonn is set to compete in a major race this Sunday while managing a significant knee injury. The event has drawn attention due to the severity of her condition, which involves damage to the anterior cruciate ligament. Former Olympic skier Chemmy Alcott commented on the situation, describing Vonn as a highly accomplished athlete who is pushing her physical limits by choosing to participate.

Her decision to race under these medical circumstances has sparked discussions about athlete endurance and risk. The upcoming competition will test her ability to perform while injured, a challenge familiar in high-stakes sports. Observers are noting the potential implications for her performance and long-term health.

Key points:
- Lindsey Vonn is competing with an anterior cruciate ligament injury.
- Her participation has been noted as a high-risk athletic endeavor.
- The race outcome may influence discussions on injury management in professional sports.
