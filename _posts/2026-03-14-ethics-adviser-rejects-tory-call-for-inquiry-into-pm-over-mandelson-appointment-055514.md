The Prime Minister’s ethics adviser has declined a request from Conservative MPs to open an investigation into the appointment of Lord Mandelson as a trade envoy. Sir Laurie Magnus, the Independent Adviser on Ministers’ Interests, stated that the established procedures for such appointments were properly observed.

A group of Tory backbenchers had raised concerns about the process, prompting the review. Sir Laurie concluded that the relevant codes and guidelines were followed when Lord Mandelson, a former Labour cabinet minister, was named as a business ambassador for the UK. The role is unpaid and involves promoting British trade interests internationally.

Key points:
* The Prime Minister’s ethics adviser found no grounds for an inquiry into Lord Mandelson’s appointment.
* The review concluded standard government appointment procedures were correctly followed.
* The position is an unpaid ambassadorial role focused on international trade promotion.
