The 2026 Winter Olympic Games will commence this Friday with the opening ceremony in Milan, Italy. The international sporting event, officially known as the Milan-Cortina Games, will run for 16 days, featuring competitions across multiple winter sports disciplines. Athletes from nations around the world are scheduled to participate in events held in various venues throughout the host region.

The Games represent a major international gathering focused on winter sports, drawing significant global viewership. Organizers have finalized preparations for the ceremonies and competitions set to begin at the end of the week. The event follows the traditional Olympic schedule, concluding with a closing ceremony after more than two weeks of scheduled events.

Key points:
* The opening ceremony for the 2026 Winter Olympics is scheduled for Friday in Milan.
* The Milan-Cortina Games will last for a duration of 16 days.
* The event involves athletes from multiple countries competing in winter sports.
