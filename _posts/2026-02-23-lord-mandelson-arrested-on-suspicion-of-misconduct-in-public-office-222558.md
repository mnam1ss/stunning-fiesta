Former UK Cabinet Minister Peter Mandelson Arrested for Questioning

London's Metropolitan Police have confirmed the arrest of a 72-year-old man on suspicion of misconduct in a public office. The individual was detained in the Camden area of London and has been taken to a police station for a formal interview. Police have not officially named the suspect, but multiple sources have identified him as Peter Mandelson, a prominent Labour Party figure who served in several high-ranking ministerial roles under former Prime Ministers Tony Blair and Gordon Brown.

The arrest follows an investigation by the Met's Specialist Crime Command. A spokesperson stated that the arrest is part of a pre-planned operation and that inquiries are ongoing. Lord Mandelson’s representatives have not yet issued a public statement regarding the development.

Key points:
• A 72-year-old man, identified as former minister Lord Mandelson, was arrested in London.
• He was detained on suspicion of misconduct in a public office and taken for police questioning.
• The arrest is part of an ongoing investigation by the Metropolitan Police.
