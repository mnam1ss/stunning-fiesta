A new non-hormonal treatment for menopausal hot flushes is now available for prescription through the National Health Service in England. The medication, taken as a daily pill, offers an alternative for individuals who cannot or choose not to use hormone replacement therapy (HRT).

The National Institute for Health and Care Excellence (NICE) has recommended the drug, fezolinetant, for use within the NHS. It is designed to address moderate to severe vasomotor symptoms, commonly known as hot flushes and night sweats, which are a primary symptom of menopause. Health officials estimate this treatment could be suitable for a significant number of people for whom HRT is not an appropriate option.

Key points:
*   A daily non-hormonal pill for menopausal hot flushes is now approved for NHS prescription.
*   The treatment provides an alternative for those unable to take hormone replacement therapy (HRT).
*   The drug, fezolinetant, targets the brain's regulation of body temperature to reduce symptoms.
