Israeli airstrikes in the Gaza Strip have resulted in the deaths of at least 27 Palestinians, according to local rescue services. The Israeli military has acknowledged carrying out the operations. These strikes occurred amid an ongoing ceasefire, a temporary halt in hostilities that both Israel and Hamas have publicly accused the other side of violating in recent days.

The latest violence underscores the fragile nature of the truce and the continued high tensions in the region. Further details regarding the specific locations and circumstances of the strikes have not been immediately released by officials.

Key points:
- At least 27 Palestinians were killed in Israeli airstrikes, as reported by Gaza rescue officials.
- The Israeli military confirmed it conducted the strikes during a period of a ceasefire.
- Both Israel and Hamas have exchanged accusations of breaching the terms of the truce.
