Photographer Captures Image of Prince Andrew Following Arrest

A photograph of Britain's Prince Andrew leaving his Windsor residence was the result of a combination of information and timing, according to the photographer involved. Reuters photographer Phil Noble stated he received an initial tip and then embarked on a lengthy drive to the royal's Norfolk property, Royal Lodge, following the prince's recent arrest. He described the successful capture of the image as involving a significant element of chance. The photograph shows the prince in the driver's seat of a car, and it was widely disseminated by international media outlets. The image was taken amid ongoing legal and public scrutiny surrounding the royal.

Key points:
• A photographer drove for several hours to a royal residence after receiving information.
• The image of Prince Andrew was described as involving a considerable degree of luck.
• The photograph was distributed globally following the prince's arrest.
