Four individuals were taken into custody by authorities on Friday, suspected of providing support to Iran's intelligence apparatus. The arrests followed an investigation into activities allegedly conducted on behalf of the foreign service. Officials have not released the identities of the detained men, citing the ongoing nature of the inquiry.

The operation was carried out by domestic security services, which have indicated the investigation is part of broader efforts to counter foreign espionage. No specific details regarding the alleged assistance or any potential targets have been made public at this stage. A statement from law enforcement confirmed the detentions are connected to national security concerns.

Legal proceedings are now underway, and the suspects are expected to face formal charges related to violations of security laws. The case highlights continuing international tensions surrounding state intelligence activities.

Key points:
• Four men were detained on Friday over allegations of aiding Iranian intelligence.
• The arrests are linked to a national security investigation into foreign espionage.
• Authorities have not disclosed specifics of the suspected activities or any charges.
