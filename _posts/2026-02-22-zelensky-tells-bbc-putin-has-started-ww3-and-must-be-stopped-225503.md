Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky stated in a recent interview that Russian President Vladimir Putin has initiated a global conflict and must be halted. The comments were made during a conversation with a BBC correspondent in Kyiv, conducted just ahead of the four-year mark of Russia's full-scale invasion of Ukraine.

In the interview, the president framed the ongoing war as a broader international struggle, suggesting its consequences extend far beyond the region. He emphasized the need for continued international support for Ukraine's defense efforts. The remarks come amid ongoing debates in Western capitals regarding the level and pace of military aid to be provided.

Key points:
*   President Zelensky characterized the war in Ukraine as the start of a wider world conflict.
*   The interview was conducted in Kyiv shortly before the fourth anniversary of the invasion.
*   Zelensky stressed the imperative of stopping President Putin and securing sustained international assistance.
