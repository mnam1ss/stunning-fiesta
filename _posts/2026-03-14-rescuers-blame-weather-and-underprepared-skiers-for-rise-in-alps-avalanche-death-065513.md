Rescue services in the French Alps have reported a significant increase in avalanche fatalities this winter season, with the number of skiers killed surpassing 100. Officials cite a combination of adverse weather conditions and insufficient preparation by some individuals venturing off-piste as primary factors.

The season has been marked by unstable snowpack, particularly at mid-altitudes, creating hazardous layers prone to collapse. Rescue teams, who have been conducting frequent operations, emphasize that many incidents involve skiers and snowboarders who enter high-risk backcountry areas without essential safety gear or adequate knowledge of current avalanche forecasts. The persistent danger has led to repeated warnings from mountain safety authorities throughout the region.

Key points:
- Avalanche deaths in the French Alps have exceeded 100 this ski season.
- Unstable snow conditions and skiers entering backcountry areas unprepared are cited as major causes.
- Rescue teams continue to stress the importance of carrying safety equipment and checking avalanche risk forecasts.
