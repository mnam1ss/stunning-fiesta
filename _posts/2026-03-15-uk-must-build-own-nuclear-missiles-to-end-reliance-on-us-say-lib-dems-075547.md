Liberal Democrat leader Sir Ed Davey has urged the UK government to develop a domestically produced nuclear missile system, arguing it is necessary to achieve a fully independent nuclear deterrent. The proposal, made in a speech on national security, calls for ending the country's long-standing reliance on the United States for the Trident missile system currently deployed on British submarines.

Sir Ed stated that while the UK's submarine fleet is domestically built, the missiles themselves are purchased and maintained through a US partnership. He contends that beginning work on a sovereign missile capability is a strategic imperative for future security. The Ministry of Defence has historically maintained that the existing Trident partnership with the US is secure and cost-effective. This call comes amid broader parliamentary debates on the renewal and future of the UK's nuclear deterrent.

Key points:
- The Liberal Democrat leader advocates for a British-built nuclear missile system to ensure independence.
- The current Trident missiles are procured from the United States.
- The proposal is likely to fuel ongoing discussions about the UK's nuclear deterrent strategy.
