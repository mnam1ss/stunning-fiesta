Motörhead guitarist Phil Campbell has died at the age of 64, according to an announcement from his family. The musician, who was a member of the influential heavy metal band for over three decades, passed away after a period of treatment in a hospital intensive care unit. His family described his passing as peaceful.

Campbell joined Motörhead in 1984 and performed on all of the band’s subsequent studio albums, contributing to their signature sound alongside frontman Lemmy Kilmister until the group disbanded in 2015 following Lemmy's death. He was known for his distinctive guitar work on classic tracks such as "Ace of Spades" and "Killed by Death." Following Motörhead's end, Campbell continued to perform with his own band, Phil Campbell and the Bastard Sons.
