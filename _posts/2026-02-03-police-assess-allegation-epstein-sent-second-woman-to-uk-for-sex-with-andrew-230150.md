Police in the United Kingdom are reportedly examining a new allegation concerning the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. According to reports, investigators are assessing a claim that Epstein arranged for a second woman to travel to the UK for a sexual encounter with Prince Andrew. The alleged incident is said to have taken place in 2010 at the Duke of York's home, Royal Lodge in Windsor.

This development follows a prior civil settlement between Prince Andrew and Virginia Giuffre, who had accused him of sexual abuse. The prince has consistently denied all allegations of wrongdoing. The Metropolitan Police have stated they are reviewing the information but have not confirmed a formal investigation.

Key points:
*   UK police are reviewing an allegation that Epstein sent a woman to Prince Andrew in 2010.
*   The claim involves a potential encounter at the prince's Windsor residence, Royal Lodge.
*   Prince Andrew has previously denied all misconduct allegations related to Epstein.
