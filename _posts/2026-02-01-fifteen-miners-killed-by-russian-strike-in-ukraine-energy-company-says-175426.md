A Russian missile strike in Ukraine on Sunday resulted in the deaths of fifteen miners, according to a statement from a local energy company. The attack occurred near a bus transporting workers from a coal mining operation.

Company officials reported that the strike killed fifteen employees and left seven others wounded. Emergency services responded to the scene to assist the injured and manage the aftermath of the attack. The incident underscores the ongoing dangers faced by civilian infrastructure and workers amid the conflict.

No immediate comment was available from Russian authorities regarding the reported strike. The area has seen repeated military activity since the invasion began.

Key points:
• Fifteen miners were killed in a reported Russian missile strike in Ukraine on Sunday.
• Seven additional workers sustained injuries in the attack near a worker transport bus.
• The strike highlights continued risks to civilian industrial sites during the war.
