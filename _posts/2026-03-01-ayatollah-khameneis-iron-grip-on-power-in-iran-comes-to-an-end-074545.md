Iranian state media has confirmed the death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei following a series of international airstrikes. The announcement came after the first day of a large-scale military operation reportedly conducted by the United States and Israel against targets within Iran.

The supreme leader, who held the country's highest authority for over three decades, was a central figure in Iran's political and religious governance. His death marks a significant and unprecedented moment in the modern history of the Islamic Republic. The immediate circumstances surrounding his death and the full scope of the military strikes are still being clarified by official sources.

Key points:
*   Iran's Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has been confirmed dead by state media.
*   The death is linked to a major aerial military operation involving U.S. and Israeli forces.
*   The event creates a major leadership vacuum at the top of Iran's political structure.
