Ian Huntley, a convicted murderer serving a life sentence, has been treated for serious head injuries following an assault in a prison workshop. The 52-year-old was attacked by another inmate at HMP Frankland, a high-security facility in County Durham.

Huntley was convicted in 2003 for the murders of 10-year-old schoolgirls Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman in Soham, Cambridgeshire. The incident is being investigated as a targeted attack. Prison staff provided immediate first aid before Huntley was transferred to an outside hospital for further treatment. Authorities have confirmed the incident did not involve any breach of prison security.

A spokesperson for the Prison Service stated that police have been informed and an internal investigation is underway. The condition of the other prisoner involved has not been disclosed.

Key points:
• Ian Huntley sustained serious head injuries in an assault at HMP Frankland.
• He is serving a life sentence for the 2002 murders of Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman.
• The incident is under investigation by both prison authorities and police.
