A civil lawsuit has commenced in the High Court in London, brought by victims of IRA bombings against former Sinn Féin leader Gerry Adams. The plaintiffs, who were injured in attacks in London in 1973 and Manchester in 1996, are seeking a legal judgment that would hold Adams personally responsible for the decisions to plant the car bombs. The case centers on allegations regarding his role within the IRA's command structure at the times of the attacks. Adams has consistently denied ever being a member of the IRA. The proceedings are expected to examine historical evidence and witness testimonies related to the bombings, which caused significant loss of life and injury.

Key points:
*   A civil case against Gerry Adams, brought by IRA bombing victims, has opened in London's High Court.
*   The plaintiffs seek a ruling that he is personally liable for bombings in London (1973) and Manchester (1996).
*   Adams denies membership in the IRA; the case will examine historical command and decision-making.
