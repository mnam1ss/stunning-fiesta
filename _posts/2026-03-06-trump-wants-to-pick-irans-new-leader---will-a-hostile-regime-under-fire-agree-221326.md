U.S. President Donald Trump has stated he would be willing to meet with Iran’s leadership to discuss a new nuclear deal, a proposal that follows heightened military and diplomatic tensions between the two nations. The offer, which includes a suggestion that the U.S. could help facilitate leadership change in Iran, is seen by analysts as an unconventional diplomatic overture. However, observers note that Iran’s government, which has consistently rejected external pressure, is unlikely to agree to terms perceived as dictated by Washington. The situation remains delicate, with recent confrontations in the Gulf underscoring the risk of further escalation.

Key points:
- President Trump has expressed openness to meeting Iranian leaders to negotiate a replacement for the 2015 nuclear accord.
- The proposal implies U.S. support for political change within Iran, a condition likely to be rejected by Tehran.
- Ongoing military tensions in the region continue to pose a significant risk of conflict.
