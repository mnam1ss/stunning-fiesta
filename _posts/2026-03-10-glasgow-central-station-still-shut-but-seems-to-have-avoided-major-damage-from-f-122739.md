Glasgow Central Station remains closed to the public following a significant fire at an adjacent building on Monday. Emergency services continue to manage the scene as safety assessments are conducted.

The fire, which engulfed a neighboring structure, prompted a large-scale response from the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service. While the flames came close to the historic station, initial inspections by Network Rail engineers indicate the main station building has not sustained serious structural damage. The full extent of any impact on rail infrastructure is still being evaluated.

Authorities have not provided a timeline for the station's reopening, stating that access will be restored only once it is confirmed to be completely safe. Rail services are being diverted or canceled, with passengers advised to check for travel updates before beginning their journeys.

Key points:
- Glasgow Central Station is closed pending safety checks after a nearby fire.
- Initial assessments suggest the station building avoided major structural damage.
- No reopening timeline is available; rail services are heavily disrupted.
