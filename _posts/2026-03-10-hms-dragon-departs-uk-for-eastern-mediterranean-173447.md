The Royal Navy frigate HMS Dragon has left the United Kingdom for a deployment to the eastern Mediterranean. The Type 45 destroyer is scheduled to operate in the region for several months. Its primary mission will involve providing air defense for the Royal Air Force base at Akrotiri on Cyprus. The base has previously been targeted by unmanned aerial systems.

The deployment is part of ongoing regional security operations. The Ministry of Defence stated the vessel will work alongside international partners. HMS Dragon is equipped with advanced radar and missile systems designed to detect and intercept aerial threats.

Key points:
*   HMS Dragon, a Type 45 destroyer, has deployed from the UK to the eastern Mediterranean.
*   Its key task will be air defense for RAF Akrotiri in Cyprus, which has faced drone attacks.
*   The deployment supports wider international security efforts in the region.
