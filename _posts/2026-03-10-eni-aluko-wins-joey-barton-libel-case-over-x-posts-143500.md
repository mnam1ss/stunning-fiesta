Former England footballer Eni Aluko has been awarded damages in a libel case against former professional footballer Joey Barton. The case concerned two social media posts published by Barton in early 2024. A High Court judge ruled that the posts contained defamatory statements about Aluko.

Mr. Barton has been ordered to pay Ms. Aluko a total sum exceeding £300,000 in damages and legal costs. The judgment follows a legal process where the court found the published comments to be false and harmful to Aluko's reputation.

This legal outcome highlights the potential consequences of making defamatory statements on public platforms, even for high-profile individuals in sports media.

Key points:
- Joey Barton must pay Eni Aluko over £300,000 for defamatory social media posts.
- A High Court judge ruled the 2024 posts contained false and damaging statements.
- The case underscores the legal liabilities associated with publishing defamatory content online.
