UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer has confirmed that the United Kingdom will permit the United States to use British military bases for potential strikes against Iranian missile sites. The announcement clarifies the UK's position amid heightened regional tensions.

Speaking to reporters, the Prime Minister emphasized that this agreement does not signify direct British participation in offensive military operations. He stated that the UK's current stance is to provide logistical support through base access, while not engaging in active combat.

The decision involves facilities like RAF Akrotiri in Cyprus, which have been used previously for operations in the Middle East. This move follows recent escalations involving Iran and its allied groups in the region. Analysts suggest it reinforces the longstanding defense and intelligence partnership between the UK and US, often referred to as the "special relationship."

Key points:
* The UK will allow US forces to use British bases for potential strikes on Iranian missile targets.
* Prime Minister Starmer stated the UK will not directly participate in offensive military action at this time.
* The decision underscores continued close defense coordination between the UK and United States.
