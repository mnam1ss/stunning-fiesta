Formula 1 has officially canceled the upcoming Grand Prix events in Bahrain and Saudi Arabia. The decision was made in response to the current regional instability and ongoing conflict in the Middle East. The Bahrain Grand Prix was scheduled to be the season-opening race on March 2, with the Saudi Arabian Grand Prix set to follow on March 9.

In a statement, Formula 1 cited the priority of ensuring the safety and security of all traveling staff, teams, drivers, and fans. The sport's governing body, the FIA, is coordinating with local promoters and authorities. Officials have stated that the decision was taken following extensive consultation and a review of the prevailing logistical and safety challenges. The calendar for the remainder of the 2025 season is under review, with potential replacements for the canceled races being considered.

Key points:
*   The season-opening races in Bahrain and Saudi Arabia have been canceled.
*   The decision is due to safety and logistical concerns stemming from regional conflict.
*   Formula 1 and the FIA are assessing options for the revised 2025 calendar.
