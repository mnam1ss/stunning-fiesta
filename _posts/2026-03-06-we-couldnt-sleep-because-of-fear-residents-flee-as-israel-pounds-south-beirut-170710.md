Israeli military operations have expanded into southern Beirut, prompting a new wave of civilian evacuations. Authorities have issued warnings to residents across multiple areas of Lebanon to depart their homes ahead of anticipated strikes targeting Hezbollah positions.

Local reports describe a climate of intense anxiety among those affected. Many residents have recounted sleepless nights and a hurried exodus from neighborhoods now within the scope of the conflict. The ongoing cross-border hostilities between Israeli forces and the Lebanese militant group Hezbollah have escalated significantly in recent days, marking a serious expansion of the fighting beyond previous front lines.

The broader conflict, which has seen near-daily exchanges of fire since October, continues to heighten regional tensions and deepen the humanitarian situation in affected Lebanese communities.

Key points:
- Israeli strikes have targeted southern Beirut, leading to civilian evacuations.
- Warnings were issued for residents in wide areas of Lebanon to leave due to military action against Hezbollah.
- The hostilities represent a notable geographical escalation of ongoing cross-border conflict.
