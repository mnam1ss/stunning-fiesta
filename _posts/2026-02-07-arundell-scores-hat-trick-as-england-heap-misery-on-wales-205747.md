England secured a decisive victory over Wales in their opening match of the Six Nations rugby championship on Saturday. The match, held at Twickenham, saw a dominant performance from the home side, who ran in multiple tries to establish a commanding lead.

Winger Henry Arundell was a standout performer, crossing the line three times to score a hat-trick. His efforts contributed significantly to a high points total for England, who controlled the game's tempo and territory. Wales struggled to contain the English attack and were unable to mount a sustained challenge.

The win marks a strong start for England as they begin their campaign in the tournament. Their last championship title was in 2020.

Key points:
*   England defeated Wales in their opening Six Nations fixture.
*   Winger Henry Arundell scored three tries, a hat-trick.
*   The victory is a first step in England's bid for a first Six Nations title since 2020.
