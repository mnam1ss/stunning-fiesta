Former civil servants have alleged that Prince Andrew, while serving as a UK trade envoy, submitted expenses for a personal massage that were paid by taxpayers. The whistleblowers state there was insufficient oversight of his official expenditure during his tenure from 2001 to 2011.

According to the reports, the claims suggest a pattern of expenses receiving minimal scrutiny from government officials. The allegations are part of broader concerns about the transparency and management of costs associated with the Duke of York's former role. Buckingham Palace has previously stated that Prince Andrew’s work as a trade envoy was entirely proper and that his office operated within the established guidelines.

A government spokesperson noted that all ministerial and royal travel is conducted in accordance with strict rules and is transparently published.

Key points:
- Whistleblowers allege Prince Andrew charged a personal massage to the public purse during his time as a trade envoy.
- The claims include assertions of inadequate scrutiny for his official expenses over a decade.
- The government maintains that all such travel and expenses follow official rules and reporting standards.
