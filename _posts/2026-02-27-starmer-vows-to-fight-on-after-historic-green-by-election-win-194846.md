Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer has pledged to continue his party's campaign following a significant by-election defeat to the Green Party. The contest was held in a constituency that has been represented by Labour for almost a century. The outcome is seen as a major setback for the opposition party and is expected to increase scrutiny of the Prime Minister's leadership amid ongoing political challenges.

The victory marks one of the Green Party's most notable electoral successes. Analysts suggest the result reflects broader voter dissatisfaction with the major political parties. The by-election was triggered by the resignation of the previous Labour MP.

Key points:
*   Labour lost a long-held parliamentary seat to the Green Party in a by-election.
*   The result is considered a historic win for the Greens and a setback for Labour.
*   The defeat adds to the political pressure on the Prime Minister.
