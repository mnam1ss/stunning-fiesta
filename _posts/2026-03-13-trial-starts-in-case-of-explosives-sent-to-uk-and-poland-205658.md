A trial has begun in the United Kingdom concerning a plot to send explosive devices through the postal system to addresses in the UK and Poland. The case is part of a broader investigation into a series of sabotage incidents reported across Europe in recent years. According to European security officials, these incidents form a pattern of activity that has been attributed to Russian operatives. The ongoing legal proceedings will examine the evidence surrounding the intercepted parcels and the individuals allegedly involved in their preparation and dispatch. Authorities in multiple countries have been cooperating on the investigation, which highlights ongoing concerns about state-sponsored sabotage campaigns on European soil.

Key points:
- A trial is underway in the UK for a plot involving explosive parcels sent to the UK and Poland.
- Officials in Europe have connected this incident to a wider pattern of sabotage attributed to Russia.
- The case involves international cooperation and underscores security concerns about hostile state activity.
