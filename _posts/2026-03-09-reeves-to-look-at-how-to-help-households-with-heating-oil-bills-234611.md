Chancellor Rachel Reeves has announced plans to examine potential support measures for households that rely on heating oil. A meeting is scheduled for this Wednesday to discuss the issue.

The move comes amid ongoing concerns about energy costs, with heating oil users often facing volatile prices that are not covered by the same price cap applied to mains gas and electricity. The government has indicated it will explore options to provide assistance to these consumers, who are frequently in rural areas not connected to the main gas grid.

Further details on specific proposals or a timeline for any potential support scheme are expected to follow the ministerial discussions.

Key points:
*   Chancellor Rachel Reeves will convene a meeting this Wednesday to address heating oil costs.
*   The discussion aims to explore assistance for households not covered by the standard energy price cap.
*   Many users affected are in rural properties without access to the mains gas network.
