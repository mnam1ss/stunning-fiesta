A new interim report on maternity services in England has identified systemic failures affecting patients throughout their care. The review, commissioned by the National Health Service, found significant issues present at all stages of the maternity process. According to the findings, racism and a lack of compassion from some staff members were key contributing factors to the poor standards of care. The report describes the overall state of maternity services as "failing" to meet acceptable levels of safety and support for many families. Investigators are continuing their work to develop final recommendations for improvement.

Key points:
* An interim review found widespread problems across all phases of maternity care in England.
* The report cited racism and insufficient compassion as major elements in the failing system.
* The findings are part of an ongoing investigation to reform national maternity services.
