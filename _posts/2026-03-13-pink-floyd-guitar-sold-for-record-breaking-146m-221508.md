A guitar owned and played by Pink Floyd guitarist David Gilmour has been sold at auction for $14.6 million, setting a new record for the most expensive guitar ever sold. The instrument, a black Fender Stratocaster, was a primary guitar used by Gilmour during the 1970s and featured prominently on several of the band's landmark albums from that era.

The sale was conducted by Christie's auction house. Proceeds from the sale are reportedly designated for a climate charity. The final price far exceeded pre-auction estimates and surpasses the previous record held by a guitar once owned by Kurt Cobain.

Key points:
*   A Fender Stratocaster played by Pink Floyd's David Gilmour sold for $14.6 million.
*   The sale sets a new world record for the most expensive guitar sold at auction.
*   The proceeds from the sale are intended to benefit climate change initiatives.
