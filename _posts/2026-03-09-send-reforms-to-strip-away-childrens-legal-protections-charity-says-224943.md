Proposed changes to England's system for supporting children with special educational needs and disabilities (SEND) have drawn criticism from a leading charity. The reforms, which were detailed by the government last month, aim to streamline processes and create more consistent support across local authorities.

However, the charity SEND Reform England has warned that the planned alterations could significantly weaken legal safeguards for children and their families. They argue the changes may reduce the clarity of entitlements and make it harder for parents to challenge decisions regarding their child's education and care.

The Department for Education has stated the reforms are intended to improve outcomes and ensure resources are focused more directly on support, rather than administrative processes. Officials maintain that children's needs will remain central to the updated system.

Key points:
*   A charity has raised concerns that government reforms to the SEND system in England may reduce legal protections.
*   The proposed changes were announced last month with the aim of creating more consistent support.
*   The government states the reforms are designed to improve outcomes and focus resources on direct support.
