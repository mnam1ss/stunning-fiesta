A 13-year-old boy in Australia is being credited with saving his family after they were swept out to sea. Austin Applebee was with his mother and two younger siblings when they were caught in a strong current off the coast of New South Wales.

According to reports, Applebee helped his siblings onto a single boogie board before beginning a lengthy swim to seek help. He stated that thoughts of his mother motivated him during the arduous swim, which lasted for several hours. Emergency services were alerted and subsequently located and rescued the mother and younger children, who were all treated for exhaustion.

Authorities have described the teenager’s actions as remarkable, noting the challenging sea conditions at the time.

Key points:
• A 13-year-old boy swam for hours to get help after his family was caught in a current.
