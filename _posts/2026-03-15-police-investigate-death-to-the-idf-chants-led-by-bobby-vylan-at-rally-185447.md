Police are investigating chants of "death to the IDF" made during a rally in central London. The chants were reportedly led by musician Bobby Vylan. The event drew a significant police presence, with approximately 1,000 officers deployed to manage the demonstration and a separate counter-protest occurring across the River Thames. Authorities monitored the situation to maintain order between the opposing groups.

The incident has prompted an official inquiry into the nature of the chants made at the gathering. The rally and the corresponding counter-protest concluded without major reported disturbances, due in part to the substantial police oversight separating the two factions.

Key points:
- Police are investigating chants led by Bobby Vylan at a London rally.
- About 1,000 officers were deployed to manage demonstrators and counter-protesters.
- The event concluded without major reported public order incidents.
