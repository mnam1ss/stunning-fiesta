Lawyers for Lord Peter Mandelson have stated that his recent arrest was based on what they call an unfounded allegation that he intended to flee the United Kingdom. According to his legal representatives, there was no validity to the claim that he was a flight risk. The arrest is connected to an ongoing investigation, though specific details of that inquiry have not been publicly disclosed by authorities. Mandelson, a former cabinet minister and European commissioner, has not been formally charged. His legal team has strongly contested the circumstances of the arrest, asserting there was no basis for the concern over his potential departure from the country. Officials have yet to issue a detailed public statement regarding the arrest rationale.

Key points:
- Lord Mandelson's lawyers deny he was a flight risk, calling the claim baseless.
- The arrest is linked to an ongoing investigation, with no charges currently filed.
- Authorities have not provided a comprehensive public explanation for the arrest.
