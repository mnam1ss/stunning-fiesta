A drone attack targeted a Royal Air Force base in Cyprus on Thursday, with the President of Cyprus attributing the strike to Iran. According to a statement from the British Ministry of Defence, the incident resulted in no injuries to personnel. The assessment also indicated that the damage to the base's infrastructure was minor.

The Akrotiri base, a strategic site used by British and allied forces, is located on the southern coast of the Mediterranean island. The reported attack comes amid heightened regional tensions. Officials have not provided further details on the specific type of drone used or the precise nature of the damage. An investigation into the incident is ongoing.

Key points:
• A drone strike attributed to Iran hit a British RAF base in Cyprus.
• No casualties were reported and damage was assessed as minimal.
• The incident occurs against a backdrop of increased regional instability.
