A Russian-operated oil tanker has sunk in the Mediterranean Sea following an incident involving explosions and a fire, according to Libyan maritime officials. The vessel, identified as the *Arctic Metagaz*, went down in international waters between the coasts of Libya and Malta.

Russian authorities have attributed the damage to Ukrainian naval drones, alleging a targeted attack. Ukraine has not issued an immediate public statement regarding the incident. The sinking raises concerns over maritime security in a region already facing heightened tensions due to the ongoing conflict.

Rescue operations were reportedly initiated, but the status of the crew remains unclear. The event marks another incident involving commercial shipping since the start of the wider war, though the exact circumstances surrounding the tanker's demise are still under investigation by multiple parties.

Key points:
- A Russian oil tanker sank after reported explosions and fire off the North African coast.
- Russian officials blame the incident on an attack by Ukrainian naval drones.
- The sinking occurs amid ongoing regional tensions, with the crew's status currently unknown.
