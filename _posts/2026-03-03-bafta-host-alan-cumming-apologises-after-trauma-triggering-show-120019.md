Bafta Host Apologizes Following On-Stage Incident

Bafta Television Awards host Alan Cumming has issued an apology after an incident during Sunday's ceremony caused distress. The apology follows an occurrence where a guest with Tourette's syndrome, who was attending as a campaigner, experienced an involuntary vocal tic. The outburst, which contained a racially offensive term, happened while two Black actors were presenting an award on stage.

In a statement posted to social media, Cumming expressed his regret for any upset caused, stating the moment was "deeply regrettable." The campaigner involved has also publicly addressed the incident, explaining the involuntary nature of tics associated with the neurological condition. The British Academy of Film and Television Arts has not released an official statement regarding the event.

Key points:
- Bafta host Alan Cumming apologized after a guest's involuntary tic caused disruption.
- The incident occurred during a live awards presentation involving two Black actors.
- The individual involved has Tourette's syndrome, where such vocalizations are not controlled.
