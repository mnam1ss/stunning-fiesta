A planned public march in London has been prohibited by local authorities following a formal request from the Metropolitan Police. The event was organized by a group that has acknowledged ties to Iran, which described the gathering as a peaceful demonstration in solidarity with Palestinians.

Police officials submitted an application to the local council to ban the procession, citing concerns over potential public disorder. While the organizing committee maintains the event's intentions were strictly peaceful, the group has faced previous criticism for its perceived representation of the Iranian government's interests.

The council reviewed the police evidence and ultimately approved the ban, preventing the march from taking place as scheduled. No further demonstrations by the group are currently permitted under this ruling.

Key points:
*   A London march organized by an Iran-linked group was banned after a police request.
*   Authorities cited public order concerns, while organizers called it a peaceful pro-Palestinian event.
*   The group has been criticized in the past for its association with the Iranian regime.
