Two commercial vessels have been struck near the strategic Strait of Hormuz, according to maritime security reports. The incidents have heightened concerns over the security of a critical waterway for global energy supplies.

In response to the events, shipping traffic has reportedly slowed significantly near the entrance to the strait. Analysts note that the immediate uncertainty has contributed to an increase in global oil prices, reflecting market worries about potential supply disruptions. The strait is a vital chokepoint for the transit of a substantial portion of the world's seaborne oil.

Official details regarding the nature of the attacks, the condition of the ships, and responsibility for the incidents have not yet been formally released. An investigation by regional authorities is understood to be underway.

Key points:
- Two ships were hit near the Strait of Hormuz, a major oil transit route.
- Shipping activity has been affected, contributing to a rise in global oil prices.
- The circumstances of the incidents are under investigation.
