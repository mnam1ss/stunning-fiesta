**U.S. and Israel Coordinated Extensively Before Tehran Strike**

A recent military operation targeting a facility in Tehran linked to Iran’s leadership was the result of months of strategic coordination between U.S. and Israeli officials, according to sources familiar with the planning. The operation, which occurred earlier this week, was reportedly initiated after planners received vital information shortly before its execution. This intelligence allowed for final adjustments to the mission’s parameters. The strike focused on a compound in central Tehran associated with the office of Iran’s Supreme Leader. Officials have not commented publicly on the specific outcomes of the action. The planning process involved detailed logistical and intelligence-sharing efforts between the two allied nations, underscoring the complexity of operations within Iran.

Key points:
* The operation followed an extended period of joint U.S.-Israeli preparation.
* Final critical intelligence was obtained mere hours before the strike was carried out.
* The target was a compound in central Tehran connected to Iran's Supreme Leader.
