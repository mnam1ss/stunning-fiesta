The U.S. Department of Justice has released a set of previously withheld documents related to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The documents include allegations from a civil lawsuit that mention former President Donald Trump, though the nature of these accusations is not detailed in the filing. According to the department, these records were not included in prior disclosures because they were mistakenly categorized as duplicates during the review process.

The release is part of ongoing court-ordered disclosures of materials connected to the Epstein case. The documents are heavily redacted and consist of deposition transcripts and legal filings. The Justice Department stated the omission was an administrative error and that the files have now been properly published.

Key points:
*   The DOJ released documents mentioning Donald Trump from the Epstein case that were previously withheld.
*   Officials stated the files were not published earlier due to an incorrect classification as duplicate material.
*   The newly public records are part of a broader series of court-mandated disclosures.
