Iran has delayed the state funeral for Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, citing a need for further logistical preparations. The three-day ceremony, during which his body will lie in state in Tehran, will now be held at a later, unspecified date. The announcement comes amid ongoing military exchanges between Israel and Hezbollah, with the United States continuing to provide military support to Israeli operations.

Regional tensions remain high following recent escalations. The postponement of the funeral, a major state event, suggests authorities are prioritizing security and ceremonial planning during a period of sustained conflict.

Key points:
*   Iran has postponed the state funeral for Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei to allow for more preparation.
*   The delay occurs during continued hostilities between Israel and Hezbollah, backed by U.S. support for Israel.
*   The multi-day ceremony will involve Khamenei lying in state in the capital, Tehran.
