Four women have secured the conviction of a man who subjected them to abuse over several decades. The women, who knew the perpetrator individually, later connected and collaborated to build a legal case against him.

Their collective testimony was presented in court, resulting in Aaron Swan being found guilty on multiple charges. The legal proceedings detailed a pattern of abusive behavior spanning many years.

The successful prosecution is noted by authorities as a significant example of survivor cooperation leading to a conviction. The case has concluded following a recent court sentencing.

Key points:
- Four women collaborated to testify against a man who abused them over decades.
- Aaron Swan was found guilty on multiple charges based on their collective evidence.
- The prosecution highlighted the case as an example of effective survivor cooperation.
