Oil and gas prices increased on Monday following a new security threat to a major global shipping route. A senior Iranian military official issued a warning regarding maritime traffic through the Strait of Hormuz, a narrow waterway vital for the transport of a significant portion of the world's seaborne oil. The official stated that vessels passing through the chokepoint could be targeted.

The remarks have heightened market concerns over potential disruptions to energy supplies from the Persian Gulf region. The Strait of Hormuz is a crucial transit point for oil exports from several Middle Eastern producers, including Saudi Arabia and the United Arab Emirates. Any instability in the area typically leads to immediate reactions in global commodity markets.

Key points:
- A senior Iranian official issued a threat against commercial shipping in the Strait of Hormuz.
- The strategic waterway is a critical passage for a large percentage of globally traded oil.
- The warning contributed to a rise in international oil and natural gas prices.
