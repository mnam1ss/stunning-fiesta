Civil rights leader and political activist Jesse Jackson has died at the age of 84. Jackson, who founded the Rainbow PUSH Coalition, was a prominent figure in American social justice movements for decades. He worked alongside Dr. Martin Luther King Jr. and later became a two-time candidate for the Democratic presidential nomination.

Following the announcement of his passing, numerous political figures issued statements acknowledging his impact. Many characterized his decades of activism as having a significant effect on national conversations surrounding equality and civil rights.

Jackson’s career spanned from the front lines of 1960s protests to international diplomacy, and he remained a vocal advocate for economic and social justice throughout his life.

Key points:
• Jesse Jackson, a major figure in the U.S. civil rights movement, has died at 84.
• He was known for his work with Martin Luther King Jr. and his later presidential campaigns.
• Political leaders have widely recognized his long-standing influence on social justice issues.
