A military drone crashed into a Royal Air Force base in Cyprus on Wednesday, according to Cypriot President Nikos Christodoulides. The president stated the unmanned aerial vehicle was of Iranian origin.

The incident occurred at RAF Akrotiri, a strategically important British base on the southern coast of the island. The United Kingdom's Ministry of Defence confirmed the event, reporting no injuries to personnel. Officials also assessed that the damage caused to the base's infrastructure was very minor.

The crash has drawn international attention due to the base's role in regional operations. Authorities from Cyprus and the United Kingdom are conducting a joint investigation into the circumstances surrounding the drone's flight path and ultimate crash.

Key points:
*   A drone identified as Iranian crashed at the RAF Akrotiri base in Cyprus.
*   UK authorities reported no casualties and only very slight damage.
*   A joint investigation by Cypriot and British officials is underway.
