UK Government Clarifies Position on Chagos Islands Negotiations

The UK government has stated that negotiations with Mauritius regarding the future of the Chagos Islands are continuing, denying reports that talks have been paused. This clarification follows comments from a government minister who indicated discussions were on hold while the UK addressed unspecified "concerns" with the United States. The US maintains a military base on Diego Garcia, the largest island in the archipelago. Recent public criticism of the UK-Mauritius framework agreement by former US President Donald Trump had brought increased attention to the long-running dispute. The UK Foreign Office maintains its position that it is committed to ongoing negotiations aimed at resolving sovereignty claims over the British Indian Ocean Territory.

Key points:
*   The UK government denies negotiations with Mauritius over the Chagos Islands have been suspended.
*   The clarification comes after a minister suggested talks were paused to address US concerns.
*   The future of the US military base on Diego Garcia remains a central issue in the discussions.
