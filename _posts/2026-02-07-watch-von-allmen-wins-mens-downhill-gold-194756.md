Swiss skier Franjo von Allmen has secured the first gold medal of the 2026 Winter Olympic Games, claiming victory in the men's downhill event. The race, held at the Milano-Cortina venues, saw von Allmen deliver a commanding performance on the demanding course. His time held through the final stages of the competition as the remaining contenders were unable to surpass it.

The result marks a significant achievement for the Swiss alpine team early in the Games. The silver and bronze medals were awarded to other competitors following tightly contested runs. The men's downhill is traditionally one of the premier events of the Olympic alpine skiing program, testing athletes' speed and technical skill on one of the longest and steepest courses.

Key points:
- Franjo von Allmen of Switzerland won the men's downhill skiing event.
- This victory represents the first gold medal awarded at the 2026 Winter Olympics.
- The event took place on the first day of full competition at the Milano-Cortina Games.
