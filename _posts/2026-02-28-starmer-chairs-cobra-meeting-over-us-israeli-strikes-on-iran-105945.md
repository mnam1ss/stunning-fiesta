Prime Minister Keir Starmer convened a meeting of the UK government’s emergency COBRA committee on Sunday in response to military strikes carried out by the United States and Israel against targets in Iran. The meeting was called to assess the situation and coordinate the British response following the escalation of hostilities.

A government spokesperson, addressing the developments, emphasized that the United Kingdom’s position is to avoid any actions that could lead to a wider regional conflict. The statement underscored a desire for stability and de-escalation.

Officials are monitoring the security implications closely, though no immediate change to the UK’s threat level has been announced. The COBRA meeting is a standard procedure for coordinating the national response to major international incidents.

Key points:
*   Prime Minister Keir Starmer chaired a COBRA meeting following US-Israeli strikes on Iran.
*   A UK government spokesperson stated an aim to prevent further regional escalation.
*   The meeting was part of standard procedure to coordinate the national response.
