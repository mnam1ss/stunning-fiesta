**Labour Leader to Outline Heating Oil Assistance Proposal**

Labour Party leader Sir Keir Starmer is expected to detail a plan to support households struggling with heating oil costs. The announcement comes amid significant price increases for the fuel, which many rural and off-grid homes rely on for heating. Market analysts attribute the recent surge in costs primarily to heightened geopolitical tensions in the Middle East, following recent military engagements.

The proposed measures aim to alleviate financial pressure on affected consumers, though specific policy details and funding mechanisms have not yet been released. The government has previously implemented temporary support schemes for energy bills, but targeted assistance for heating oil users has been a subject of ongoing political debate. Further reaction from other political parties and consumer groups is anticipated following the formal announcement.

Key points:
*   Labour leader Keir Starmer will propose a support plan for heating oil expenses.
*   Heating oil prices have risen sharply due to Middle East conflict.
*   The plan targets off-grid households facing increased winter heating costs.
