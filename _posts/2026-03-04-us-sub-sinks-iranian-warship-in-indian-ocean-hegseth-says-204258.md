A U.S. Navy submarine was involved in the sinking of an Iranian military vessel in the Indian Ocean, according to a statement from U.S. military analyst Pete Hegseth. The reported incident occurred off the southern coast of Sri Lanka. Separately, Sri Lankan naval authorities have announced that a military vessel sank in the same general area, with approximately 140 personnel currently unaccounted for. The relationship between these two events, if any, has not been officially confirmed by U.S. or Iranian defense officials. The developments highlight ongoing regional maritime tensions.

Key points:
• A U.S. analyst reports a U.S. submarine sank an Iranian warship in the Indian Ocean.
• Sri Lanka's navy states a vessel sank off its coast, with about 140 people missing.
• No official confirmation has linked the two reported incidents.
