Residents in Tehran are reportedly purchasing increased quantities of food and household goods amid heightened regional tensions. According to local accounts, concerns are growing over potential supply disruptions and rising costs for basic items.

This activity comes as a series of labor strikes have affected operations within the Iranian capital, contributing to anxieties about market stability. Shoppers have indicated that securing everyday groceries has become a primary focus, with some noting fluctuations in product availability and pricing at local markets.

The situation reflects broader public apprehension as international diplomatic efforts continue.

Key points:
- Tehran residents are increasing purchases of essential goods due to supply concerns.
- Ongoing strikes in the capital are cited as a factor affecting market conditions.
- Reports indicate worries over both product availability and potential price increases.
