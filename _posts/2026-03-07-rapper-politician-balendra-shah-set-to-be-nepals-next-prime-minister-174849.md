Balendra Shah, a rapper and independent politician, is poised to become Nepal's next prime minister following recent parliamentary negotiations. His expected appointment comes after a period of political realignment in the wake of the country's general election held last Thursday.

That election was the first national vote since widespread youth-led demonstrations resulted in the collapse of the previous government in September. Shah, who is also an engineer, first gained prominence as Kathmandu's mayor before entering national politics. His rise reflects shifting dynamics within Nepal's political landscape, where traditional party structures are being challenged by independent figures.

Key points:
- Balendra Shah, an independent rapper-politician, is set to lead Nepal's new government.
- His expected appointment follows a general election held after protests toppled the prior administration.
- The election marked the first national vote since September's youth-led political upheaval.
