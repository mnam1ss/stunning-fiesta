A new BBC documentary examines the lasting impact of a 1996 school shooting in Dunblane, Scotland, which resulted in the deaths of 16 children and one teacher. The program traces how the public response to the tragedy led to significant changes in British firearms legislation.

Following the incident, a sustained campaign by victims' families and supporters culminated in the government passing two new acts of Parliament. These laws effectively instituted a near-total ban on private handgun ownership across the United Kingdom. The documentary features interviews and archival material to chronicle the path from grief to political action.

Key points:
- A 1996 school shooting in Dunblane, Scotland, killed 16 children and a teacher.
- Public and campaigner pressure after the tragedy led to new UK legislation.
- The laws resulted in a comprehensive ban on private handgun ownership.
