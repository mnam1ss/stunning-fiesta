**Avalanche in California's Lake Tahoe Region Leaves Multiple Skiers Dead**

Rescue teams have concluded search operations following a major avalanche in the Lake Tahoe area of California. The incident, which occurred on Tuesday, involved a group of fifteen skiers who were reported missing after the slide. Authorities have now confirmed the recovery of eight deceased individuals from the site.

One person from the original group remains unaccounted for. Officials have stated that, given the conditions and elapsed time, the missing skier is presumed to have also perished. The remaining six members of the party were located alive earlier in the search efforts.

The avalanche struck in a backcountry area, prompting a large-scale response from multiple agencies. An investigation into the precise cause and circumstances of the avalanche is ongoing.

Key points:
- Eight skiers have been confirmed dead following an avalanche in California's Lake Tahoe region on Tuesday.
- One individual remains missing and is presumed deceased, while six others were found alive.
- Search operations have concluded as authorities continue to investigate the incident.
