Recent reports indicate that Iran has expanded its military targeting to include civilian infrastructure in Gulf Arab states, according to an analysis of recent attacks. These nations, which are key strategic partners of the United States, have reportedly experienced strikes on non-military assets. Observers suggest this represents a shift in tactics, moving beyond confrontations solely with U.S. military forces in the region.

The pattern of incidents points to a broader campaign affecting the stability and security of allied governments. The attacks have raised concerns about the safety of critical economic and public facilities in these countries. This development occurs amid ongoing regional tensions and diplomatic efforts to address security challenges.

Key points:
*   Iran's recent strikes are assessed to have targeted civilian infrastructure in Gulf states.
*   These Gulf nations are allied with the United States and host U.S. military assets.
*   The tactic marks an apparent expansion beyond previous engagements with U.S. forces.
