A new report suggests that increasing the number of women in the heavy goods vehicle sector could help address the ongoing driver shortage. The haulage industry has faced significant recruitment challenges in recent years, creating supply chain pressures.

The case of one driver from East Yorkshire illustrates the current gender disparity. At her firm, she is the sole female driver among a workforce of 75. Industry analysts note that women represent a very small percentage of the total HGV driver population, indicating a largely untapped recruitment pool. Logistics companies are now being encouraged to consider targeted outreach and support programs to make the profession more accessible to a wider demographic. Proponents argue that such measures could provide a sustainable solution to the staffing shortfall while promoting greater diversity within the transport sector.

Key points:
* Industry reports identify recruiting more women as a potential strategy to alleviate the HGV driver shortage.
* Female drivers currently make up a very small minority of the total workforce in the haulage sector.
* Logistics firms are being urged to adopt more inclusive recruitment and training practices.
