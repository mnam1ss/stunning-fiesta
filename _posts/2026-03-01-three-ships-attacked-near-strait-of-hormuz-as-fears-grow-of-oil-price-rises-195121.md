Three commercial vessels were reportedly targeted in attacks near the strategic Strait of Hormuz on Wednesday, according to regional maritime authorities. The incidents have heightened concerns over the security of a critical waterway for global energy supplies.

Preliminary reports indicate the attacks caused damage to the ships, though there were no immediate reports of casualties. The exact nature of the assaults and who was responsible remains under investigation by international agencies.

In response to the security incident, shipping traffic has reportedly slowed significantly near the entrance to the strait. Analysts note that market fears of potential supply disruption have already contributed to an increase in global crude oil prices.

Key points:
• Three commercial ships were attacked near the Strait of Hormuz.
• Maritime traffic has slowed in the area following the security incident.
• Concerns over supply disruption have contributed to rising global oil prices.
