**BBC Documentary Examines Dunblane's Lasting Impact on UK Gun Laws**

A new BBC documentary revisits the 1996 Dunblane school shooting, an event that profoundly altered firearm legislation in the United Kingdom. The program traces the aftermath of the tragedy in which 16 primary school children and their teacher were killed in the Scottish town.

The documentary outlines how the public outcry and a sustained campaign led by bereaved families resulted in decisive parliamentary action. Within two years of the shooting, new laws were passed that effectively banned the private ownership of handguns across Great Britain. The film reportedly explores both the immediate grief of the community and the long-term political journey that led to this legislative change.

Key points:
*   A new documentary details the aftermath of the 1996 Dunblane school shooting.
*   The tragedy prompted a successful campaign for stricter firearm controls.
*   UK law was changed to ban private handgun ownership by 1998.
