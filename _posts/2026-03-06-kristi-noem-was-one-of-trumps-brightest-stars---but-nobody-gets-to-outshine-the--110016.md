South Dakota Governor Kristi Noem, once considered a prominent figure within the political circles aligned with former President Donald Trump, has seen her national profile shift in recent months. Initially gaining attention for her hands-off approach to pandemic restrictions, Noem was frequently mentioned as a potential vice-presidential candidate. However, a series of controversies, including the promotion of her new book and the graphic account of a dog shooting within it, have drawn significant criticism from both political opponents and some former allies. This has led to increased scrutiny of her standing and future ambitions within the Republican Party.

Key points:
* Governor Kristi Noem’s national political standing has changed after earlier being viewed as a rising star.
* Recent controversies, including details in her memoir, have prompted a notable backlash.
* Her relationship with former President Trump’s orbit and her future role are now under question.
