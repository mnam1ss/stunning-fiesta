**New Penalties Proposed for Fly-Tipping Offenses**

Government proposals aim to introduce stricter penalties for individuals caught illegally dumping waste, commonly known as fly-tipping. Under the new measures, offenders could face penalty points on their driving licenses in addition to existing fines. Local authorities are also set to receive strengthened powers to confiscate and destroy vehicles used in such offenses.

The initiative seeks to deter a persistent environmental crime by escalating the consequences for perpetrators. Officials argue that linking the offense directly to a driver's license and removing the vehicle used could act as a more significant deterrent than monetary penalties alone. The plans are part of a broader effort to address waste crime and its impact on communities and the environment.

Key points:
*   Drivers convicted of fly-tipping may receive penalty points on their licenses.
*   Councils could be empowered to seize and crush vehicles involved in illegal dumping.
*   The measures are intended to provide a stronger deterrent against waste crime.
