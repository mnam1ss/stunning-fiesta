Household energy bills in the UK are set to decrease starting in April following regulatory adjustments announced in the recent Budget. The changes involve a modification to the standing charge, a fixed daily fee paid by consumers regardless of energy usage, which will be lowered. Simultaneously, the unit price for the energy consumed will see a slight increase.

The net effect for all households will be an overall reduction in their total bill, according to official estimates. However, the precise amount saved will vary for each customer, depending on their specific energy consumption patterns. The government and energy regulator state the move is designed to make the charging system fairer, particularly for lower users.

Key points:
- A shake-up of energy charges will lead to lower total bills for all households from April.
- Savings will vary per household based on individual energy usage.
- The change involves reducing the daily standing charge while slightly increasing the unit rate for consumed energy.
