Several major UK lenders have announced increases to selected mortgage rates, citing rising borrowing costs in financial markets. Nationwide, HSBC, and Coventry Building Society confirmed adjustments affecting a range of residential mortgage products.

Financial analysts attribute the upward pressure on mortgage pricing to increased global uncertainty, with recent geopolitical tensions in the Middle East specifically noted as a factor influencing investor sentiment and the cost of funds. The changes apply to new fixed-rate deals and some product transfers, though existing customers on fixed terms will not see an immediate change to their monthly payments.

The moves are seen as part of a broader recalibration in the home loan market, following a period of relative stability earlier in the year. Prospective borrowers are advised to consult with financial advisors or brokers to understand the latest available rates.

Key points:
- Nationwide, HSBC, and Coventry Building Society are raising rates on various mortgage offers.
- Lenders cite higher market borrowing costs, linked in part to Middle East geopolitical tensions.
- The changes impact new deals, not existing fixed-rate contracts.
