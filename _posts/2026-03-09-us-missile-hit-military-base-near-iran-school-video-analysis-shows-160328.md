A recent analysis of video evidence indicates a U.S. Tomahawk missile struck an Iranian military facility located in close proximity to a primary school in southern Iran. The strike, which occurred earlier this week, targeted the military base. Iranian officials have reported that the attack resulted in 168 fatalities. The video verification was conducted by independent experts who cross-referenced the footage with satellite imagery and other visual data to confirm the weapon's origin and impact site.

The incident has heightened regional tensions, though the exact context and timing of the strike have not been officially detailed by U.S. authorities. The presence of a civilian school near the military target raises questions about the operation's planning and the resulting casualties reported.

Key points:
• Expert video analysis confirms a U.S. missile hit an Iranian military base near a school.
• Iranian authorities state the attack caused 168 deaths.
• The strike's proximity to a civilian area is a focal point of the report.
