A growing number of general practitioners in the UK are choosing to work outside the National Health Service, according to recent reports. This trend coincides with increasing patient demand for private healthcare services. Observers note that pressures on the NHS, including long waiting lists and high patient volumes, are contributing factors for both doctors and patients considering alternative options. The shift indicates a changing landscape for primary care access in the country, though the majority of healthcare continues to be provided through the public system. The development has sparked discussions about the long-term sustainability and capacity of public health services amid rising demand.

Key points:
- Rising patient demand and system pressures are influencing career choices for some doctors.
- A concurrent increase in patients seeking private healthcare alternatives has been noted.
- The trend highlights ongoing debates about capacity and access within the public health system.
