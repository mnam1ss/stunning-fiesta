Liberal Democrat Party leaders have called for the cancellation of King Charles III's upcoming state visit to the United States, citing recent political tensions. The request follows public criticism from former U.S. President Donald Trump regarding the United Kingdom's diplomatic approach to the ongoing conflict involving Iran. The party argues that the planned trip should be postponed until bilateral relations are on more stable footing, suggesting the current climate is not suitable for such a high-profile diplomatic engagement. No official response has yet been issued by Buckingham Palace or the UK government regarding the proposed change. The King's travel itinerary, which is typically planned well in advance, remains unaltered as of now.

Key points:
- The Liberal Democrat Party has formally requested the cancellation of the King's state visit to the U.S.
- The call is linked to political friction following criticism from former President Trump about UK foreign policy.
- There has been no immediate change to the royal travel plans following this request.
