A man has been sentenced to life in prison for the murder of his wife, whose body was discovered buried in their garden. Alireza Askari was convicted at Reading Crown Court for killing Paria Veisi at their home in Berkshire in May 2023.

The court heard that Askari took extensive measures to conceal the crime following the killing. He subsequently reported his wife as a missing person, initiating a police search. The investigation ultimately led officers to the garden of the couple's property, where Veisi's remains were found.

In sentencing, the judge described the killing as a premeditated act. Askari will be required to serve a minimum of 18 years before being eligible for parole consideration.

Key points:
• Alireza Askari received a life sentence for murdering his wife, Paria Veisi.
• He concealed her body in their garden and falsely reported her as missing.
• The judge set a minimum term of 18 years before parole eligibility.
