Former civil servants have alleged that Prince Andrew, while serving as the UK's Special Representative for International Trade and Investment, submitted expenses for personal massage services that were paid by the public. The whistleblowers state there was insufficient oversight of his expenditures during his tenure from 2001 to 2011.

The allegations, reported by the BBC, suggest a lack of rigorous internal checks on the royal's official costs. Buckingham Palace has previously stated that all of the Duke of York's work as a trade envoy was entirely appropriate and that his office operated within the guidelines set by the government. The government department responsible for overseeing the role at the time has not yet issued a new statement regarding these specific claims.

Key points:
• Former officials allege public funds covered Prince Andrew's massage expenses during his trade envoy role.
• Whistleblowers claim there was inadequate scrutiny of his spending while in the position.
• The Palace has historically defended the appropriateness of his official work and adherence to guidelines.
