A United States military refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, resulting in four fatalities, according to a statement from U.S. Central Command. The incident involved a KC-135 Stratotanker, an aircraft designed for aerial refueling operations. Officials confirmed there were no survivors from the crash.

The statement indicated that recovery operations are currently underway at the site. The cause of the crash is not yet known and remains under investigation. The aircraft was operating as part of ongoing U.S. military missions in the region.

No further details regarding the identities of the deceased or the specific circumstances leading to the crash have been released at this time.

Key points:
• A U.S. Air Force KC-135 refueling plane crashed in western Iraq, killing all four personnel on board.
• Recovery efforts are ongoing at the crash site, with the cause of the incident under investigation.
• The U.S. military has not released additional information about the victims or the events preceding the crash.
