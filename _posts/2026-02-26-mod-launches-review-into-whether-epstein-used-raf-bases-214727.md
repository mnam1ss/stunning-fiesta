The UK Ministry of Defence has initiated an internal review to examine potential links between the late financier Jeffrey Epstein and Royal Air Force facilities. The inquiry will assess historical records and correspondence to establish if the convicted sex offender utilized any RAF bases during his visits to the United Kingdom.

Officials stated the review is a precautionary measure to ensure all relevant information is properly examined. The MoD has not indicated what prompted the investigation or specified a timeline for its completion. A spokesperson confirmed the department is looking into the matter as part of its duty to maintain transparency and accountability.

Key points:
• The UK MoD is reviewing records to see if Jeffrey Epstein used RAF bases.
• The examination will analyze historical documents and emails.
• The review is described as a precautionary transparency measure.
