In an interview with the BBC's Newsnight program, Gisèle Pelicot, a central figure in France's largest-ever rape trial, has discussed her experience in the aftermath of the case. She described feeling overwhelmed by the horror of the events but stated she does not harbor feelings of anger. Pelicot spoke at length about her sense of betrayal, her ongoing process of healing, and her conscious decision to focus on a constructive path forward.

The trial, which concluded earlier this year, involved multiple defendants and was closely followed across the country. Pelicot's testimony was considered pivotal to the proceedings. Her decision to speak publicly is seen as a rare insight into the personal impact of such high-profile legal cases.

Key points:
* Gisèle Pelicot, a key witness in France's biggest rape trial, has given a detailed interview to the BBC.
* She described feeling crushed by the events but emphasized she does not feel anger, focusing instead on healing.
* Pelicot discussed themes of betrayal and her choice to pursue a positive path following the trial's conclusion.
