UK Defence Secretary John Healey has arrived in Cyprus for talks with Cypriot officials, following public criticism from the Cypriot government regarding information sharing after recent drone attacks. The visit comes amid heightened regional tensions and focuses on the security partnership between the two nations.

During the meetings, the two sides are expected to discuss coordination and intelligence protocols. The Cypriot government had previously expressed concerns over the timeliness and detail of information provided by the UK following aerial incidents. The discussions aim to address these operational concerns and reaffirm the strategic importance of the British military bases on the island.

Key points:
- UK Defence Secretary John Healey is in Cyprus for meetings following Cypriot criticism of intelligence sharing.
- The talks focus on security coordination after drone attacks in the region.
- The outcome may impact the operational relationship surrounding the UK's sovereign base areas in Cyprus.
