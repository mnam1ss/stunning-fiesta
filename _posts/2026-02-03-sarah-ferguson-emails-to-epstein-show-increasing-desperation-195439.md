Newly released correspondence from Sarah Ferguson, the former Duchess of York, to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein reportedly reveals a tone of growing personal distress. The emails, which are part of ongoing legal proceedings, are said to span a period from 2010 to 2011. According to reports, the messages describe feelings of isolation and significant emotional strain. The documents have been made public through a court filing in a related case.

A spokesperson for the Duchess has previously stated that she deeply regrets her association with Epstein, who was a convicted sex offender. The release of these communications provides further context to her previously acknowledged connection with him, which she has characterized as a serious error in judgment.

Key points:
• Released emails reportedly show Sarah Ferguson expressing feelings of isolation and distress to Jeffrey Epstein between 2010 and 2011.
• The correspondence has emerged from court filings in an ongoing legal case.
• Ferguson has publicly stated she regrets her past association with the convicted financier.
