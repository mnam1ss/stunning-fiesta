A United States military aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, prompting an immediate search and rescue operation. The incident involves a KC-135 Stratotanker, an aerial refueling plane, according to statements from U.S. Central Command. The command confirmed the crash and stated that efforts to locate the crew and secure the site are currently active.

The cause of the crash is not yet known, and an investigation is expected to follow the initial emergency response. The aircraft was operating as part of ongoing U.S. military missions in the region. No further details regarding the number of personnel on board or their condition have been released at this time.

Key points:
*   A U.S. Air Force KC-135 refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq.
*   U.S. Central Command has initiated search and rescue operations for the crew.
*   The cause of the incident is under investigation.
