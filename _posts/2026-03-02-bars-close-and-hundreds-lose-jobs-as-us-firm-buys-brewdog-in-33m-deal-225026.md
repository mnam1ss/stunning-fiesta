US beverage and cannabis company Tilray Brands has acquired the assets of the UK-based Brewdog bar chain in a deal valued at approximately £33 million. The acquisition follows the craft brewery and pub operator entering administration earlier this year.

Tilray’s purchase includes the Brewdog brewery, the brand itself, and 11 of the company’s bar locations. As a result of the transaction, the majority of the chain’s bars have been closed, leading to hundreds of job losses across the UK. The move consolidates Tilray’s position in the beverage sector, adding a prominent craft beer brand to its portfolio.

Administrators stated the sale was necessary to secure the future of the brand and brewery operations. The remaining open bars are expected to continue trading under the new ownership.

Key points:
- Tilray Brands has purchased Brewdog's assets for about £33 million.
- The deal includes the brewery, brand, and 11 bars, with most other locations closing.
- Hundreds of jobs have been cut as a result of the bar closures.
