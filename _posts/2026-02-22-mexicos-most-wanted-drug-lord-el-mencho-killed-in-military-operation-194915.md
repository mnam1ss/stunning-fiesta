Mexican authorities have reported the death of a high-profile cartel leader following a military operation. Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes, widely known by his alias "El Mencho," was killed during an armed confrontation with security forces. He was the leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel (CJNG), considered one of the most powerful and violent criminal organizations in Mexico. The operation, which involved the Mexican military, resulted in several casualties.

The CJNG has been a primary target for both Mexican and U.S. law enforcement due to its extensive drug trafficking operations and involvement in widespread violence. The death of its leader is a significant event in Mexico's ongoing efforts to combat organized crime. Official statements confirm the incident but have not released further operational details.

Key points:
- Nemesio Oseguera Cervantes, leader of the Jalisco New Generation Cartel, was killed in a military operation.
- The CJNG is a major drug trafficking organization and a key target for security forces.
- The incident marks a major development in Mexico's campaign against powerful criminal cartels.
