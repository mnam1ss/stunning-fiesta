**Veteran Skater Claims Historic Olympic Gold**

Dutch speed skater Jorrit Bergsma has secured a place in Olympic history by winning the men's Mass Start event. At 40 years old, Bergsma is now the oldest athlete ever to win a gold medal in Olympic speed skating. The victory occurred during the latest Winter Games.

Bergsma, a former champion in long-distance events, demonstrated significant endurance and tactical skill throughout the race. His performance culminated in a final sprint that secured the top position on the podium. This achievement adds another gold medal to his previous Olympic successes from earlier in his career.

The result highlights the longevity possible in endurance sports, with Bergsma competing against athletes significantly younger than himself. The Dutch team continues its strong tradition in speed skating with this win.

Key points:
*   Jorrit Bergsma of the Netherlands won Olympic gold in the men's Mass Start speed skating.
*   At age 40, he set a new record as the oldest gold medalist in Olympic speed skating history.
*   The victory extends the Dutch team's notable record in the sport at the Winter Games.
