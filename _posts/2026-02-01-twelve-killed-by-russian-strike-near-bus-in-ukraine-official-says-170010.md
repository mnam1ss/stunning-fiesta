A Russian missile strike on a civilian area in Ukraine's Kharkiv region resulted in multiple casualties on Sunday, according to a Ukrainian official. The attack reportedly hit near a bus stop.

Preliminary information from local authorities states that twelve people were killed in the incident. An additional seven individuals sustained injuries and received medical treatment.

The strike is part of ongoing hostilities in the region, which has seen frequent shelling and missile attacks. Emergency services responded to the scene following the explosion. An investigation into the precise circumstances is ongoing.

Key points:
• A Russian missile strike in Ukraine's Kharkiv region killed twelve people on Sunday.
• Seven other individuals were injured in the attack near a bus stop.
• Local officials provided the preliminary casualty figures as emergency services responded.
