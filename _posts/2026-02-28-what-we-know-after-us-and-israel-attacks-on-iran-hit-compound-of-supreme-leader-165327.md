Following military strikes by the United States and Israel that reportedly struck a compound associated with Iran's supreme leader, regional tensions have escalated significantly. Iran has launched a substantial missile attack toward Israel in response. Concurrently, residents and officials in several Gulf nations, including Qatar, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and the United Arab Emirates, reported hearing explosions, though the origins and targets of these blasts remain unclear. The situation marks a serious and direct exchange of fire between the long-standing adversaries, raising immediate concerns about a broader regional conflict. Authorities are assessing the damage and casualties from the various strikes.

Key points:
- Iran fired missiles at Israel following US and Israeli strikes on an Iranian site linked to its leadership.
- Explosions were reported in multiple Gulf states, including Qatar, Bahrain, Saudi Arabia, and the UAE.
- The exchange represents a significant escalation, increasing fears of a wider regional war.
