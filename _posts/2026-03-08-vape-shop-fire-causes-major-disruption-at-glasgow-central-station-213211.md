A fire at a retail unit in central Glasgow caused significant travel disruption on Sunday afternoon. Emergency services were called to Union Street, near the city's main railway hub, shortly before 4 p.m.

The incident, involving a vape shop, required a substantial response from the Scottish Fire and Rescue Service. Multiple fire appliances and a specialized unit were deployed to the scene to tackle the blaze. While there have been no immediate reports of serious injuries, the fire led to the closure of surrounding streets and impacted operations at the adjacent Glasgow Central Station. The disruption caused delays to rail services and affected passenger movement in the busy area for several hours. Investigations into the cause of the fire are now underway.

Key points:
- A fire at a vape shop on Union Street prompted a major emergency response on Sunday.
- The incident caused significant disruption to Glasgow Central Station and local road closures.
- No serious injuries have been reported; the cause of the fire is under investigation.
