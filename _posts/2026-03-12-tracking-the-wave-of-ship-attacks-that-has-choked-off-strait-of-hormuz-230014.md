A series of maritime incidents in the Gulf region has significantly heightened tensions and disrupted shipping traffic through the critical Strait of Hormuz. According to recent reports, six commercial vessels were targeted in the area over a span of less than two days. These latest events bring the total number of ships reportedly affected by such hostilities since the outbreak of the current conflict to 18. The strategic waterway is a vital conduit for global oil shipments, and the attacks have raised concerns over regional security and the free flow of maritime commerce. No single entity has claimed responsibility for the most recent wave of incidents, which are under investigation by international authorities.

Key points:
• Six ships were reportedly attacked in the Gulf within a 48-hour period.
• The total number of vessels affected during the ongoing conflict has reached 18.
• The incidents have increased security concerns around the Strait of Hormuz, a key global oil transit route.
