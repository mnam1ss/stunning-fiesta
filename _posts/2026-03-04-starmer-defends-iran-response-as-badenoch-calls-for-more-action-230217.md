Prime Minister Keir Starmer has defended the government's response to recent tensions with Iran, following criticism from former US President Donald Trump. The remarks come as Business Secretary Kemi Badenoch has publicly called for stronger measures against Iran.

The controversy stems from the UK's decision not to permit the use of its military bases for initial US-Israel strikes. This refusal was a point of contention in Trump's critique of the Prime Minister's approach. The government maintains its actions are part of a calibrated diplomatic and security strategy.

Key points:
*   PM Starmer has publicly supported the current government stance on Iran.
*   The UK declined a request to host US-Israel strike operations from its bases.
*   Senior cabinet minister Kemi Badenoch is advocating for a more forceful policy.
