A trade association representing fuel retailers has expressed strong disagreement with recent government statements regarding pricing. The dispute arose during a scheduled meeting with government ministers, which the retailers nearly abandoned in protest. They objected to what they described as the use of provocative terms by officials when discussing recent increases in the cost of petrol and diesel. The industry group contends that such language mischaracterizes their pricing practices. The government has been under public pressure to address high fuel costs, which have been a significant concern for motorists and businesses. The meeting ultimately proceeded, though tensions over the characterization of the issue remain unresolved.

Key points:
- Fuel retailers nearly withdrew from a government meeting over a dispute about language used to describe pricing.
- The industry group objected to terms it considered inflammatory regarding recent fuel price increases.
- The underlying issue involves ongoing government scrutiny of high costs at the pump.
