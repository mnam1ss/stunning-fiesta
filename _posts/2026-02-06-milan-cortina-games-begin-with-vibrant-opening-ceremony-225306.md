The 2026 Winter Olympic Games commenced in Italy with an opening ceremony held in Milan on Friday. The event marks the start of the first Winter Olympics hosted in Italy since the 2006 Turin Games. Athletes from participating nations paraded into the stadium during the traditional ceremony, which featured a blend of cultural performances and musical acts.

The ceremony included performances by internationally recognized artists, including Italian tenor Andrea Bocelli and American singer Mariah Carey. The program highlighted themes of Italian culture and winter sports as the Games officially opened. The competitions will be staged across multiple venues in Milan and the Alpine region of Cortina d'Ampezzo over the coming weeks.

Key points:
• The Milan-Cortina Winter Olympics opened with a ceremony in Milan.
• Performers at the event included Andrea Bocelli and Mariah Carey.
• The Games are the first Winter Olympics in Italy since 2006.
