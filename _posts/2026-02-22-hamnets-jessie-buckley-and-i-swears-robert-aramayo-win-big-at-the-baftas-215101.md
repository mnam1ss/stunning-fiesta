**British and Irish Actors Secure Major BAFTA Honors**

At the recent British Academy of Film and Television Arts (BAFTA) awards, several notable wins highlighted the success of performers in stage and screen adaptations. Actor Robert Aramayo, recognized for his role in the film *I Swear*, received the award for Best Actor. His victory placed him ahead of several prominent American nominees in the category.

In the theatre awards segment, Jessie Buckley was awarded Best Actress for her performance in the stage production *Hamnet*. Meanwhile, the film *One Battle After Another* emerged as a significant winner of the evening, securing a total of six awards across various categories. The ceremony celebrated achievements in both cinematic and theatrical arts.

Key points:
- Robert Aramayo won the BAFTA for Best Actor for his role in *I Swear*.
- Jessie Buckley won the BAFTA for Best Actress for her performance in *Hamnet*.
- The film *One Battle After Another* received six awards at the ceremony.
