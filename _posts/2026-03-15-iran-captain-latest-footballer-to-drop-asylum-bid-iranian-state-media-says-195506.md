Iranian state media reports that the captain of the country's women's national football team has reversed a decision to seek asylum in Australia. The player was part of a delegation that traveled to Australia for a recent tournament.

According to the reports, a majority of the seven delegation members who initially expressed a desire to stay abroad have now opted to return to Iran. This brings the total number of individuals choosing to go back to five.

The development follows a period of uncertainty regarding the delegation's status after their sporting event concluded. No specific reasons for the change in plans were provided in the state media announcement.

Key points:
• Iran's women's football captain has withdrawn an asylum application in Australia.
• Five of seven delegation members who initially sought to stay are now returning home.
• The information was reported by Iranian state media, which did not cite reasons for the reversal.
