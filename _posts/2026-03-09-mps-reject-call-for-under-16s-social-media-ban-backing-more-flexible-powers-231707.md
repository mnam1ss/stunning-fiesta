UK Parliament Rejects Proposed Social Media Ban for Under-16s

Members of Parliament have voted against a legislative proposal to prohibit children under the age of 16 from accessing social media platforms. The decision follows earlier support for such a ban from the House of Lords. Instead, MPs have endorsed a more adaptable regulatory framework, granting authorities broader powers to address online safety concerns for young people.

The move signals a preference for flexible enforcement tools over a blanket age restriction. The government's stated focus remains on implementing the Online Safety Act, which places duties on tech companies to protect children from harmful content. Proponents of the rejected ban argued it was necessary for mental health, while opponents cited enforcement challenges and parental choice.

Key points:
• MPs voted down a proposal to ban under-16s from social media platforms.
• The decision favors granting regulators more flexible powers over a strict age prohibition.
• The government will continue implementing the broader Online Safety Act.
