Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer has acknowledged that the appointment of Lord Peter Mandelson to a government role was an error, following the release of documents concerning the peer. The documents indicate that prior to the appointment, officials had advised that Lord Mandelson's past association with the late financier Jeffrey Epstein could present a risk to the government's reputation.

A spokesperson for the Prime Minister's office at 10 Downing Street has stated there was no attempt to conceal this information from the public. The released records show the advice was part of the standard vetting process conducted before Lord Mandelson's recent return to an advisory position.

Key points:
*   The Prime Minister has stated the appointment of Lord Mandelson was a mistake.
*   Released documents show officials previously warned of a potential reputational risk due to the peer's connection to Jeffrey Epstein.
*   Downing Street denies any cover-up, stating the advice was part of normal vetting procedures.
