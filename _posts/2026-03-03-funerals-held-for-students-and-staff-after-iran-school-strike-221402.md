Funerals have been held in Iran for individuals described by authorities as students and school staff members. The ceremonies follow an incident in the southern city of Minab this past Saturday.

Iranian officials have attributed the deaths to a military strike. They have publicly stated that the United States and Israel were responsible for carrying out the attack. The claims have not been independently verified, and there has been no immediate comment from the U.S. or Israeli governments regarding the specific allegation.

The event has drawn local mourners and highlights ongoing regional tensions. Further details about the circumstances or the exact number of casualties have not been officially released.

Key points:
• Funerals were conducted in Iran for people identified as students and school employees.
• Iranian authorities allege a U.S.-Israeli strike in Minab caused the deaths on Saturday.
• The accusation remains unverified externally, with no direct response from the named nations.
