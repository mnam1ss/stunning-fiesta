Iranian Kurdish opposition groups based in northern Iraq have stated they are prepared to cross into Iran but deny having already done so, according to a BBC report. Representatives from these exiled groups emphasized they have conducted extensive preparations. Their comments come amid ongoing protests within Iran and regional tensions.

The groups, operating from the Kurdistan Region of Iraq, did not specify a timeline for any potential cross-border movements. They maintain that their activities are organized and planned. Iranian authorities have previously accused neighboring countries of harboring groups they consider separatist threats.

**Key points:**
*   Exiled Iranian Kurdish groups in northern Iraq say they are prepared to enter Iran but deny current incursions.
*   The statements follow a period of domestic unrest in Iran and longstanding regional frictions.
*   Iranian officials have repeatedly voiced concerns about opposition activities originating from Iraqi Kurdistan.
