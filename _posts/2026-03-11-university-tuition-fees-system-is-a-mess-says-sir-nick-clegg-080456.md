Former Deputy Prime Minister Sir Nick Clegg has described the UK's university tuition fees system as a "mess." He made the remarks during a BBC interview, acknowledging he would accept any criticism directed at him for his role in the policy's development. Clegg was a prominent figure in the coalition government that oversaw a significant increase in tuition fees in England.

The current system, which includes student loans and a graduate repayment scheme, has been the subject of ongoing public and political debate regarding its cost and fairness.

Key points:
*   Sir Nick Clegg has stated the university tuition fee system in the UK is now a "mess."
*   He acknowledged his own involvement in the policy and said he would accept related criticism.
*   The tuition fees model continues to be a major topic of political and social discussion.
