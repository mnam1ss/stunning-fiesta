US news anchor Savannah Guthrie has issued a new public appeal regarding the disappearance of her 84-year-old mother, Nancy Guthrie. In a video statement, Guthrie indicated she is aware of communications from those believed to be involved, directly stating a willingness to provide payment for her mother's safe return. The elderly woman is suspected to have been abducted, though specific details surrounding the circumstances have not been publicly released by authorities. The case continues to be an active investigation. Guthrie's latest plea follows previous efforts to secure information that could lead to her mother's location.

Key points:
- Savannah Guthrie has publicly addressed individuals connected to her mother's suspected abduction.
- The news anchor expressed a readiness to offer payment for Nancy Guthrie's safe release.
- Law enforcement officials are continuing their investigation into the disappearance.
