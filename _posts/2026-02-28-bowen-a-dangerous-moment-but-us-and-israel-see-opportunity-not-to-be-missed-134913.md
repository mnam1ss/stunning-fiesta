**US and Israel Assess Iran's Internal Challenges as Strategic Moment**

Senior officials from the United States and Israel reportedly assess that the Iranian government is currently facing significant internal pressures, creating a period of strategic vulnerability. According to analysts, this view is shaping diplomatic and security calculations between the two allies. The assessment is said to be based on Iran's ongoing economic difficulties and the sustained domestic unrest following widespread protests. While the situation is described by some observers as a delicate and potentially dangerous phase, the prevailing analysis in Washington and Jerusalem suggests it may also present a unique opportunity to influence Tehran's regional behavior. The developments are being closely monitored as part of broader international efforts regarding Iran's nuclear program and regional activities.

Key points:
*   U.S. and Israeli analysts believe Iran's government is weakened by internal economic and social pressures.
*   This period is viewed as both a sensitive moment and a potential opening for diplomatic influence.
*   The assessments are informing allied strategies on issues including regional security and nuclear negotiations.
