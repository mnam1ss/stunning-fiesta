A Russian missile strike in Ukraine on Sunday resulted in the deaths of twelve miners and injured seven others, according to a statement from the affected energy company. The attack occurred near a bus that was transporting the workers.

Ukrainian authorities reported that the strike hit the Donetsk region, an area that has seen intense fighting. Emergency services responded to the scene to assist the wounded and recover the deceased. The company has not released the identities of the victims pending notification of their families.

Russian officials have not issued an immediate comment on the reported incident. The attack is part of a wider pattern of strikes on civilian infrastructure and personnel in the region.

Key points:
• Twelve miners were killed and seven injured in a missile strike.
• The attack targeted a worker transport vehicle in the Donetsk region.
• Russian authorities have not yet commented on the reported incident.
