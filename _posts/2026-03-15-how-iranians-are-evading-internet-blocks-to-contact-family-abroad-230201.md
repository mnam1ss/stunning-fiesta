Iranians are increasingly using technical methods to circumvent government-imposed restrictions on digital communication, according to a recent report. With official blocks on popular phone and internet services, citizens are seeking alternative ways to maintain contact with relatives living outside the country. The methods reportedly involve a range of tools and applications designed to bypass state controls, allowing for the exchange of messages and information. This activity highlights ongoing challenges in digital access within the nation, as authorities continue to enforce broad limitations on online connectivity. The situation reflects a persistent effort by individuals to navigate around censorship measures to preserve personal and familial links across borders.

Key points:
- Citizens are employing various technical tools to bypass state internet and phone restrictions.
- The primary aim is to maintain communication with family members residing internationally.
- These efforts occur against a backdrop of sustained government controls on digital connectivity.
