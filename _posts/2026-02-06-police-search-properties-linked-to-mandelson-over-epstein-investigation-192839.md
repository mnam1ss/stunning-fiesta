Police in the United Kingdom have executed search warrants at properties connected to Lord Peter Mandelson, a former Labour government minister and ambassador to the United States. The searches are part of an ongoing investigation into potential misconduct in a public office, which is being conducted in connection with the wider inquiry concerning the late financier Jeffrey Epstein.

Authorities have not disclosed the specific locations searched or detailed the nature of any evidence sought. The investigation is being led by officers from the Metropolitan Police, who stated the action is a planned development in their established lines of inquiry. Lord Mandelson has previously stated he had no knowledge of any criminal activity by Epstein at the time of their social acquaintance.

Key points:
*   Properties linked to former minister Lord Peter Mandelson have been searched by police.
*   The action is part of a misconduct probe related to the Jeffrey Epstein investigation.
*   The Metropolitan Police confirm this as a planned step in their ongoing inquiry.
