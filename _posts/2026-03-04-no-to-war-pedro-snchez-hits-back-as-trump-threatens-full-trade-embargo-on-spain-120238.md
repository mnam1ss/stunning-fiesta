Spanish Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez has issued a firm response to recent economic threats from the United States. The statement comes after former U.S. President Donald Trump suggested he would impose a full trade embargo on Spain if he were to return to office.

In his public remarks, Prime Minister Sánchez emphasized a stance of diplomacy and opposition to conflict, directly countering the nature of the threat. He did not specify potential retaliatory economic measures but framed his country's position as one favoring peaceful international relations.

The exchange highlights ongoing tensions in the transatlantic partnership, with European allies closely monitoring U.S. political rhetoric ahead of the upcoming election. The possibility of such a severe trade restriction has raised concerns about its potential impact on both economies.

Key points:
*   Spanish PM Pedro Sánchez has publicly rejected a threat from Donald Trump to halt all trade with Spain.
*   Sánchez advocated for a diplomatic approach, contrasting with the warning of an economic embargo.
*   The incident underscores current strains in U.S.-Europe relations and economic anxieties.
