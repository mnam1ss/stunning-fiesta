Iranian military forces have carried out strikes against bases belonging to Iranian Kurdish opposition groups in northern Iraq, according to regional reports. The operations targeted what Iranian authorities described as militant headquarters. The attacks occur within a broader regional context of heightened tensions.

Analysts note that the strikes coincide with ongoing discussions about the role of various Kurdish factions in the wider geopolitical landscape of the Middle East. There is speculation among observers regarding the potential alignment of these groups with other international powers involved in regional disputes. Iraqi officials have previously protested against such cross-border military actions, citing violations of sovereignty.

Key points:
*   Iranian forces conducted strikes on bases of Iranian Kurdish opposition groups inside Iraq.
*   The military action occurs amid regional tensions and discussions about Kurdish groups' allegiances.
*   Cross-border strikes have historically drawn diplomatic protests from Iraqi authorities.
