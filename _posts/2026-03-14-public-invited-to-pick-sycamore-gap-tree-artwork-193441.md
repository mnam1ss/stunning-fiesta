The public is being asked to vote on proposals for a permanent artwork to commemorate the Sycamore Gap tree in Northumberland. The historic tree, which stood beside Hadrian's Wall, was illegally cut down last year. The competition, organized by the Northumberland National Park Authority and the National Trust, invited artists to submit concepts for a new piece. The winning artist will receive approximately half of the salvaged timber from the felled tree to create their work. Online voting for the shortlisted proposals is now open and will close on 28 March. The selected artwork is intended to provide a lasting legacy for the site, which remains a significant landmark.

Key points:
*   A public vote will determine the winning design for a Sycamore Gap memorial artwork.
*   The selected artist will be granted use of about 50% of the original tree's timber.
*   The online voting period for the shortlisted proposals concludes on 28 March.
