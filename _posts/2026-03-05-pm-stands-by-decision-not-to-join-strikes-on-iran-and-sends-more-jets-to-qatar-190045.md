**UK Prime Minister Defends Iran Stance, Bolsters Regional Air Presence**

The UK Prime Minister has reaffirmed the government's position not to participate in recent military strikes against Iran, emphasizing a preference for diplomatic solutions. Speaking to reporters, the leader stated that pursuing a peaceful resolution through dialogue remains the most effective path. The goal, according to the Prime Minister, is to achieve an agreement where Iran abandons its nuclear weapons program.

Concurrently, the Ministry of Defence has announced the deployment of additional Royal Air Force fighter jets to Qatar. Officials describe this move as a routine reinforcement of existing operations in the region, aimed at ensuring stability and supporting allies. The deployment is not characterized as a direct response to the recent strikes.

Key points:
*   The UK government will not join military actions against Iran, advocating for diplomatic negotiations instead.
*   Additional RAF fighter jets have been sent to Qatar as part of ongoing regional operations.
*   The stated objective is a negotiated settlement to halt Iran's nuclear ambitions.
