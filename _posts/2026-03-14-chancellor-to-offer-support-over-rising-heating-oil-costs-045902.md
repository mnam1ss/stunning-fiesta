Chancellor Rachel Reeves has announced that the Treasury is developing measures to assist households struggling with high heating oil expenses. The statement comes amid ongoing concerns over energy affordability for many consumers. Officials are currently examining a range of potential support mechanisms, with a focus on those considered most at risk from continued high bills. Further details regarding the specific nature of the assistance or a timeline for implementation were not provided in the initial announcement. The government's attention to this issue follows sustained pressure regarding the broader cost of living, with heating oil costs being a significant concern for many homes not connected to the main gas grid.

Key points:
*   The Treasury is formulating support options for households facing high heating oil costs.
*   Chancellor Rachel Reeves indicated the plans would target those most vulnerable to energy bills.
*   Specific policy details and a rollout schedule have not yet been disclosed.
