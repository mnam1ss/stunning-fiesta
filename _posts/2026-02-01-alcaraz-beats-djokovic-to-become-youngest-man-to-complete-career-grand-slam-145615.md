Carlos Alcaraz has won the Australian Open men's singles title, defeating Novak Djokovic in the final. With this victory, the 20-year-old Spaniard has become the youngest male tennis player in history to achieve a career Grand Slam, having now won all four major championships. The win at Melbourne Park also halted Djokovic's bid for a record-extending 25th Grand Slam singles title. The match concluded Alcaraz's successful campaign to secure the one major trophy that had eluded him, following his previous victories at the US Open, Wimbledon, and the French Open.

Key points:
*   Carlos Alcaraz is the youngest man to win all four Grand Slam tournaments.
*   The victory prevented Novak Djokovic from winning a 25th major title.
*   Alcaraz secured the Australian Open, the final major needed for his career Grand Slam.
