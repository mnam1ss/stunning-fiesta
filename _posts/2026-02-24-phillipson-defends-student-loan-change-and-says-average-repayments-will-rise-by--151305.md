**Education Secretary Defends Student Loan Adjustments Amid Graduate Concerns**

Education Secretary Bridget Phillipson has defended recent changes to the student loan repayment system in England, stating that the average graduate will see their monthly repayments increase by approximately £8. The adjustments have sparked criticism from some graduates, who report that higher repayments are financially burdensome and have influenced decisions to reduce their working hours.

Phillipson addressed the policy in a statement, framing the change as a necessary measure for the long-term sustainability of the higher education funding model. The government maintains that the system remains progressive, with repayments tied to income. However, the reaction highlights ongoing debates over the cost of university education and graduate debt.

Key points:
*   The Education Secretary states the average monthly student loan repayment will rise by about £8.
*   Some graduates report the changes are affecting their finances and work choices.
*   The government describes the adjustment as part of ensuring the funding system's viability.
