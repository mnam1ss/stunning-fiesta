Transport for London (TfL) experienced a significant data breach in 2024 that impacted an estimated 10 million individuals, according to a BBC report. The incident, which involved unauthorized access to TfL's systems, is one of the largest known breaches of a UK public transport authority. While the full scope of the compromised information is still under review, it is understood to include customer contact details and journey history.

In response to the report, TfL stated it has maintained communication with its customers regarding the incident since its discovery and is committed to implementing all required measures to address the situation. The organization has not provided specific details on how the breach occurred or whether any financial data was accessed. Investigations by relevant authorities are ongoing.

Key points:
*   A 2024 cyber attack on Transport for London affected approximately 10 million people.
*   TfL states it has informed customers and will take necessary action following the breach.
*   The nature of the accessed data and the cause of the incident remain under investigation.
