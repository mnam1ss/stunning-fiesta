A new interim report on maternity services in England has identified systemic issues across all stages of care. The review, commissioned by the government, states that services are currently failing to meet acceptable standards of safety and support.

The analysis cites severe staffing shortages as a primary challenge, leading to overstretched services and increased risk. It also highlights significant disparities in care quality and outcomes linked to racial and ethnic background, indicating that some groups receive a lower standard of treatment.

The report's findings are part of an ongoing investigation aimed at formulating recommendations for a substantial overhaul of the maternity system to ensure consistent and equitable care for all families.

Key points:
*   An interim review finds England's maternity services are failing to provide consistently safe care.
*   Severe staff shortages and racial disparities in treatment are cited as key contributing factors.
*   Problems have been identified at every stage of the maternity journey, from pregnancy to postnatal support.
