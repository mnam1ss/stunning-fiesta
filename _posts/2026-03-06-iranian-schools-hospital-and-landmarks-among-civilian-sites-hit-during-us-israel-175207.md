**Civilian Infrastructure Reportedly Damaged in Strikes on Iran**

A series of strikes in Iran has reportedly damaged several civilian sites, including schools, a hospital, and cultural landmarks, according to local reports. The strikes, which have been attributed to US and Israeli forces, have caused significant disruption in the capital, Tehran. Residents describe an exodus from the city, with one local source characterizing the atmosphere as one of widespread evacuation.

The full extent of the damage and any potential casualties remains unclear as official assessments are ongoing. The reported targeting of non-military infrastructure has raised immediate concerns about the impact on civilian populations. Authorities have not yet released a comprehensive statement detailing the scope of the incidents.

**Key points:**
*   Strikes attributed to US and Israeli forces are reported to have hit schools, a hospital, and landmarks in Iran.
*   Witnesses in Tehran describe a mass departure of residents from the city following the attacks.
*   The extent of damage and casualties is currently unconfirmed as investigations proceed.
