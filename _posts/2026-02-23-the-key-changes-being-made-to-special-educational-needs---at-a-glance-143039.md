The Department for Education has announced a series of planned reforms to the Special Educational Needs and Disabilities (SEND) system in England. The proposals, detailed in a new government response, outline a multi-year strategy aimed at improving consistency and outcomes for children and young people. Officials state the changes are intended to address concerns over variable support across local authorities and lengthy waiting times for assessments. The plan emphasizes creating clearer national standards for support, strengthening accountability for local councils and health services, and increasing the availability of specialist school places. The government has indicated that further details and a timeline for implementation will be developed in consultation with local authorities, schools, and families.

Key points:
*   New national standards are proposed to make SEND support more consistent across England.
*   The reforms aim to improve accountability for local councils and integrated health services.
*   Plans include expanding the capacity of specialist educational settings and provisions.
