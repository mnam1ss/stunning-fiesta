On the final day of the UK's Covid-19 public inquiry, its chair has addressed concerns over the investigation's duration and expense. Baroness Heather Hallett stated that concluding the extensive hearings in under four years represented a significant logistical achievement. The inquiry, which began in 2022, has heard testimony from hundreds of witnesses, including former Prime Ministers and senior officials, to examine the nation's pandemic preparedness and response.

However, the process has faced scrutiny over its reported total cost, which is estimated to be around £200 million. Critics have questioned whether this expenditure represents value for money for the public. The inquiry's final report, analyzing the evidence gathered, is expected to be published at a later date.

Key points:
*   The inquiry chair highlighted completing the hearings in under four years as an achievement.
*   The total cost, estimated at £200 million, has been questioned by critics.
*   A final report with the inquiry's findings will be published following the conclusion of hearings.
