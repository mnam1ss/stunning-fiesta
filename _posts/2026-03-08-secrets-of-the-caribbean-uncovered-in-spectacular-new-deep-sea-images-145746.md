**New Deep-Sea Survey Reveals Caribbean Seafloor Features**

A recent scientific expedition has documented previously uncharted regions of the Caribbean seafloor, capturing high-resolution imagery of its underwater topography and ecosystems. The survey, conducted by a team of marine researchers, utilized advanced submersible technology to explore depths seldom reached by prior studies.

The collected data includes detailed scans of extensive submerged mountain ranges, also known as seamounts. The imagery further shows complex geological formations and dense communities of deep-sea corals, some exhibiting notable structures and coloration. During the exploration, researchers also recorded several marine organisms that appear to be undocumented in existing scientific literature, indicating potential new species. The findings are expected to contribute significantly to the understanding of deep-sea biodiversity and geological processes in the region.

Key points:
*   The expedition mapped substantial underwater mountain ranges and coral formations.
*   Imagery revealed multiple marine species that may be newly identified by science.
*   The data aims to enhance knowledge of deep-sea ecosystems and geology.
