The Duke of York has returned the lease on a property located on the Crown Estate in Ascot, according to official records. The move involves a residence that was previously held under his name. Documentation related to the lease indicates the yearly rental cost for the property was set at a substantial figure. This action represents a change in the Duke’s residential arrangements concerning Crown Estate holdings.

The Crown Estate, a collection of lands and holdings belonging to the British monarch in right of the Crown, manages its properties independently. The Ascot area is known for its royal connections and high-value real estate. This development follows other recent adjustments to the royal family’s use of various properties and financial arrangements.

Key points:
- The Duke of York has relinquished the lease for a Crown Estate home in Ascot.
- Official lease documents listed the annual rent for the property.
- The Crown Estate is a commercial property portfolio managed separately from the private holdings of the royal family.
