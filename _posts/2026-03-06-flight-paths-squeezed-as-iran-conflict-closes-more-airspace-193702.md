A recent drone strike on Azerbaijan has further complicated flight operations in the region, leading to additional airspace restrictions. This incident contributes to ongoing aviation disruptions stemming from heightened tensions in the Gulf area.

Airlines are now facing a more limited set of flight path options as they adjust routes to avoid newly closed or risky airspace. The narrowing corridors are causing some flights to be rerouted, which can result in longer travel times and increased operational costs for carriers. Aviation authorities are monitoring the situation closely and advising flight planners to exercise caution.

The closures are part of a broader pattern of airspace limitations affecting commercial aviation due to regional conflicts, forcing continuous adjustments to international flight schedules.

Key points:
• A drone attack in Azerbaijan has led to new airspace closures.
• Airlines are experiencing fewer available flight paths, causing reroutes.
• The situation adds to existing aviation disruptions in the Gulf region.
