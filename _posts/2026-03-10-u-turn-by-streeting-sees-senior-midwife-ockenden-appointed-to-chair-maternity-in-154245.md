**Senior Midwife to Lead Leeds Maternity Services Inquiry**

The independent inquiry into maternity services at University Hospitals Leeds NHS Trust has a new chair following a reversal of the initial appointment. Donna Ockenden, a senior midwife with extensive experience leading high-profile maternity investigations, has been named to head the review.

The inquiry was established to examine concerns over the quality and safety of maternity care provided by the trust. Ms. Ockenden previously chaired the landmark review into maternity failings at the Shrewsbury and Telford Hospital NHS Trust, which published its findings in 2022.

Her appointment comes after the initial choice for chair, a former NHS trust chair, was withdrawn. Health officials stated the change was made to ensure the review had the most appropriate clinical expertise and experience for the task ahead. The inquiry is expected to engage with affected families and staff.

Key points:
*   Donna Ockenden, who led the Shrewsbury maternity review, will chair the Leeds inquiry.
*   The appointment follows a reversal of the government's initial choice for the role.
*   The investigation will examine maternity care standards at University Hospitals Leeds.
