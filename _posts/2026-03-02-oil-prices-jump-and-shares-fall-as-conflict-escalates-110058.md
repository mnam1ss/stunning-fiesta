Global financial markets reacted sharply Monday to a significant escalation in Middle East hostilities. The price of Brent crude oil surged over 4% in early trading, reflecting investor concerns over potential disruptions to supply from a key oil-producing region. Concurrently, major stock indices in Europe and Asia declined as the heightened geopolitical risk prompted a shift away from equities.

Market analysts note that the immediate price movement is a reaction to the increased uncertainty. However, they caution that the duration and scope of the conflict will be the primary determinant of its longer-term economic impact. A sustained military engagement raises the prospect of continued volatility and further increases in energy costs worldwide, which could dampen economic growth and intensify inflationary pressures.

Key points:
• Oil prices rose sharply due to supply disruption fears.
• Global stock markets fell amid increased investor risk aversion.
• Analysts warn prolonged conflict may lead to higher energy costs and economic strain.
