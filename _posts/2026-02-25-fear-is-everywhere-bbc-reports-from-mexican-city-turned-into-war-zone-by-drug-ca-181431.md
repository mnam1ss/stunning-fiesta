Residents of the northern Mexican city of Culiacán are reporting widespread fear as intense clashes between criminal groups severely disrupt daily life. According to a recent international news report, the violence stems from an internal conflict within the Sinaloa cartel, where rival factions are fighting for dominance. The situation has transformed parts of the city into areas resembling active conflict zones, with frequent shootings and blockades on major roads.

Local authorities have acknowledged the surge in hostilities, which has led to business closures and restricted movement for civilians. Officials have deployed additional security forces in response to the escalating confrontations. The ongoing feud highlights the persistent challenges faced by regions with a strong cartel presence, where internal power struggles can quickly erupt into public violence.

Key points:
- Internal conflict within the Sinaloa cartel is causing severe violence in Culiacán.
- The clashes have created a climate of fear, disrupting normal civilian activities.
- Security forces have been mobilized in response to the escalating situation.
