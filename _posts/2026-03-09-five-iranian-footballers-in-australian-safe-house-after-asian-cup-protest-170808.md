Five members of Iran’s national football team are reportedly staying in a secure location in Australia following a political gesture made during the recent Asian Cup tournament. According to international media reports, the players did not participate in singing the national anthem before a match, an act that has drawn significant criticism from some quarters within Iran. One prominent commentator in the country has labeled the athletes as "wartime traitors" for their silent protest. The situation has raised concerns for the safety and welfare of the players and the wider team. Australian authorities have not publicly commented on the reports regarding the players' current accommodations or security status. The incident highlights ongoing tensions surrounding expressions of political dissent by Iranian athletes competing internationally.

Key points:
- Five Iranian footballers are said to be in a safe house in Australia after not singing their national anthem.
- The silent protest during the Asian Cup led to them being called "traitors" by a critic in Iran.
- The players' safety has become a focal point following the controversial incident.
