The Royal Air Force is currently responding to a suspected drone strike at a British military installation in Cyprus, according to a statement from the UK Ministry of Defence. The incident occurred at RAF Akrotiri, a strategic airbase located on the southern coast of the island. Initial reports indicate the attack involved an unmanned aerial system. While the full extent of the damage is being assessed, authorities have confirmed there were no injuries to personnel stationed at the base. The base is a key operational site for UK and allied forces in the region. An investigation into the origin and specifics of the incident is now underway.

Key points:
- The UK MoD confirmed a response to a suspected drone strike at RAF Akrotiri in Cyprus.
- No casualties have been reported following the incident.
- An assessment of damage and an investigation into the attack are in progress.
