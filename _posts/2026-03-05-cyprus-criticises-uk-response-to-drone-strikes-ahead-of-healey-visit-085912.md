The Cypriot government has expressed dissatisfaction with the United Kingdom's approach to sharing intelligence following recent drone attacks in the region. The criticism comes ahead of a scheduled visit to Cyprus by UK Defence Secretary John Healey.

Officials in Nicosia have reportedly raised concerns about the timeliness and detail of information provided by British counterparts after incidents involving unmanned aerial vehicles. The nature of the specific drone strikes referenced was not detailed in the initial reports. The diplomatic remarks highlight ongoing discussions about security cooperation between the two nations, particularly regarding stability in the Eastern Mediterranean.

Key points:
*   Cyprus has formally criticized the UK's handling of intelligence sharing after drone attacks.
*   The comments were made prior to an upcoming visit by UK Defence Secretary John Healey.
*   The issue centers on the flow of security information between the two allied nations.
