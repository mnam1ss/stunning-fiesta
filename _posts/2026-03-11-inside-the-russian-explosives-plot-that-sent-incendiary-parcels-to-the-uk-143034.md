A man charged with terrorism offenses for allegedly acting on behalf of Russia has given an interview to the BBC. Aleksandr Suranovas is accused of being involved in a plot to send incendiary devices through the postal system to addresses in the United Kingdom earlier this year. In the interview, he reportedly discusses the nature of the alleged operation. British authorities have previously stated that the parcels, which were safely intercepted, were intended to cause harm and were part of a wider pattern of hostile activity. The case is currently proceeding through the UK legal system.

Key points:
- A suspect has spoken publicly about allegations of a Russian-linked plot to mail incendiary devices to the UK.
- The parcels were intercepted and did not cause any physical harm.
- The individual faces terrorism charges and the judicial process is ongoing.
