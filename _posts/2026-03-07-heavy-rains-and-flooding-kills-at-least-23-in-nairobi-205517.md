At least 23 people have died following days of intense rainfall that caused severe flooding in Nairobi, Kenya’s capital. Emergency services are conducting rescue operations as water levels continue to rise in several neighborhoods.

The severe weather has significantly disrupted transportation across the city. Major roadways are impassable due to high water, hindering both emergency response and general movement. Air travel has also been affected, with numerous flight cancellations and delays reported at the city’s main international airport.

Authorities have advised residents in low-lying areas to seek higher ground and are assessing the full extent of the damage. The situation remains fluid as weather forecasts predict further rainfall in the coming days.

Key points:
- The death toll from flooding in Nairobi has reached 23.
- Major road and air travel disruptions are ongoing.
- Further rainfall is expected, complicating relief efforts.
