A Conservative member of the House of Lords has resigned following an investigation by the chamber's standards watchdog. The inquiry examined his communications with government ministers and their advisers regarding the procurement of personal protective equipment (PPE) during the COVID-19 pandemic.

The peer, who had been suspended from the parliamentary group, stepped down ahead of the publication of the full findings. The standards commissioner's investigation focused on whether proper procedures were followed in his interactions concerning PPE contracts, a sector that saw rapid and high-value government spending during the health crisis. His resignation brings the matter to a close within the Lords' disciplinary system.
