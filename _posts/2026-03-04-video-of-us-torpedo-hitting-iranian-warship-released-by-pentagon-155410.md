Pentagon Releases Footage of Anti-Ship Strike in Indian Ocean

The U.S. Department of Defense has publicly released video footage showing a torpedo strike on a vessel it identifies as an Iranian warship. The incident reportedly occurred in the Indian Ocean. In a statement, U.S. Secretary of Defense Pete Hegseth confirmed the U.S. military action, stating the targeted vessel was sunk.

The release of the operational footage is a rare move, providing visual documentation of the engagement. The Pentagon has not provided immediate details on the specific circumstances leading to the strike, including the date of the incident or whether there were any casualties. The action underscores ongoing military tensions in key maritime regions. Iranian officials have not yet issued a public response to the Pentagon's latest release.

Key points:
• The U.S. Pentagon has released video showing a torpedo striking a vessel described as an Iranian warship.
• U.S. Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth stated the U.S. military sank the ship in the Indian Ocean.
• The circumstances of the engagement and any potential casualties have not been detailed.
