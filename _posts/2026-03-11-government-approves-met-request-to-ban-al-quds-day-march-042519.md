The UK government has approved a request from London's Metropolitan Police to prohibit a planned Al Quds Day march in the capital this Sunday. The decision follows a formal application from police authorities, who cited concerns over public safety and the potential for disorder. The march, which was scheduled to proceed through central London, will now not be permitted to take place.

Authorities have stated that the ban is a specific operational measure for this single event, based on an assessment of intelligence and the risk of serious disruption. Organizers of the annual demonstration, which expresses solidarity with Palestinians, have previously held the event without a government-imposed prohibition. Officials emphasize the decision is not taken lightly and is based solely on current security assessments for the upcoming weekend.
