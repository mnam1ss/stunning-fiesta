Hamas has publicly called on Iran, a major financial and military backer, to cease its recent military actions against neighboring Gulf states. The Palestinian political and militant organization issued the statement amid heightened regional tensions. In the same communication, Hamas reiterated its support for Iran's right to respond to what it described as aggressive actions from the United States and Israel. The appeal highlights the complex diplomatic relationships in the Middle East, where alliances often intersect with conflicting regional interests. The statement did not specify which recent Iranian actions it was referencing, but it comes during a period of reported drone and missile strikes in the region.

Key points:
* Hamas has requested that Iran stop its attacks on Gulf Arab states.
* The group simultaneously affirmed Iran's right to self-defense against the U.S. and Israel.
* The statement underscores the intricate and sometimes contradictory nature of Middle Eastern alliances.
