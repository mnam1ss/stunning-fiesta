Israeli President Isaac Herzog has responded to international legal questions surrounding the country's military actions against Iran by expressing strong disagreement with the premise of such doubts. In recent remarks, Herzog stated that Israel's operations are conducted as a measure of protection, not only for its own citizens but also for the security of the broader Middle East and European nations. He characterized the suggestion that these actions might be unlawful as a bewildering concept, emphasizing the defensive nature of the campaign. The comments come amid ongoing scrutiny from some global bodies and foreign governments regarding the legal justification for the strikes.

Key points:
- President Isaac Herzog firmly rejected questions about the legality of Israel's military actions against Iran.
- He asserted the operations are defensive, aimed at protecting Israel, the region, and Europe.
- The statement addresses increasing international scrutiny over the legal basis for the conflict.
