Residents of Tehran have expressed growing concerns about potential food shortages and rising prices as ongoing strikes affect the capital's supply chains. According to reports, locals are worried about the availability of basic groceries, with the situation linked to the continuation of regional conflict.

The disruptions have led to increased anxiety among the population regarding how long current conditions may persist. Market observers note that the strikes are impacting the distribution of goods, contributing to both scarcity and higher costs for consumers.

Officials have not yet released a comprehensive statement addressing the specific supply chain issues or outlining mitigation measures. The developing situation is being monitored as residents adjust their daily purchasing habits.

Key points:
- Tehran residents report concerns over grocery availability and increasing prices.
- Ongoing strikes in the capital are disrupting supply chains.
- The duration of regional conflict is cited as a contributing factor to the uncertainty.
