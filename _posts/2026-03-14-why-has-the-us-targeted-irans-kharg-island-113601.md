The United States has recently imposed sanctions on Iran's Kharg Island, a significant location within the Persian Gulf. The island serves as a primary export terminal for the country's crude oil, housing extensive storage and loading facilities. This action is part of a broader series of U.S. measures aimed at increasing economic pressure on Iran.

The sanctions specifically target the infrastructure and entities operating on the island, which is a central hub for Iran's energy sector. U.S. officials state the move is intended to further restrict Iran's oil revenue. Analysts note that the island's strategic importance makes it a focal point in ongoing geopolitical tensions surrounding Iran's nuclear program and regional activities.

Key points:
*   Kharg Island is a major oil export terminal for Iran.
*   U.S. sanctions target its critical energy infrastructure.
*   The action aims to constrain Iran's oil revenue.
