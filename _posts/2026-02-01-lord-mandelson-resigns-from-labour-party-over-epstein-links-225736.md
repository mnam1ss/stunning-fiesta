Former Labour cabinet minister Lord Peter Mandelson has announced his resignation from the party, citing his past association with the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. In a statement, Lord Mandelson indicated the decision was made to avoid creating additional difficulties for the party, acknowledging that his connection to the convicted sex offender had become a source of controversy.

Lord Mandelson, a key architect of New Labour under Tony Blair, served in several high-profile ministerial roles. His resignation follows renewed scrutiny of his links to Epstein, who died in prison in 2019 while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges. The Labour Party has not issued an official comment on the resignation.

Key points:
- Lord Peter Mandelson has resigned from the Labour Party.
- He cited his past association with Jeffrey Epstein as the reason, stating he wished to prevent further embarrassment for the party.
- The former minister was a central figure in the New Labour governments of the 1990s and 2000s.
