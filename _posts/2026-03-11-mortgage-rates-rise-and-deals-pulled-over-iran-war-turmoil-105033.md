**Mortgage Market Sees Significant Rate Increases Amid Global Tensions**

Average mortgage rates have reached their highest level since August of last year, according to new market data. This marks the most substantial disruption to the UK mortgage market since the period following the 2022 mini-Budget. The recent surge is attributed to rising global uncertainty, particularly surrounding the conflict between Israel and Iran, which has caused volatility in financial markets.

Lenders have responded by withdrawing a significant number of fixed-rate mortgage deals from the market. This rapid repricing activity reflects concerns over future interest rate trends and increased borrowing costs for banks. The shift is expected to impact prospective homebuyers and those seeking to remortgage, potentially raising monthly payments for new loans.

Key points:
*   Average mortgage rates have climbed to a peak not seen in over eight months.
*   Lenders have pulled numerous deals, citing market volatility linked to Middle East tensions.
*   The upheaval is described as the most severe since the 2022 fiscal event.
