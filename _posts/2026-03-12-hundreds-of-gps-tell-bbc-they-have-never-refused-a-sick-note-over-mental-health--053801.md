A recent survey of general practitioners in the UK indicates that many have not declined requests for medical certificates related to mental health. The findings come amid a broader trend of increasing numbers of such certificates being issued across England. Official figures show that over 11 million fit notes were approved last year, reflecting a significant rise in their use.

The survey, conducted by a major broadcaster, gathered responses from hundreds of family doctors. Many reported that they have never refused to provide a note for a patient citing mental health concerns. This practice highlights the evolving approach within primary care to recognize and validate mental health conditions as legitimate reasons for work absence. The data contributes to ongoing discussions about workplace health, sickness absence, and the role of GPs in supporting patient wellbeing.

Key points:
• A survey found numerous GPs report never having refused a sick note for mental health reasons.
• Over 11 million fit notes were issued in England last year, indicating a rising trend.
• The findings underscore the medical recognition of mental health as a valid cause for work absence.
