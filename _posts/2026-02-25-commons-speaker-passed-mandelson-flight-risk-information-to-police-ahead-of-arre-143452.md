House of Commons Speaker Sir Lindsay Hoyle has informed MPs that he provided information to police regarding Lord Peter Mandelson prior to a recent arrest. Hoyle stated he acted in what he believed was his official duty, passing along details he considered pertinent to law enforcement.

The Speaker addressed the Commons to confirm his actions, emphasizing that his primary motivation was to fulfill his responsibilities. He asserted that he believed the information was relevant and that he acted with proper intentions. The matter relates to a police operation that led to an arrest, though specific details of the information shared have not been publicly disclosed.

This disclosure follows standard protocols for officials who may come into possession of information potentially relevant to police investigations. No further comment on the ongoing legal matter was provided during the statement.

Key points:
- The Commons Speaker confirmed he gave information to police about Lord Mandelson before an arrest.
- Sir Lindsay Hoyle stated he acted in good faith to fulfill his duty.
- The specific content of the information passed to authorities remains undisclosed.
