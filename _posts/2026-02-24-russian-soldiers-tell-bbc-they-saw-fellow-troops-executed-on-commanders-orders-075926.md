**Russian Soldiers Report Executions for Refusing Orders in Ukraine**

Four Russian soldiers have provided accounts to the BBC detailing severe conditions and alleged executions within their own ranks during the war in Ukraine. The soldiers claim that commanders have ordered the killing of personnel who refused to follow military orders.

Two of the men stated they personally witnessed fellow soldiers being shot after declining to participate in combat operations. Their testimonies describe a brutal environment where such disciplinary measures are reportedly used. The BBC report, based on these firsthand accounts, highlights internal tensions and alleged violations of military law within Russian units deployed in Ukraine.

The Russian Ministry of Defense has not publicly commented on these specific allegations. The claims emerge amid ongoing international scrutiny of conduct by all parties in the conflict.

**Key points:**
*   Four Russian soldiers allege executions for disobedience occurred within their units.
*   Two soldiers say they were direct witnesses to these shootings.
*   The allegations have not been addressed by Russian defense officials.
