A government minister has publicly supported the Prime Minister's decision to appoint Lord Peter Mandelson to a strategic advisory role. This defense follows the release of documents indicating that Sir Keir Starmer, prior to the appointment, was advised that Lord Mandelson's past association with the late financier Jeffrey Epstein could present a potential risk to his reputation.

The minister stated that the Prime Minister considered all relevant factors and stands by the choice, emphasizing Lord Mandelson's experience and the value he brings to the position. The advisory role is focused on economic growth and business engagement. The released advice has prompted questions from opposition figures regarding the due diligence process for high-profile appointments.

Key points:
*   A minister defended the PM's choice to appoint Lord Mandelson as an advisor.
*   Released documents show Sir Keir Starmer was previously warned the appointment carried a reputational risk due to Mandelson's link to Jeffrey Epstein.
*   The Prime Minister is reported to have considered the advice before proceeding with the appointment.
