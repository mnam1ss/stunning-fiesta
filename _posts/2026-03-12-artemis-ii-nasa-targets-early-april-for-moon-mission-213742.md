NASA has announced it is targeting early April for the launch of its Artemis II mission, a critical crewed flight around the Moon. The mission, which will carry four astronauts aboard the Orion spacecraft, had faced delays due to technical issues with its Space Launch System (SLS) rocket. Agency officials now state those problems have been resolved following extensive testing and review, clearing the path for final launch preparations. Artemis II will not land on the lunar surface but will perform a flyby, testing systems with a crew ahead of future landing missions. This flight represents the first crewed journey to lunar vicinity since the Apollo program ended over 50 years ago.

Key points:
- NASA aims for an early April launch window for the Artemis II Moon mission.
- Technical issues that delayed the SLS rocket have been resolved after testing.
- The crewed mission will orbit the Moon but not land, serving as a systems test.
