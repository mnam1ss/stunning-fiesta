Ukrainian President Volodymyr Zelensky has criticized some European Union member states over a dispute involving a key oil pipeline. The remarks were made in response to discussions about resuming the flow of Russian oil through the Druzhba pipeline's southern branch, which transits Ukraine.

President Zelensky stated that restoring this supply would effectively undermine international sanctions imposed on Russia. He characterized the pressure from certain EU allies to restart the pipeline as a form of "blackmail," suggesting it prioritizes energy needs over unified political consequences for Russia's actions.

The pipeline has been a significant route for Russian oil into Central Europe. Its operation was disrupted following the conflict's onset, leading to ongoing negotiations among affected nations regarding energy security and sanctions enforcement.

Key points:
*   President Zelensky equated restarting the Russian oil pipeline through Ukraine to lifting sanctions.
*   He accused some EU countries of applying pressure he described as "blackmail."
*   The dispute centers on the Druzhba pipeline, a major former route for Russian oil into parts of the EU.
