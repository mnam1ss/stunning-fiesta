Police Scotland has been issued a significant financial penalty following a data breach involving a complainant in a sexual assault case. The Information Commissioner's Office (ICO), the UK's data protection watchdog, imposed a fine of £66,000 after the force failed to safeguard highly sensitive personal information.

The breach occurred when the mobile phone contents of a woman, who had accused a police officer of rape, were improperly shared with colleagues of the accused. This included sharing the data with the professional standards department investigating the officer. The ICO determined that Police Scotland did not have adequate measures in place to handle such sensitive data, leading to the unauthorized disclosure.

Key points:
*   Police Scotland fined £66,000 by the Information Commissioner's Office for a data protection failure.
*   The breach involved the phone data of a complainant in a sexual assault case being shared internally.
*   The watchdog found the force lacked proper safeguards for handling highly sensitive personal information.
