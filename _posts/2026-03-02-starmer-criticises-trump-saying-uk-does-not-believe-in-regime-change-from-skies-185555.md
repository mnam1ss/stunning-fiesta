UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer has publicly addressed comments made by former US President Donald Trump regarding recent military actions in the Middle East. Speaking in Parliament, Starmer stated that the United Kingdom does not support the concept of imposing political change through aerial bombardment. He confirmed that Trump had communicated his disapproval of the UK's choice to refrain from participating in the opening stages of a specific series of airstrikes. The Prime Minister's remarks underscore a divergence in strategic approach between the two allies on this particular international security matter. The exchange highlights ongoing diplomatic discussions following the military operations.

Key points:
*   Prime Minister Starmer stated the UK's opposition to enforced regime change via airstrikes.
*   He confirmed former President Trump disagreed with the UK's initial non-participation in the military action.
*   The comments reveal a policy difference between the UK and the US on the handling of the situation.
