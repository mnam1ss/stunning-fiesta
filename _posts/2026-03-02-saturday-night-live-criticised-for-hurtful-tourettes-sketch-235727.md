A recent sketch on the television program *Saturday Night Live* has drawn criticism from advocacy groups for its portrayal of Tourette syndrome. The segment, which aired during the show's latest episode, featured a character whose behavior was based on common stereotypes associated with the neurological condition.

In response, the charity Tourette Action issued a statement asserting that the condition is a serious medical matter and should not be used as a source of comedy. The organization expressed concern that such portrayals can be damaging and perpetuate misunderstandings. This controversy emerges amid ongoing discussions about representation and sensitivity in media, following similar debates at recent industry award ceremonies.

Key points:
- A *Saturday Night Live* sketch has been criticized for its depiction of Tourette syndrome.
- The charity Tourette Action stated the condition is serious and such comedy can be harmful.
- The incident adds to broader conversations about responsible media portrayals.
