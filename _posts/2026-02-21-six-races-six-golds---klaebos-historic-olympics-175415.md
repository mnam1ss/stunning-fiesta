Norwegian cross-country skier Johannes Høsflot Klæbo has set a new record for the most gold medals won at a single Winter Olympic Games. The athlete secured his sixth gold medal of the competition in his final event, surpassing the previous benchmark for a single edition of the Winter Olympics.

Klæbo, a dominant figure in the sport, achieved victories across multiple sprint and distance events throughout the Games. His performance cements his status as one of the most successful athletes in Winter Olympics history. The record was previously held by several athletes who had each won five golds at a single Games.

The achievement marks a significant milestone in cross-country skiing and adds to Norway's considerable medal tally at the event. Officials and fellow competitors have acknowledged the historic nature of the accomplishment.

Key points:
- Johannes Høsflot Klæbo won six gold medals at a single Winter Olympics.
- This breaks the previous record for most golds at one Winter Games.
- The achievement occurred across multiple cross-country skiing events.
