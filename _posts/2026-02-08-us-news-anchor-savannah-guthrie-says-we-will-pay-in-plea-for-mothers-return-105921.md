US news anchor Savannah Guthrie has issued a new video statement regarding the disappearance of her 84-year-old mother, Nancy Guthrie. In the appeal, Guthrie directly addressed an unspecified party, stating an acknowledgment of a received communication. She emphasized a commitment to securing her mother's safe return, which authorities are treating as a suspected abduction. The case continues to be under active investigation by law enforcement.

The family's public appeals have drawn significant media attention to the situation. Officials have not released further details about the nature of the communication or the ongoing investigative steps. The plea represents the latest effort by the Guthrie family to seek information that could lead to Nancy Guthrie's whereabouts.

Key points:
*   Savannah Guthrie has made a new video plea concerning her missing mother, Nancy Guthrie, 84.
*   Authorities are investigating the case as a suspected abduction.
*   The news anchor indicated in her statement that a message from an unnamed party had been received.
