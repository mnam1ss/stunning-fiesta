**Proposed Limits on Jury Trials Advance in Parliament**

A government proposal to restrict the right to a jury trial in some criminal cases in England and Wales has passed its initial parliamentary stage. The measure, championed by the Justice Secretary, is presented as a necessary step to address a significant and growing backlog of cases in the crown court system. Officials argue that allowing judges to hear certain cases alone would accelerate proceedings.

The plan faced opposition during the debate, with the Labour Party voting against it. Critics of the legislation have raised concerns about the erosion of a fundamental legal right and the principle of trial by one's peers.

The legislation will now proceed to further scrutiny in the House of Commons.

**Key points:**
*   A bill to limit jury trials for some offenses has passed its first vote in Parliament.
*   The government states the change is needed to reduce a large backlog of crown court cases.
*   The opposition Labour Party voted against the proposal, citing concerns over legal rights.
