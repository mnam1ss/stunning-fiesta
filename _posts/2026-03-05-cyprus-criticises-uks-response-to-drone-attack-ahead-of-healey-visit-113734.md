**Cyprus Voices Concern Over UK Information Sharing Following Drone Incidents**

The government of Cyprus has formally expressed dissatisfaction with the United Kingdom's procedures for sharing intelligence related to recent drone attacks. The diplomatic criticism comes ahead of a scheduled visit to the region by UK Defence Secretary John Healey.

Officials in Nicosia stated that the current level and timeliness of information exchange from British authorities have been inadequate following the aerial incidents. Cyprus, which hosts sovereign UK military bases, emphasized the importance of close coordination on security matters. The specific drone attacks referenced were not detailed in the initial statements.

The UK government has acknowledged the Cypriot concerns but has not provided a detailed public response regarding its intelligence-sharing protocols. The upcoming visit by the British defence chief is now expected to include discussions on enhancing bilateral security cooperation.

**Key points:**
*   Cyprus has raised issues with the UK's handling of intelligence after drone attacks.
*   The remarks precede a planned visit by UK Defence Secretary John Healey.
*   The island nation hosts key British military installations.
