Five Iranian football players have been granted Australian visas after previously facing criticism for not singing their national anthem during a match. The athletes, who were competing in Australia, had declined to participate in the anthem as a form of protest. This action drew significant backlash from some quarters within Iran, with one critic labeling the players as "wartime traitors." The situation had raised concerns about the team's safety and future. Australian authorities have now approved their visa applications, allowing them to remain in the country. The decision follows international attention on the incident and the broader context of protests in Iran.

Key points:
- Five Iranian footballers received Australian visas following an anthem protest.
- They faced severe criticism in Iran for not singing the anthem before a match.
- The visa grants allow the players to stay in Australia after the incident.
