Israeli airstrikes in the Gaza Strip have resulted in the deaths of at least 28 Palestinians, according to local rescue services. The Israeli military has acknowledged carrying out the operations. These strikes occurred amid a tenuous ceasefire, with both Israeli officials and Hamas leadership publicly accusing the other side of violating the agreed-upon pause in hostilities. The recent violence marks a significant escalation following the period of relative calm.

Key points:
- Casualty figures from Gaza report a minimum of 28 fatalities from the Israeli airstrikes.
- The Israeli military has confirmed it conducted the operations.
- The attacks took place during a ceasefire, with both sides trading accusations of violations.
