Global financial markets experienced significant volatility on Monday as geopolitical tensions in the Middle East intensified. The price of Brent crude oil rose sharply, surpassing $110 per barrel, following reports of military escalation involving Iran. Concurrently, major stock indices in Europe and Asia declined.

Analysts indicate the conflict has raised immediate concerns over the security of key shipping routes and potential constraints on oil exports from the region. This development poses a risk of increased energy costs worldwide, which could impact both household expenses and industrial operations. Market participants are assessing the potential for prolonged supply disruptions and broader economic repercussions.

Key points:
• Brent crude oil prices exceeded $110 per barrel amid rising Middle East tensions.
• Global stock markets fell as investors reacted to the increased geopolitical risk.
• The situation threatens to elevate energy costs for consumers and businesses internationally.
