Venezuela's interim president has announced the country will pursue cooperative efforts with the United States on mining sector developments. The statement followed a meeting at the presidential palace in Caracas between the Venezuelan leader and U.S. Interior Secretary Doug Burgum.

The discussions focused on potential frameworks for collaboration in resource extraction and development. Specific details regarding the scope or timeline of the proposed cooperation were not immediately disclosed. The engagement marks a notable diplomatic interaction between the two nations, whose relations have been strained for years.

Key points:
*   Venezuela's interim leader stated the country will work with the U.S. on mining projects.
*   The announcement came after a meeting with U.S. Interior Secretary Doug Burgum in Caracas.
*   The talks represent a significant diplomatic contact amid historically tense relations.
