South East Water is facing a proposed penalty of £22 million from the industry regulator Ofwat following significant service failures. The regulator's investigation concluded that the company did not adequately maintain its water supply network or prepare for periods of high demand, which resulted in widespread disruptions for households and businesses.

According to Ofwat, these operational shortcomings caused considerable inconvenience and distress to customers during supply interruptions. The proposed fine is part of the regulator's ongoing enforcement actions against companies that fail to meet their statutory obligations. South East Water has acknowledged the provisional findings and stated it is reviewing the details. The final decision on the penalty will be made after considering any representations from the company.

Key points:
- Regulator Ofwat has provisionally fined South East Water £22 million for service failures.
- The penalty relates to inadequate network maintenance and preparation for high demand periods.
- The company is reviewing the findings before a final decision is made.
