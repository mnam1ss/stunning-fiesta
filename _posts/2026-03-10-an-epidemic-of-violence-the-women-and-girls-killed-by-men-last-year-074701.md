A new analysis has examined the scale of fatal violence against women and girls in the UK over the past year. The review, which compiled data from public reports and official sources, sought to provide a detailed assessment of these incidents nationwide.

The findings indicate a significant number of women and girls were killed in circumstances involving male perpetrators. The report's authors gathered information by monitoring case reports and verifying details with law enforcement and legal authorities. This approach aimed to build a clearer picture of the patterns and contexts surrounding these deaths.

The analysis contributes to ongoing public and policy discussions regarding violence against women, highlighting the need for continued examination of such cases.

Key points:
*   An annual review has detailed the number of women and girls killed by men in the UK.
*   The analysis was built using media reports and information from police and prosecutors.
*   The findings are part of broader efforts to understand and address violence against women.
