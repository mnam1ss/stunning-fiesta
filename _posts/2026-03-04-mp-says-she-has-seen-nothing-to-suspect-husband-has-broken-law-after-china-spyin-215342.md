A British Member of Parliament has stated she has seen no evidence to suggest her husband has broken any laws, following recent arrests related to alleged espionage activities. The statement comes after London's Metropolitan Police detained three men on suspicion of offenses under the National Security Act. Authorities are investigating whether the individuals provided assistance to Chinese intelligence services operating within the United Kingdom.

The MP, who has not been arrested or accused of any wrongdoing, made her comments regarding her spouse, who is among those detained. The police operation underscores ongoing concerns about foreign state interference. An investigation remains active, with the suspects currently released on bail pending further inquiries.

Key points:
• Three men have been arrested in the UK on suspicion of assisting Chinese intelligence.
• An MP connected to one suspect says she has seen no indication of illegal activity by her husband.
• The investigation is ongoing under the National Security Act, with all suspects bailed.
