Sir Chris Wormald, the head of the UK Civil Service, is to step down from his role. The Cabinet Office announced the departure, stating it was a decision reached by mutual agreement. The announcement comes after a period of sustained public scrutiny, with several media outlets having published critical assessments of his leadership in recent months.

Wormald, who has served as Permanent Secretary for nearly a decade, will leave the post later this year. A process to appoint his successor will begin immediately. The Cabinet Office expressed thanks for his long service across several government departments.

*   Sir Chris Wormald will depart as the head of the UK Civil Service.
