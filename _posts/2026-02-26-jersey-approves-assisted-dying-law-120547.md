Jersey has passed legislation to permit assisted dying, making it the second jurisdiction in the British Isles to fully legalize the practice. The law was approved by the island's parliament following a series of debates and a public consultation process. It establishes a framework allowing terminally ill, mentally competent adults meeting specific criteria to request medical assistance to end their lives. The legislation includes safeguards such as mandatory assessments by multiple doctors and a required period of reflection. As a Crown Dependency, Jersey has its own legislative authority, and this decision does not affect the laws of the United Kingdom or other British Isles territories. The move aligns with ongoing discussions about end-of-life care options in various regions.

Key points:
• Jersey's parliament has approved a law permitting assisted dying for terminally ill adults under strict conditions.
• The island becomes the second place in the British Isles, after the Isle of Man, to fully legalize the practice.
• The law includes multiple medical assessments and reflection periods as procedural safeguards.
