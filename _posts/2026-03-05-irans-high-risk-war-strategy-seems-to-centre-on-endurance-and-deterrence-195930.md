Iran's military strategy in current regional conflicts appears to be based on principles of long-term endurance and deterrence, according to an analysis. Observers note the approach suggests a calculation that Tehran can withstand prolonged military and economic pressure more effectively than its adversaries can sustain the associated costs and political pain. This strategy is seen as a method to project strength and discourage direct confrontation by emphasizing resilience over immediate escalation.

The analysis indicates this posture influences Iran's responses to strikes and its support for allied groups across the Middle East. The focus on absorbing and outlasting adversaries forms a central part of its strategic planning.

Key points:
*   Iran's current strategy emphasizes enduring conflict and deterring opponents.
*   The approach is based on an assessment of comparative resilience to sustained costs.
*   This posture shapes its regional military and proxy engagements.
