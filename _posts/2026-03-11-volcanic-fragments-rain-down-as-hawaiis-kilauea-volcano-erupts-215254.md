**Volcanic Debris Forces Evacuations on Hawaii's Big Island**

Hawaii's Kilauea volcano began a new eruptive phase early Monday, propelling volcanic material into nearby residential areas. The activity, centered at the summit, generated a plume that deposited fragments including fine ash, lightweight pumice, and volcanic glass across communities downwind.

The falling debris, known as tephra, has prompted immediate safety measures. Authorities have closed several major highways as a precaution due to reduced visibility and potential hazards on roadways. Officials have also ordered the evacuation of visitors from the Hawaii Volcanoes National Park and advised residents in affected zones to shelter indoors to avoid respiratory irritation from the ash.

Geologists from the U.S. Geological Survey's Hawaiian Volcano Observatory are monitoring the situation closely, noting that the eruption's intensity can change rapidly.

**Key points:**
*   Kilauea volcano is erupting, sending ash and volcanic fragments over nearby communities.
*   Safety measures include highway closures and tourist evacuations from the national park.
*   Residents have been advised to stay indoors to avoid exposure to airborne ash.
