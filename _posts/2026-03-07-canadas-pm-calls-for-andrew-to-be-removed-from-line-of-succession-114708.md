Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau has publicly stated that Prince Andrew should be removed from the line of succession to the throne. This follows the arrest of the Duke of York last month on allegations related to the abuse of his former official role. The Prince, a member of the British Royal Family, has faced prolonged scrutiny over his associations and conduct.

While the line of succession is a matter for the United Kingdom and the Commonwealth realms, Trudeau's remarks highlight the ongoing international repercussions of the case. British authorities have confirmed an investigation is proceeding, though no formal charges have been announced. The Royal Household has not issued a new statement following the Prime Minister's comments.

Key points:
*   Canada's Prime Minister has called for Prince Andrew's removal from the royal line of succession.
*   The statement follows the Prince's arrest last month on suspicion of misconduct linked to his past public duties.
*   The case continues to be investigated by UK authorities, with broader implications for the monarchy.
