Independent fuel retailer Goran Raven has stated that his business is not taking advantage of current high prices to increase profits. He described the surge in global oil costs as a difficult situation impacting both his operation and his customers.

Raven reported that despite this position, staff at his forecourt have faced verbal abuse from members of the public who are frustrated by rising fuel prices. The retailer emphasized that independent stations operate on narrow margins and are also subject to the same wholesale market pressures.

The comments highlight the challenges facing smaller fuel retailers during periods of price volatility, caught between supplier costs and customer dissatisfaction.

Key points:
• An independent fuel retailer denies making excessive profits from current high prices.
• The business owner says soaring oil costs are challenging for both his station and consumers.
• Staff have reportedly received abuse from customers angered by the cost of fuel.
