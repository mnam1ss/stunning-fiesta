Russian soldiers who have fled their units have provided accounts of severe disciplinary measures within the Russian military in Ukraine. In interviews, these individuals described witnessing the killing of fellow troops who attempted to retreat or refused combat orders. They claim such actions are used to enforce discipline on the front lines.

The soldiers, now in hiding, shared their experiences of the conditions and events they observed while serving. Their testimonies contribute to a broader picture of the pressures within Russian forces engaged in the conflict. These allegations have not been independently verified by other sources.

Key points:
- Fleeing Russian soldiers allege troops are killed for refusing orders or retreating.
- The accounts describe harsh disciplinary measures on the front lines in Ukraine.
- The soldiers' claims have not been independently confirmed.
