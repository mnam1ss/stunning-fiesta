A Royal Air Force base in Cyprus was the target of a suspected drone attack, according to a statement from the UK Ministry of Defence. The incident occurred at RAF Akrotiri, a permanent British military installation located on the southern coast of the island. Officials have confirmed that the event caused no injuries to personnel stationed at the base. An assessment of any potential material damage is currently underway.

The base is a significant strategic asset for UK and allied operations in the Eastern Mediterranean region. While the origin of the suspected drone has not been officially confirmed, the attack follows a period of heightened regional tensions. The Ministry of Defence has stated that the situation is being investigated and that further details will be provided as they become available.

Key points:
- A suspected drone strike targeted the RAF Akrotiri base in southern Cyprus.
- The UK Ministry of Defence confirmed there were no casualties from the incident.
- The origin of the attack and extent of any damage remain under investigation.
