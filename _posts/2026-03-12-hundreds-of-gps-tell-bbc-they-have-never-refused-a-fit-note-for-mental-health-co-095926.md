A recent survey of general practitioners in the UK has found that a significant number report they have never declined a request for a fit note related to mental health. The findings come amid a broader trend of increasing medical certification for work absences. Official data indicates that over 11 million such notes were authorized in England during the last recorded year.

The discussion highlights the complex role GPs play in balancing patient advocacy with workplace requirements. Medical professionals note that the decision to issue a note involves a clinical assessment of how health conditions affect an individual's capacity for work. The rising figures have prompted wider conversations about workplace support systems and the pressures on primary care services.

Key points:
• Hundreds of surveyed GPs state they have not refused a mental health-related fit note.
• More than 11 million fit notes were issued in England last year.
• The increase has fueled debate on workplace health support and GP responsibilities.
