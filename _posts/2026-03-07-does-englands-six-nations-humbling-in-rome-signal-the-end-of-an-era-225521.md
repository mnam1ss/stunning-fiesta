England suffered a narrow defeat to Italy in their final match of the 2024 Six Nations rugby tournament in Rome. The result, which saw Italy win 24-21, has prompted significant discussion regarding the current state of the English team. Analysts are questioning the team's strategic direction and overall performance following a campaign that yielded only two victories.

The loss in Rome has intensified scrutiny on the coaching staff and team management. With the tournament concluded, attention is now turning to the review process for England's campaign and potential changes ahead of future international fixtures.

Key points:
*   England lost 21-24 to Italy in the final round of the Six Nations.
*   The defeat has led to increased examination of the team's leadership and strategy.
*   The team's performance review is expected to be a focal point in the coming weeks.
