**Reality TV Star's Call for Mandatory Ice Skating Gloves Denied**

A proposal from television personality Chloe Lewis to require protective gloves at ice rinks has been formally declined by safety regulators. The initiative followed a serious incident involving her young son, who sustained a partial finger amputation during a skating session.

Lewis, known for appearing on the reality series *The Only Way is Essex*, had advocated for the rule change, citing the injury as a preventable accident. She argued that such a mandate would enhance safety for all skaters, particularly children. Industry representatives and rink operators, however, raised concerns about the practicality and enforcement of a blanket regulation, suggesting that promoting general safety awareness and optional protective gear was a more balanced approach. The relevant oversight body concluded that current safety guidelines were sufficient and did not warrant a new compulsory measure.

Key points:
*   A celebrity's campaign for mandatory ice skating gloves was rejected by officials.
*   The push followed an accident where her son partially lost a finger while skating.
*   Regulators maintained existing safety protocols are adequate without a new rule.
