UK Parliament Votes to Release Documents on Prince Andrew’s Trade Role

Members of Parliament have voted in favor of releasing official files related to Prince Andrew’s former role as a UK trade envoy. The vote follows recent parliamentary comments from a government minister who described the Duke of York’s conduct during that period as “rude and arrogant.”

The former prince served as a special representative for international trade and investment for a decade, from 2001 to 2011. In this unpaid position, he undertook numerous overseas visits aimed at promoting British business interests. The motion, passed by MPs, calls for government departments to publish relevant correspondence and records concerning his work and the termination of his duties.

The government is now expected to review and prepare the documents for publication, a process that will determine the timing and extent of the release.

Key points:
- MPs have supported a motion to release official files concerning Prince Andrew’s past work as a UK trade representative.
- The role, which lasted from 2001 to 2011, involved extensive international travel to promote British trade.
- A minister recently characterized the Duke of York’s behavior during his tenure as “rude and arrogant” in parliamentary remarks.
