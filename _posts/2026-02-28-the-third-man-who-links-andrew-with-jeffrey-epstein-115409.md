A new report has identified a third individual who is said to have facilitated interactions between the Duke of York and the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. According to the BBC, David Stern is described as having served as a significant point of contact between the two men for a period of nearly ten years.

The report states that Stern's role involved arranging meetings and communications. This connection places him alongside two other previously known associates, Ghislaine Maxwell and Epstein’s assistant, who have been central to investigations into Epstein’s network. The Duke of York has previously faced scrutiny over his association with Epstein, who died in jail while awaiting trial on sex trafficking charges. Buckingham Palace has consistently declined to comment on the ongoing reporting.

Key points:
• A new report names David Stern as a key contact between Prince Andrew and Jeffrey Epstein.
• This alleged connection reportedly lasted for approximately a decade.
• The Duke of York’s associations with Epstein have been the subject of previous public and legal scrutiny.
