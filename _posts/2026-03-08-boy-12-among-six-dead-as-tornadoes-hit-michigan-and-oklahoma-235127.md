Severe storms and multiple tornadoes struck parts of Michigan and Oklahoma on Tuesday, resulting in at least six fatalities. The victims include a 12-year-old boy in Michigan, where the most significant damage occurred. The extreme weather caused widespread destruction, including structural damage to homes and commercial buildings, with some structures having their roofs torn off. Numerous trees were pulled from the ground and utility lines were knocked down, leading to power outages for thousands of residents across the affected regions. Emergency crews in both states are conducting search and rescue operations while assessing the full extent of the damage. The National Weather Service has dispatched teams to survey the storm paths and confirm the number and strength of the tornadoes.

Key points:
• At least six people were killed, including a child in Michigan.
• Significant property damage reported, with roofs torn off and trees uprooted.
• Assessment and recovery efforts are ongoing in both states.
