**Proposed Powers Would Grant Officers New Authority in Waste Crime Investigations**

Government officials are considering a significant expansion of enforcement powers to combat illegal waste dumping, commonly known as fly-tipping. Under the proposed measures, officers designated to handle environmental crime would be granted authorities similar to those held by police. This would include the ability to conduct searches of properties without first obtaining a judicial warrant, as well as the power to detain individuals suspected of involvement and confiscate property believed to be connected to offenses.

The initiative aims to address what authorities describe as a growing and costly problem of organized waste crime, which blights communities and burdens local councils with cleanup costs. Proponents argue that stronger investigative tools are necessary to disrupt criminal networks profiting from illegal disposal. The plans are currently under review, with further details on implementation and oversight expected to be released for consultation.

Key points:
*   Officers could gain powers to search properties without a warrant and seize assets.
*   The measures target suspected criminal fly-tipping operations.
*   The proposals are under consideration to strengthen enforcement against illegal waste dumping.
