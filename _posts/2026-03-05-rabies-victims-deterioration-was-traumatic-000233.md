A coroner's inquest into a recent rabies death has heard emotional testimony from the victim's family, who described the rapid and distressing progression of the disease. The family member stated the experience was profoundly difficult to witness and expressed a hope that sharing their story could prevent similar tragedies for others.

The case highlights the extreme dangers of the rabies virus, which is nearly always fatal once symptoms appear. Health officials routinely stress that immediate medical intervention following any potential exposure from an animal bite or scratch is critical for prevention.

**Key points:**
*   Family members of a rabies victim have described the fatal illness's progression as a deeply distressing experience.
*   The testimony was given during an official inquest into the circumstances of the death.
*   The family's stated aim is to raise public awareness to help prevent other families from suffering a similar loss.
