A United States military refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, resulting in the deaths of all personnel on board, according to U.S. Central Command. The incident involved a KC-135 Stratotanker, an aircraft used for aerial refueling operations. Officials stated the crash occurred during a routine mission and that there were no indications of hostile activity involved at this stage of the investigation.

Recovery operations are underway at the crash site. The names of the deceased service members are being withheld pending notification of their families. The cause of the crash remains under active investigation by U.S. military authorities.

Key points:
* A U.S. Air Force KC-135 refueling plane crashed in western Iraq, killing all crew members.
* U.S. Central Command stated there is no current evidence the crash resulted from enemy action.
* An investigation into the cause of the incident is ongoing.
