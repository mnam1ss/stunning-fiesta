The F1 Academy racing series is expanding its efforts to identify and train female drivers for potential careers in Formula 1. The initiative aims to provide a structured development pathway for women in motorsport, addressing a historical gender gap at the sport's highest levels. The program focuses on comprehensive training, including on-track experience and technical education, to prepare participants for professional competition. Organizers state the goal is to cultivate talent capable of progressing to Formula 2 and, ultimately, Formula 1 teams. The series represents a formalized approach within the F1 organization to increase female participation in a field where they have been significantly underrepresented.

Key points:
- The F1 Academy is a dedicated series to develop female racing drivers.
- Its objective is to create a pathway for women to reach Formula 1.
- The program provides specialized training and competitive experience.
