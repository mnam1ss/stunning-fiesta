The first race of Formula 1's new technical era, held at the Australian Grand Prix, has prompted varied reactions from competitors regarding the evolving nature of the sport. Drivers have expressed differing views on the impact of the latest aerodynamic and chassis regulations designed to improve racing. Some participants noted challenges with car behavior under the new rules, raising questions about the balance between driver skill and vehicle engineering. The event has sparked a broader discussion within the paddock about the fundamental character of modern Grand Prix competition.

Key points:
- Drivers reported mixed impressions following the season-opening race in Melbourne.
- New technical regulations for car design were a central focus of post-race discussions.
- Debates emerged concerning the role of advanced engineering versus traditional driving prowess.
