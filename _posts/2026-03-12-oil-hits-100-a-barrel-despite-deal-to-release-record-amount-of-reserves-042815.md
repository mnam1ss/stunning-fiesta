Global oil prices have reached $100 per barrel, a significant threshold not seen in recent months. This increase comes despite a coordinated international agreement to release a record volume of oil from strategic reserves in an effort to stabilize markets and curb rising costs.

The price surge is occurring alongside heightened security concerns in a major global shipping lane. Recent weeks have seen a series of maritime incidents involving Iranian forces targeting commercial vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz. This narrow waterway is a critical chokepoint for the transport of a substantial portion of the world's seaborne oil.

Market analysts suggest the geopolitical tensions are currently outweighing the potential supply impact of the reserve releases, contributing to trader anxiety and upward price pressure.

Key points:
*   Global oil prices have risen to $100 per barrel.
*   The increase persists despite a major release from international strategic reserves.
*   Attacks on ships in the Strait of Hormuz are intensifying security concerns.
