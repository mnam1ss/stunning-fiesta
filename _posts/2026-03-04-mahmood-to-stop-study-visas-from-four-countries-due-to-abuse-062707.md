The government has announced it will suspend the issuance of new study visas for citizens of four specific nations. The policy, which will affect applicants from Afghanistan, Cameroon, Myanmar, and Sudan, is being implemented due to official concerns regarding the misuse of the student visa system.

Authorities stated that the decision follows an internal review which identified patterns of abuse within the application process from these countries. The suspension is intended to address these systemic issues, though officials have not provided specific details on the nature of the alleged abuses. The change is effective immediately for new applications, while the status of existing visa holders from these nations is not expected to be affected.

Key points:
*   New study visas are suspended for applicants from Afghanistan, Cameroon, Myanmar, and Sudan.
*   The government cites concerns over the abuse of the student visa system as the reason.
*   The policy applies to new applications and does not impact current visa holders.
