The UK government has approved a request from London's Metropolitan Police to prohibit a planned Al Quds Day march this weekend. The demonstration, which was scheduled to take place in central London on Sunday, will not be permitted to proceed.

Authorities cited concerns over public order and safety as the primary reasons for the ban. The annual event, which centers on solidarity with Palestinians, has previously drawn counter-protests and led to police investigations into offensive placards and chants. The Met Police stated that the decision was made based on intelligence and a risk assessment indicating a high likelihood of serious disorder.

The organizers of the march have condemned the decision, arguing it infringes on the right to peaceful protest.

Key points:
*   The UK government has banned a planned Al Quds Day march in London following a police request.
*   The Metropolitan Police cited a high risk of public disorder and safety concerns.
*   Event organizers have criticized the move as a restriction on peaceful assembly.
