A chance meeting on the London Underground has resulted in the birth of a child through egg donation. According to a recent report, two women who met by happenstance on the Piccadilly line later pursued in-vitro fertilization (IVF) together.

The donor, who was not previously acquainted with the recipient, agreed to provide her eggs after their initial conversation. The successful treatment led to the recipient giving birth to a baby. The report details the unusual beginning of their journey, which started with a conversation during a routine commute.

The story highlights one of the less conventional pathways to assisted reproduction, outside of formal clinic matching programs or known-donor arrangements.

Key points:
*   A child was born after an egg donation arranged between two strangers.
*   The donor and recipient initially met during a commute on the London Tube.
*   The arrangement proceeded through formal IVF treatment.
