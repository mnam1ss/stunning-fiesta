Two horses died during the final day of the Cheltenham Festival on Friday, adding to the event's equine fatalities for the year. The animals, identified as Envoi Allen and Saint Le Fort, sustained injuries during separate races. Both were subsequently euthanized by veterinary teams on site.

The festival, a major highlight of the British jump racing calendar, concluded its four-day run with these incidents. Organizers and racing authorities have stated that a full review of the circumstances will be conducted, as is standard procedure following such events. The deaths have renewed focus on equine welfare within the sport, which sees stringent safety protocols but continues to face scrutiny.

Key points:
- Two racehorses, Envoi Allen and Saint Le Fort, died from injuries on the last day of the Cheltenham Festival.
- The incidents occurred during separate races, leading to on-site euthanasia by veterinarians.
- Festival organizers have confirmed a review will take place, amid ongoing discussions about horse safety in racing.
