**Trump's Call for Iran Unrest Draws Historical Parallels to Iraq**

President Donald Trump's recent public encouragement of protests in Iran has prompted analysis comparing the situation to the 1991 uprising in Iraq. Observers note that while U.S. support for opposition movements can escalate quickly, the long-term outcomes are often complex and unpredictable.

The comparison stems from events following the Gulf War, where a U.S.-backed revolt against Saddam Hussein was ultimately crushed after initial success, leading to a prolonged and difficult period in U.S.-Iraq relations. Analysts suggest such historical precedents highlight the challenges of managing foreign policy crises once they are set in motion, where initial actions can have unintended and enduring consequences.

Key points:
*   President Trump has publicly expressed support for protesters in Iran.
*   Analysts are drawing comparisons to the failed 1991 Shia uprising in Iraq, which the U.S. initially supported.
*   The historical parallel underscores the potential difficulty in controlling the aftermath of such international interventions.
