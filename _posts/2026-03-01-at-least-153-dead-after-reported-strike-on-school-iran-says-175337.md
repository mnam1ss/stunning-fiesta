At least 153 people were killed in a reported strike on a school in Iran, according to Iranian officials. The incident has drawn international attention and conflicting statements from involved nations.

Iranian authorities have publicly attributed responsibility for the attack to the United States and Israel. In response, a U.S. spokesperson stated that American officials are currently reviewing reports of the event to verify the details. The Israel Defense Forces issued a statement indicating they have no knowledge of any military actions taking place in the specified area at the time.

The situation remains fluid as details continue to emerge from the region regarding the circumstances of the strike and the casualties involved.

Key points:
• Iranian officials report a strike on a school resulted in 153 fatalities.
• Iran has accused the United States and Israel of carrying out the attack.
• The U.S. says it is assessing reports, while the IDF states it is unaware of operations there.
