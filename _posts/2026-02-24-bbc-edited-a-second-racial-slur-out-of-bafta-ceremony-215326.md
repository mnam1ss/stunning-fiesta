BBC Removes Second Racial Slur from Bafta Broadcast Following Error

The BBC has confirmed it edited a second instance of a racial slur from its broadcast of the British Academy Television Awards (Bafta) ceremony. The incident occurred during a recorded segment featuring actor Timothy Spall. According to Kate Phillips, the BBC's Chief Content Officer, the inclusion of the offensive term was the result of a genuine mistake in the editing process. The corporation had previously removed a different slur from the live broadcast of the same event. The BBC stated that the material, which was pre-recorded, should not have aired and apologized for the offense caused. The broadcaster says it is reviewing its procedures to prevent similar errors in the future.

Key points:
• The BBC edited a second racial slur from its Bafta awards broadcast, found in a pre-recorded segment.
• Chief Content Officer Kate Phillips stated the airing was due to a genuine editorial error.
• The broadcaster has apologized and is reviewing its processes following the incidents.
