At least six people have died following a bus fire in western Switzerland on Tuesday. The incident occurred in the canton of Fribourg, according to local police.

Emergency services responded to the scene, where the vehicle was reported to be fully engulfed in flames. Authorities have confirmed that a number of other individuals sustained injuries in the fire. The injured have been transported to area hospitals for treatment, though the severity of their conditions has not been disclosed.

An investigation into the cause of the blaze is now underway. Police officials stated that the origin of the fire is not yet known and that the inquiry remains in its early stages. The bus was reportedly carrying passengers at the time of the incident, but further details about the route or the identities of those involved have not been released.

Key points:
• Six fatalities confirmed after a bus fire in Fribourg, Switzerland.
• Multiple other passengers were injured and taken to hospitals.
• The cause of the fire is currently unknown and under investigation.
