The Metropolitan Police has issued an apology to the Speaker of the House of Commons, Sir Lindsay Hoyle, regarding an incident involving a tip-off about Lord Peter Mandelson. The apology was made public during a session in Parliament.

Addressing MPs, Sir Lindsay stated that his actions were taken with honest intentions, in line with what he believed were his official obligations. He explained he had shared the information because he considered it pertinent to parliamentary business.

The matter relates to the police informing the Speaker about a separate investigation. The specifics of the tip-off concerning Lord Mandelson were not detailed in the parliamentary statement. The Met's apology concludes this particular aspect of the incident.

Key points:
• The Metropolitan Police has apologised to Commons Speaker Sir Lindsay Hoyle.
• Sir Lindsay told MPs he acted in good faith, believing the information was relevant.
• The incident involved a police tip-off concerning Lord Peter Mandelson.
