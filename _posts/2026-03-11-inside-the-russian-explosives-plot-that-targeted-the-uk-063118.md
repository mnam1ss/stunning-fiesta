A man charged with terrorism offenses in the United Kingdom has given an interview detailing an alleged Russian plot to target British infrastructure. Aleksandr Suranovas, who is currently in custody, spoke to the BBC about the accusations against him.

According to the report, Suranovas is alleged to have been involved in plans to use explosives in the UK on behalf of Russian intelligence services. The interview provides his account of the events leading to his arrest and the nature of the alleged operation. British authorities have previously warned of increased hostile activity by state actors against domestic targets.

The case is part of a broader investigation into suspected Russian-backed sabotage activities across Europe. Legal proceedings against Suranovas are ongoing.

Key points:
• A suspect in a terrorism case has described an alleged Russian plot to attack UK sites.
• The individual, Aleksandr Suranovas, is charged with conducting an act of terrorism for Russia.
• The allegations form part of wider European investigations into state-sponsored sabotage.
