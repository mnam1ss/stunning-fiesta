Former actor John Alford has died while in custody at a prison in Norfolk. The 54-year-old was found deceased at HMP Bure. Alford was best known for his roles in the popular British television series *Grange Hill* and *London’s Burning* during the 1980s and 1990s.

Alford's early career as a teenage actor on the long-running school drama *Grange Hill* led to his prominent role as firefighter Billy Ray in the fire service drama *London’s Burning*. His acting career was later overshadowed by legal issues. In 2022, he was convicted and sentenced for serious criminal offenses, which led to his imprisonment.

Prison authorities have confirmed the death and have referred the matter to the coroner. An investigation into the circumstances is now underway, which is standard procedure for all deaths in custody.

Key points:
- John Alford, known for roles in *Grange Hill* and *London’s Burning*, has died in prison at age 54.
- He was found deceased at HMP Bure in Norfolk; the death has been referred to the coroner.
- Alford was serving a sentence following a 2022 conviction for serious criminal offenses.
