A man has been charged in connection with the recent defacement of a statue of Sir Winston Churchill in central London. The incident, which involved spray paint, occurred earlier this week at the monument located on Parliament Square.

Police arrested a 38-year-old individual on Friday. The suspect, identified as Caspar San Giorgio, is currently of no fixed address. He has been formally charged with criminal damage and is scheduled to appear before a magistrates' court.

The statue, a prominent landmark, has been the subject of protests and vandalism in the past, often sparking broader debates about historical figures and public monuments. Authorities have since cleaned the graffiti from the statue.

Key points:
*   A 38-year-old man, Caspar San Giorgio, was arrested Friday and charged with criminal damage.
*   The charge relates to the defacement of the Winston Churchill statue in Parliament Square.
