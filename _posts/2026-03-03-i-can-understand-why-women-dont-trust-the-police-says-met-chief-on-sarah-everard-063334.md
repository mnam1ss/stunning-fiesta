Met Police Commissioner Sir Mark Rowley has acknowledged the ongoing challenge of rebuilding women's trust in the police, marking five years since the murder of Sarah Everard by a serving officer. Speaking on the anniversary, Rowley stated he understands why many women remain distrustful of the force. He emphasized that restoring public confidence is a long-term process requiring sustained effort and tangible change within the service.

The murder of Sarah Everard in March 2021 by Wayne Couzens, then a Metropolitan Police firearms officer, triggered a national outcry and intense scrutiny of police culture and vetting. The case profoundly damaged trust, particularly among women, regarding their safety when interacting with law enforcement. Commissioner Rowley's comments reflect the continued pressure on the force to address systemic issues and demonstrate accountability.

Key points:
*   The Metropolitan Police Commissioner has stated that regaining women's trust will be a lengthy endeavor.
*   His remarks coincide with the fifth anniversary of Sarah Everard's murder by a serving police officer.
*   The case remains a pivotal moment highlighting concerns over police conduct and public safety.
