Oil markets experienced unprecedented volatility on Monday, with prices swinging wildly following a series of geopolitical developments. Analysts suggest recent public statements from former U.S. President Donald Trump may have contributed to a partial easing of an earlier price surge. However, market conditions remain highly unstable as traders assess the ongoing situation.

The trading session is being described by financial observers as one of the most turbulent on record for the global oil market. The initial price spike was driven by significant supply concerns, leading to sharp increases before a moderated pullback. Experts warn that the underlying factors causing the disruption are still in effect, indicating continued uncertainty in the days ahead.

Key points:
* Global oil trading saw extreme price swings, marking a historically volatile session.
* Market analysts note a moderated price pullback following recent political commentary.
* Fundamental supply concerns persist, suggesting ongoing market instability.
