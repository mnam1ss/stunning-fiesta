Two women have spoken about the loss of their newborn sons while receiving care from University Hospitals Sussex NHS Foundation Trust. Sophie and Beth, who met in an antenatal group, each experienced the death of their babies shortly after birth.

An independent review is examining maternity services at the trust, which has faced previous scrutiny. The trust has stated it is fully cooperating with the investigation and expressed its condolences to the families affected.

The cases highlight ongoing national concerns regarding maternity safety and the experiences of bereaved parents within the National Health Service.

Key points:
- Two mothers have shared their experiences of losing their babies under the trust's care.
- An independent review into maternity services at University Hospitals Sussex NHS Trust is underway.
- The trust has offered condolences and says it is cooperating with the investigation.
