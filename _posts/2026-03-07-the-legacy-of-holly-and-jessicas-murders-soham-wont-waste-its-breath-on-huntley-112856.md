**Soham Reflects on Lasting Impact of 2002 Murders**

Two decades after the murders of schoolgirls Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman, the community of Soham, Cambridgeshire, continues to navigate the long-term effects of the crime. The 2002 case, which involved local school caretaker Ian Huntley, remains a defining event for the village.

Residents report that the profound trauma has fostered a lasting sense of caution and a collective focus on supporting one another. Community figures indicate that while the anniversary is marked with quiet remembrance, there is a conscious effort to direct energy toward positive local initiatives and the well-being of current generations. The prevailing sentiment suggests a community determined to define itself by its resilience and unity rather than by the actions of the convicted offender.

Key points:
*   The 2002 murders of Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman continue to shape community life in Soham.
*   Residents emphasize resilience and mutual support, choosing to focus on the present and future.
*   The village's approach centers on remembrance of the victims rather than on the perpetrator.
