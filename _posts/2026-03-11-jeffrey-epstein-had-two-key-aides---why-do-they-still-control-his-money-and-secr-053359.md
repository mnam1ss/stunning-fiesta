Two individuals who served as key aides to the late financier Jeffrey Epstein continue to oversee his financial estate, according to recent court documents. Richard Kahn and Darren Indyke, who held roles as accountants and business managers for Epstein, were named as co-executors of his estate following his death in 2019. Legal filings from a civil case have raised questions about their ongoing control, suggesting they may have had prior knowledge of or involvement in Epstein’s alleged criminal activities. The estate, valued in the hundreds of millions, funds a compensation program for victims. The administrators have denied any wrongdoing through their attorneys. The situation highlights the complex legal and financial aftermath surrounding Epstein’s case.

Key points:
- Richard Kahn and Darren Indyke remain in control of Jeffrey Epstein’s substantial estate as court-appointed executors.
- Allegations in civil court filings suggest the aides may have been aware of or complicit in Epstein’s alleged crimes.
- The estate finances a victim compensation fund, while the administrators deny any legal misconduct.
