A public inquiry has heard that Valdo Calocane, who was convicted of killing three people in Nottingham last year, assaulted two colleagues in separate incidents weeks prior to the fatal attacks. The inquiry is examining the events and contacts with services leading up to the June 2023 killings.

According to evidence presented, Calocane assaulted a male and a female coworker at a warehouse where he was employed. These assaults occurred approximately one month before he fatally stabbed university students Barnaby Webber and Grace O'Malley-Kumar, and school caretaker Ian Coates. Calocane was later sentenced to an indefinite hospital order after admitting to manslaughter on the grounds of diminished responsibility.

Key points:
- The inquiry heard Calocane assaulted two colleagues weeks before the Nottingham killings.
- The fatal attacks in June 2023 claimed the lives of three people.
- Calocane is currently detained under a hospital order after manslaughter pleas.
