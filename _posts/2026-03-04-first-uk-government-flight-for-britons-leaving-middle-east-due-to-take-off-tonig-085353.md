The first UK government-chartered flight for British nationals departing the Middle East is scheduled to depart this evening. The flight will leave from Muscat, the capital of Oman, on Wednesday night. This initiative is part of a broader effort to assist citizens wishing to leave the region amid ongoing regional tensions. Eligible individuals are receiving direct notifications to board the aircraft.

The Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office has organized the flight but has not disclosed the total number of passengers or the specific destination within the UK. Officials have stated that further flights may be arranged depending on demand and the evolving situation. The government is advising other British nationals in the area to register their presence with official channels for potential future assistance.

Key points:
- A UK government flight for departing citizens is set to depart from Muscat, Oman tonight.
- The flight is offered to British nationals amid heightened regional tensions.
- Further flights may be organized based on demand and conditions.
