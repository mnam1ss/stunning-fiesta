**Protests Emerge in Cyprus Following Drone Attack on British Base**

Recent military activity has intensified a long-standing debate in Cyprus regarding the presence of British military installations on the island. The discussion follows a reported drone strike targeting RAF Akrotiri, a sovereign base area operated by the United Kingdom. In response, demonstrators have organized under the slogan "British Bases Out," calling for the removal of UK forces from Cypriot soil.

The bases, retained by Britain after Cyprus gained independence in 1960, are legally separate from the Republic of Cyprus. UK officials have stated the facilities are critical for operations in the Middle East and for humanitarian missions. Protesters, however, argue the installations make Cyprus a target in regional conflicts. The Cypriot government has acknowledged the incident and reiterated its position of non-involvement in military conflicts.

**Key points:**
*   A drone strike on the UK's RAF Akrotiri base has sparked fresh protests in Cyprus.
*   Activists are demanding the removal of British military installations, citing security risks.
*   The UK maintains the bases are sovereign territory and vital for regional operations.
