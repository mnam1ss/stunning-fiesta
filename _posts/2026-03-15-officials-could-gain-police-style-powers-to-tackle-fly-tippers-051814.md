Local authorities in England and Wales may soon be granted enhanced enforcement powers to combat illegal waste dumping, commonly known as fly-tipping. According to a government proposal, certain council officers would be authorized to conduct searches of private property without first obtaining a warrant in specific circumstances. These officers would also have the ability to detain individuals suspected of waste-related offenses and confiscate property, such as vehicles, believed to be involved in criminal activity.

The proposed measures aim to address what officials describe as a significant and costly environmental crime. Proponents argue that current regulations often hinder prompt investigations, allowing offenders to evade consequences. The plan is part of a broader consultation on strengthening enforcement against waste crime, which costs taxpayers millions annually for cleanup.

Key points:
*   Council officers could conduct warrantless property searches under certain conditions.
*   New powers would include the authority to make arrests and seize assets like vehicles.
*   The proposal targets fly-tipping, an illegal waste disposal problem with high cleanup costs.
