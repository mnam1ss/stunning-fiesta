A woman has been sentenced to 13 years in prison for holding a vulnerable person in conditions of modern slavery. Mandy Wixon, 56, was convicted at a court in the UK for offenses including forced labor and false imprisonment.

The court heard that Wixon exploited the victim over a period of time, compelling her to perform domestic chores in a poorly maintained residence. The victim was reported to have endured a life of significant hardship and control under Wixon's authority. The sentencing reflects the severity with which the court viewed the systematic abuse and deprivation of liberty.

Key points:
* Mandy Wixon received a 13-year prison sentence for modern slavery offenses.
* The victim was forced to perform cleaning duties in a squalid home.
* The conviction involved charges of forced labor and false imprisonment.
