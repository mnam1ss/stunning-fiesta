Dentists across the UK have returned approximately £900 million in government funding to the National Health Service (NHS) over a recent period, according to new figures. The funds were allocated to support the provision of NHS dental care but were not fully utilized. The returned amount is equivalent to roughly one-seventh of the total funding provided to dental practices by the NHS.

The data indicates that a significant portion of contracted dental work was not completed, with many practices failing to meet their agreed patient treatment targets. Industry analysis suggests this is partly due to dentists choosing to focus on more lucrative private patient work instead of fulfilling their NHS commitments. This situation has contributed to ongoing difficulties for patients attempting to access NHS dental services.

Key points:
* Dentists in the UK have handed back around £900 million in unspent NHS funding.
* The sum represents about £1 for every £7 of funding they received from the health service.
* The under-spend is linked to practices not meeting patient targets and a shift toward private work.
