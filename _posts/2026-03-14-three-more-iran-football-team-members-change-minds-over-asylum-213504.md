Three additional members of Iran's women's football delegation have reversed their initial decision to seek asylum in Australia, opting instead to return to Iran. This brings the total number of players and staff from the group choosing to go home to four, leaving three whose plans remain unclear. The delegation had been in Australia participating in a recent tournament.

The shift follows a period of deliberation after the team's arrival. Australian authorities have processed the necessary travel documents for those departing. No specific reasons for the change of plans have been officially disclosed by the individuals or the involved football associations.

Key points:
- Four of seven delegation members who initially sought asylum will now return to Iran.
- The reversal reduces the number potentially staying in Australia to three.
- Official reasons for the change in decisions have not been made public.
