A significant fire in central Glasgow has led to the closure of a major railway station, with transport authorities warning of extended travel disruption.

The incident began on Sunday afternoon when a blaze broke out in a commercial premises on Union Street. The situation escalated hours later when the affected building suffered a partial collapse. Due to the proximity of the incident and ongoing safety operations, Glasgow Central station has been closed to all services.

Network Rail Scotland has stated that the station will remain shut while structural engineers assess the safety of adjacent buildings. Rail passengers are advised to seek alternative travel arrangements and expect cancellations and major alterations to services for the foreseeable future. Emergency services remain at the scene.

Key points:
• Glasgow Central station is closed following a nearby building fire and collapse.
• The incident began Sunday on Union Street, causing significant safety concerns.
• Rail passengers face major disruption as safety assessments are conducted.
