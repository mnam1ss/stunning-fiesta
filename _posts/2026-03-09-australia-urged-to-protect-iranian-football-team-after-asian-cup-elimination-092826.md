**Australia Asked to Ensure Safety of Iranian Football Team Following Asian Cup Exit**

Australian authorities are being called upon to provide security assurances for the Iranian national football team, which remains in the country after its elimination from the Asian Cup. The request follows expressions of concern for the players' welfare after they faced criticism from some quarters for their conduct during the tournament.

The team drew attention during the competition for its actions related to the Iranian national anthem. This prompted a strong rebuke from at least one commentator in Iran, who used inflammatory language against the players. The situation has led to appeals from some groups for host nation Australia to formally guarantee the squad's safety before its scheduled departure.

Key points:
*   Australian officials are being urged to offer security protections to the Iranian football team currently in the country.
*   The calls follow criticism directed at the players over their behavior regarding Iran's national anthem during matches.
*   The team's actions led to them being labeled as traitors by one critic in Iran, raising safety concerns.
