A significant change to the United Kingdom's asylum policy will be implemented this week, shifting the nature of protection offered to those recognized as refugees. Starting Monday, individuals who are granted asylum will receive temporary permission to stay, rather than an immediate route to permanent settlement.

This new system means successful applicants will be afforded refugee status for a limited period, after which their need for protection will be reassessed. The policy is part of broader government efforts to manage migration. Officials state the change is intended to ensure that protection is provided for as long as it is needed, while also considering circumstances in a person's home country that may improve over time.

Key points:
* From this week, refugees in the UK will be granted temporary protection status.
* Their cases will be subject to a review after the initial permission period ends.
* The change is a central part of the government's revised asylum strategy.
