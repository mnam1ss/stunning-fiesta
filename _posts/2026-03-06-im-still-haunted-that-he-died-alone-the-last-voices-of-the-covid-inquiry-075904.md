The UK's Covid-19 public inquiry has concluded its three-year series of public hearings, with final testimony provided by representatives of bereaved families. The inquiry, which began in 2022, has heard extensive evidence from scientists, government officials, and health leaders regarding the country's pandemic response.

In the final phase, family members who lost relatives to the virus were given a platform to share personal accounts of their loss and experiences with the official response. Many described the profound and lasting personal impact of the pandemic, including feelings of grief and isolation.

The inquiry chair will now analyze the vast body of evidence collected during the hearings to prepare a final report. This report is expected to contain findings and recommendations intended to inform future pandemic planning.

Key points:
*   The UK's Covid-19 inquiry has finished gathering evidence after three years of public hearings.
*   The final testimonies came from bereaved families, who shared personal accounts of loss.
*   The inquiry chair will now prepare a final report with findings and recommendations.
