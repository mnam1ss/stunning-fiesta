A United States military refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, resulting in the deaths of all four crew members on board, according to U.S. Central Command. The KC-135 Stratotanker went down during a mission, though the specific cause of the incident remains under investigation. Officials have stated there are no initial indications the crash resulted from hostile action.

Search and recovery operations are ongoing at the crash site. The aircraft, which is used for aerial refueling of other military planes, was operating as part of ongoing U.S. military activities in the region. The identities of the deceased service members are being withheld pending notification of their families.

Key points:
- A U.S. Air Force KC-135 refueling plane crashed in western Iraq, killing all four crew members.
- The cause is under investigation, with officials stating no signs of hostile fire.
- Recovery efforts continue at the site of the incident.
