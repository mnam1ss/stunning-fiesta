Chinese Anti-Corruption Campaign Continues After 14 Years

The extensive anti-corruption campaign initiated in China over a decade ago remains an active and prominent feature of the political landscape. The ongoing effort, which has led to the investigation and discipline of numerous officials across party, government, and military ranks, continues to draw analysis regarding its long-term objectives.

Observers note the campaign has consistently targeted high-ranking figures within the People's Liberation Army, leading to commentary about its role in reinforcing command structures and ensuring adherence to central leadership. The sustained nature of these disciplinary actions, years after the campaign's launch, is a focal point for external discussion about its evolving scope and enduring priorities within the Chinese political system.

Key points:
*   A nationwide anti-corruption drive in China, ongoing for 14 years, continues to discipline officials.
*   The campaign has frequently involved purges within the military's senior ranks.
*   Analysts abroad often interpret the prolonged effort as a mechanism for consolidating control and enforcing loyalty.
