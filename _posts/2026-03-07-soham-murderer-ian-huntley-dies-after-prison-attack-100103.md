Ian Huntley, convicted of the 2002 murders of two schoolgirls in Soham, Cambridgeshire, has died following a serious assault in prison. The incident occurred at HMP Frankland, where Huntley was serving a life sentence. He was reportedly attacked by another inmate and subsequently placed on life support before his death was confirmed.

Huntley was convicted in 2003 for the murders of 10-year-olds Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman. The case drew intense national attention and led to significant changes in UK child protection and vetting procedures. He had been held in high-security facilities since his conviction.
