Italian authorities are investigating what they have described as a "serious sabotage" operation targeting the country's railway network. The incidents occurred as the Winter Olympics commenced, causing significant travel disruptions for passengers.

Police reports indicate that three separate acts of sabotage were carried out against rail infrastructure. These actions resulted in widespread delays across the network, though specific details regarding the locations or methods used have not been publicly released. Officials have not yet identified any suspects or determined a motive for the attacks.

The timing of the sabotage, coinciding with the high-profile international sporting event, has raised concerns about security and the potential for further disruptions. Transportation officials are working to restore normal service while the investigation continues.

Key points:
- Italian police are investigating three acts of sabotage on railway infrastructure.
- The incidents caused major travel delays as the Winter Olympics began.
- No suspects or motives have been identified publicly at this time.
