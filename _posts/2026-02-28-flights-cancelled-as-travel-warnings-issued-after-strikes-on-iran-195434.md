Major airlines have adjusted flight schedules and canceled some services following military strikes in Iran, leading to new travel advisories for the region. Several carriers, including British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and Wizz Air, have announced significant changes to their routes, particularly those flying over Iranian airspace. The operational shifts come in response to heightened regional tensions and ongoing security assessments.

Passengers are advised to check directly with their airlines for the latest information on specific flights. Aviation authorities in multiple countries are monitoring the situation closely, with some issuing formal warnings about the risks of operating in the affected airspace. The disruptions are expected to continue in the near term as companies evaluate safety protocols.

Key points:
- Multiple airlines, including British Airways and Virgin Atlantic, have altered flight paths and canceled services.
- Travel warnings have been issued due to security concerns following strikes in Iran.
- Passengers are experiencing disruptions and should confirm their flight status with carriers.
