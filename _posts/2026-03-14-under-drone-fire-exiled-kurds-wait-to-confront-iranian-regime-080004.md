A Kurdish armed group operating from northern Iraq has stated it is prepared to enter Iran, as it reports coming under repeated drone strikes from Iranian forces. The group, comprised of Iranian Kurdish exiles, says it is currently based in Iraq's semi-autonomous Kurdistan region.

Members of the organization have declared their readiness to return to Iran, framing it as a confrontation with the government in Tehran. The Iranian military has not publicly commented on the specific recent drone attacks alleged by the fighters. Tensions along the Iran-Iraq border have persisted for years, with Iranian forces periodically targeting what they describe as separatist militant bases.

**Key points:**
*   An Iranian Kurdish armed group in northern Iraq reports being targeted by Iranian drone strikes.
*   The exiled fighters have announced they are prepared to cross into Iran to confront the government.
*   Iran has historically conducted strikes against Kurdish groups it accuses of operating from Iraqi territory.
