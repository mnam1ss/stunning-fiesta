Former UK cabinet minister Lord Peter Mandelson has been released on bail following his arrest earlier this week. The Metropolitan Police confirmed a 72-year-old man was detained on suspicion of misconduct in a public office. He has since been released under investigation, pending further inquiries by officers.

The arrest is connected to an ongoing police investigation, though specific details of the allegations have not been publicly disclosed. Lord Mandelson, a prominent figure in the Labour Party, served in several high-profile roles under former Prime Ministers Tony Blair and Gordon Brown, including as Business Secretary.

A representative for Lord Mandelson has stated he denies any wrongdoing and is cooperating fully with the authorities. The investigation continues.

Key points:
- Lord Peter Mandelson was arrested and released on bail over misconduct allegations.
- The Metropolitan Police investigation is ongoing, with details of the case not yet public.
- The former minister denies the allegations and is cooperating with police.
