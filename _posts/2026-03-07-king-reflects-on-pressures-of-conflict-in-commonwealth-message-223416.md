King Charles has used his annual Commonwealth Day message to acknowledge the challenges facing the global community, stating that the organization’s role is especially vital during periods of international tension. In his address, the monarch highlighted the shared values and cooperation among the 56 member nations as a source of strength. He emphasized the importance of listening and mutual support in overcoming contemporary global divisions. The message was released ahead of the annual Commonwealth Service at Westminster Abbey, which brings together representatives from across the member states. The Commonwealth, a voluntary association of mostly former British territories, focuses on development, democracy, and peace.

Key points:
*   The King emphasized the Commonwealth's importance during times of international division.
*   His message highlighted cooperation and shared values as central to the organization.
*   The remarks were made ahead of the annual Commonwealth Day observance in London.
