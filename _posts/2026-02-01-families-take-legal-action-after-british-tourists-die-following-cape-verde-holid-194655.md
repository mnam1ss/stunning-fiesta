**Families Pursue Legal Action Following Tourist Deaths in Cape Verde**

The families of six British tourists who died after falling ill during holidays in Cape Verde have initiated legal proceedings, according to a law firm representing them. The firm confirmed it is acting on behalf of the families but did not disclose the specific causes of death or the exact locations of the incidents. The cases involve individuals who traveled to the island nation off the west coast of Africa and subsequently became seriously unwell.

Authorities have not yet released an official statement regarding a potential common cause for the illnesses. The legal action seeks to investigate the circumstances surrounding the deaths. Cape Verde is a popular destination for European tourists, known for its beaches and volcanic landscapes.

Key points:
*   A law firm is representing the families of six deceased British tourists who fell ill in Cape Verde.
*   The specific causes of death and details of the illnesses have not been publicly confirmed.
*   Legal proceedings have been initiated to examine the circumstances of the deaths.
