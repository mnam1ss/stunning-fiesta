Brazilian skier Lucas Pinheiro Braathen has won the men's giant slalom, securing Brazil's first-ever medal at a Winter Olympic Games. The victory marks a historic achievement for a nation with limited winter sports tradition.

Braathen, who has dual Norwegian and Brazilian citizenship, completed two runs on the course with a combined time that placed him ahead of all competitors. The silver and bronze medals were awarded to skiers from Austria and Switzerland, respectively.

The result is considered a significant milestone for the Brazilian Olympic Committee, which has been working to develop athletes for winter competitions. Braathen's performance is expected to draw increased attention to winter sports within Brazil.

Key points:
- Lucas Pinheiro Braathen won gold in the men's giant slalom.
- This is Brazil's first medal in Winter Olympics history.
- The skier holds dual citizenship with Brazil and Norway.
