Young Iranians are adapting their daily lives amid ongoing military strikes, according to recent reports. With an aerial bombardment campaign by the United States and Israel continuing, many residents are reportedly spending most of their time in their homes. Public areas and streets are frequently described as having little activity, as people limit non-essential travel outside. Despite the persistent threat, individuals express a determination to maintain a sense of normalcy and routine where possible. The situation illustrates the civilian experience of conflict, where life continues under significantly altered and dangerous circumstances.

Key points:
• Many Iranians are staying indoors for safety, leading to sparse activity in public spaces.
• A U.S.-Israeli air campaign is cited as the cause of the current security conditions.
• Civilians, including youth, report striving to continue daily life despite the persistent threat of attacks.
