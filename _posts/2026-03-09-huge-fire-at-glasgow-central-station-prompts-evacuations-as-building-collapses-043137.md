**Fire at Glasgow Central Station Leads to Evacuation and Building Collapse**

A significant fire broke out near Glasgow Central Station on Sunday afternoon, leading to the evacuation of the area and the subsequent collapse of a building. The blaze is reported to have originated in a retail unit on Union Street. Emergency services responded to the incident, and firefighting operations continued for several hours before the structure gave way. No injuries have been reported at this time. Authorities have cordoned off the vicinity while investigations into the cause of the fire begin. The incident has caused considerable disruption to travel and local business operations in the city center.

Key points:
- A major fire near Glasgow Central Station on Sunday caused a building to collapse.
- The blaze began in a shop on Union Street, prompting area evacuations.
- No casualties have been reported, and an investigation is underway.
