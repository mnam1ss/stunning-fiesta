Two people have died following an outbreak of meningitis at a university in the Canterbury area, according to reports. Public health officials are responding to the serious incident, which has also resulted in a number of hospitalizations. The BBC has reported that eleven other individuals are currently receiving hospital treatment for serious illness believed to be linked to the outbreak.

Authorities are working to identify the specific strain of the disease and to trace contacts of those affected. University officials and health services are coordinating their response, which includes providing information and support to the student population and wider community. Meningitis is an infection of the protective membranes surrounding the brain and spinal cord and can be life-threatening if not treated promptly.

Key points:
- Two fatalities confirmed in a university meningitis outbreak.
- Eleven additional individuals are seriously ill in hospital.
- Health authorities are conducting contact tracing and providing community support.
