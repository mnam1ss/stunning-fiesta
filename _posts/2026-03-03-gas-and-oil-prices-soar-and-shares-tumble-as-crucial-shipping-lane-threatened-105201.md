Global energy markets experienced significant volatility on Wednesday following a sharp escalation in regional tensions. An Iranian military official issued a warning directed at maritime traffic in the Strait of Hormuz, a critical waterway for global oil shipments. The official stated that vessels passing through the strait could be targeted.

The statement prompted immediate concern over potential disruptions to the supply of crude oil and liquefied natural gas. In response, benchmark prices for both Brent crude and West Texas Intermediate rose sharply in trading. Concurrently, major stock indices in Europe and Asia declined as investors assessed the risk to trade flows through the chokepoint, which handles approximately one-fifth of the world's oil consumption.

Key points:
- A senior Iranian official warned that ships transiting the Strait of Hormuz could be attacked.
- Oil and natural gas prices increased significantly on fears of supply disruption.
- Global stock markets fell in reaction to the heightened geopolitical risk.
