Police have intervened to separate rival football supporters following a pitch invasion at Ibrox Stadium in Glasgow. The incident occurred after Celtic defeated Rangers 4-2 in a penalty shootout to win the Scottish Cup semi-final. Following the final whistle, a significant number of fans from both clubs entered the field of play. The situation escalated as pyrotechnic devices were launched within the stadium. Officers moved to establish a cordon and restore order, preventing further clashes between the groups. The match had been a tense affair, with Celtic equalizing late in normal time before securing the victory. Authorities are now reviewing the incident, which marred the conclusion of a major domestic cup fixture.

Key points:
*   A pitch invasion by both sets of fans occurred after Celtic's penalty shootout victory over Rangers.
*   Police separated the groups as the situation escalated with the use of flares.
*   The disturbance followed a Scottish Cup semi-final at Ibrox Stadium.
