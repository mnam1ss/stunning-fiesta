A recent BBC report explores the experiences of women who express regret over their decision to become mothers. The article details personal accounts of individuals who describe feeling a profound sense of loss for their former lives and identities. Many interviewees report experiencing relentless pressure and a feeling of being confined by their parental responsibilities.

The report highlights the complex emotions surrounding this topic, which is often considered taboo. Women cited in the article describe a significant shift in their personal freedom, career trajectories, and mental well-being after having children. Experts note that discussing such regrets openly remains socially difficult, though acknowledging these feelings can be an important step for maternal mental health.

Key points:
*   Some mothers report mourning their pre-child lives and feeling trapped by parental duties.
*   The social stigma around expressing regret makes it a difficult topic to discuss openly.
*   The article suggests these feelings are more common than publicly acknowledged.
