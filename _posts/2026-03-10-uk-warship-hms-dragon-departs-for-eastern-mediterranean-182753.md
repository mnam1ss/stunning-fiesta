The Royal Navy destroyer HMS Dragon has deployed from Portsmouth to the eastern Mediterranean. The Type 45 air defense destroyer will operate as part of a multinational task force. Its primary mission is to provide air defense for the Royal Air Force base at Akrotiri on Cyprus. The base has previously been targeted by unmanned aerial systems.

The deployment is part of ongoing regional security operations. The warship is equipped with advanced radar and missile systems designed to detect and intercept aerial threats. The crew will conduct patrols and work alongside allied forces during the assignment. No specific timeline for the deployment has been publicly released by the Ministry of Defence.

Key points:
*   HMS Dragon, a Type 45 destroyer, has sailed for the eastern Mediterranean.
*   Its main task is air defense for RAF Akrotiri following previous drone attacks.
*   The vessel will integrate with allied forces as part of a multinational task group.
