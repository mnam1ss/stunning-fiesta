Oil prices have increased in global markets, even after a major international agreement to release a significant volume of strategic petroleum reserves. The price movement comes amid heightened security concerns in a key maritime region. Reports indicate a recent escalation of incidents involving commercial vessels near the Strait of Hormuz, a critical passageway for global oil shipments.

Analysts suggest that market concerns over potential supply disruptions are currently outweighing the anticipated additional supply from the coordinated reserve release. The situation highlights the ongoing sensitivity of energy markets to geopolitical tensions in the Middle East. Market participants are monitoring developments closely for any impact on shipping traffic and oil flow through the vital waterway.

Key points:
*   Oil prices rose following an international pact to tap strategic reserves.
*   Security incidents targeting ships have increased near the Strait of Hormuz.
*   Supply disruption concerns appear to be offsetting the extra reserve supply.
