Speculation is growing in Iran and among international observers regarding the potential succession of the country's supreme leadership. With Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei now 85 years old, attention has turned to his second son, Mojtaba Khamenei, as a likely candidate for the role.

Mojtaba Khamenei, 56, has historically maintained a private profile, avoiding the public political stage occupied by other officials. However, analysts note his deep involvement in the country's religious and security structures behind the scenes. He is reported to have significant influence within the powerful Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps and among conservative clerical circles.

The Assembly of Experts, an elected clerical body, will be responsible for selecting the next supreme leader upon a vacancy. Many analysts anticipate that if chosen, Mojtaba Khamenei would be expected to uphold the conservative ideological and foreign policy direction established during his father's decades-long leadership.

Key points:
*   Mojtaba Khamenei, son of the current Supreme Leader, is seen as a leading candidate to succeed his father.
*   The 56-year-old maintains a low public profile but holds reported influence within Iran's security and clerical establishments.
*   Analysts widely expect a continuation of existing hardline policies if a leadership transition occurs.
