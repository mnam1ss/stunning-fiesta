George Russell secured the top starting position for the Australian Grand Prix after a qualifying session that saw championship leader Max Verstappen crash. The Mercedes driver set the fastest time in Melbourne, leading a one-two result for his team as teammate Lewis Hamilton qualified second. The session was interrupted when Red Bull’s Verstappen lost control and hit the wall at Turn 4, bringing out a red flag. His car sustained significant damage to the right rear, though he was unhurt. Ferrari’s Carlos Sainz will start third on the grid. The incident and Mercedes’ strong performance set the stage for an unpredictable race at Albert Park Circuit.

Key points:
- George Russell will start from pole position for Mercedes, with Lewis Hamilton alongside in second.
- Max Verstappen crashed during qualifying, causing a red flag, but was not injured.
- Carlos Sainz qualified third for Ferrari, completing the top three starters.
