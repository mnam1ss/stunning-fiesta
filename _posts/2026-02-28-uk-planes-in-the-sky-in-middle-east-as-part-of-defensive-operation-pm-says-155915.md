UK Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer has confirmed that British military aircraft are currently operating in Middle Eastern airspace as part of a defensive operation. The announcement follows a government decision to elevate protective measures for UK bases and service personnel to their highest level.

The Prime Minister stated the deployments are a precautionary and defensive move, though he did not specify the exact location of the aircraft or detail the nature of the perceived threat. The increased security posture applies to British military installations and forces in the region.

This development occurs amid ongoing regional tensions. The government's statements emphasize a focus on protection and deterrence rather than offensive action.

Key points:
• UK military planes are active in the Middle East for defensive purposes.
• Security for British bases and personnel has been raised to its maximum level.
• The government describes the moves as precautionary, without detailing specific threats.
