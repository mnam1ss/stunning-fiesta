George Russell secured victory at the Australian Grand Prix, marking the first win of Formula 1's new regulatory era. The Mercedes driver established a lead after an initial competitive phase with Ferrari's Charles Leclerc and maintained a clear advantage for the remainder of the race at Melbourne's Albert Park Circuit. His win represents a significant early result for his team under the revised technical regulations.

The race proceeded without major disruption, allowing teams to gather initial data on car performance under the new conditions. Leclerc finished in second position, with the podium completed by another driver. The event provided the first competitive glimpse into the evolving team hierarchies for the season ahead.

Key points:
*   George Russell won the Australian Grand Prix for Mercedes.
*   The race is the first under Formula 1's latest set of technical regulations.
*   Charles Leclerc finished in second place for Ferrari.
