Iran has launched a series of retaliatory strikes against Israel and several Gulf Arab states following the reported death of Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei. The attacks involved missiles and drones targeting locations that include areas hosting U.S. military personnel. The reported death of the country's highest-ranking official marks a significant and unprecedented development in regional tensions.

The military action comes amid a period of heightened conflict between Iran and Israel. Targets in the Gulf region are reported to include nations that provide basing for American forces. There has been no immediate independent confirmation of the Supreme Leader's status, and details regarding casualties or damage from the strikes are still emerging.

Key points:
- Iran conducted retaliatory strikes using missiles and drones against Israel and multiple Gulf states.
- The attacks follow reports of the death of Iran's Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei.
- Several targeted Gulf countries host U.S. military bases.
