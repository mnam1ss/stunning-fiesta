Team GB has secured its first-ever Winter Olympic gold medal in a snow event, with Charlotte Bankes and Huw Nightingale winning the mixed team snowboard cross competition. The victory marks a historic milestone for British athletes at the Winter Games, where previous gold medals have come in ice-based disciplines.

The pair successfully navigated the head-to-head knockout format, which involves multiple riders racing simultaneously over a course featuring jumps and turns. Their performance culminated in a final run that secured the top position on the podium. This achievement adds to Team GB's medal tally at the current Winter Olympics.

Key points:
- Charlotte Bankes and Huw Nightingale won gold in the snowboard cross mixed team event.
- This is Great Britain's first Winter Olympic gold medal in any snow sport.
- The victory contributes to the nation's overall medal count at the Games.
