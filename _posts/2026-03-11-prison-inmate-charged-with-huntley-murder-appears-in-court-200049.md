**Inmate Charged in Huntley Murder Case Appears in Court**

A prison inmate has appeared in court charged with the murder of Ian Huntley, who was serving a life sentence for the 2002 killings of two 10-year-old girls. The defendant, whose identity has not been formally released in all reports, is accused of attacking Huntley at HMP Frankland in County Durham earlier this year. Huntley, who was convicted in 2003 for the murders of Holly Wells and Jessica Chapman in Soham, Cambridgeshire, died from his injuries. The case was heard at a preliminary hearing, with proceedings focused on administrative matters. The incident is now subject to both a police investigation and an internal prison service inquiry. Further court dates are expected to be set in the coming weeks.

Key points:
*   An inmate has been charged with murdering Ian Huntley, who was serving life for the 2002 Soham murders.
*   The alleged attack occurred earlier this year at HMP Frankland, a high-security prison.
*   The case remains under active investigation by authorities.
