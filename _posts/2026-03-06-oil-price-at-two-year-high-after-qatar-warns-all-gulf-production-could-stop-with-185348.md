Oil prices have reached their highest level in two years following a warning from Qatar's energy minister that a broader regional conflict could severely disrupt production. Minister Saad al-Kaabi stated that if tensions involving Iran continue to escalate, all oil output from the Gulf region could potentially halt within days. He further indicated that such a disruption could drive crude prices as high as $150 per barrel.

The comments have heightened market concerns over the stability of supplies from a critical oil-producing area. The price surge reflects investor anxiety about the potential for a significant supply shock impacting the global economy. Analysts are monitoring the situation closely, as prolonged instability could lead to sustained higher energy costs.

Key points:
• Oil prices have hit a two-year peak following Qatar's warning about potential Gulf production halts.
• The nation's energy minister stated ongoing regional conflict could push prices to $150 a barrel.
• Markets are reacting to the risk of a major supply disruption from a key production region.
