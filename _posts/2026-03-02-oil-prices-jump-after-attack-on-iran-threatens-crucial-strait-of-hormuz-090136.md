Global oil prices rose sharply on Monday following reports of an attack on Iran, raising concerns over the security of the Strait of Hormuz, a critical maritime passage for energy shipments.

The incident has heightened market anxieties about potential disruptions to the flow of crude oil from the Persian Gulf. Analysts note that any sustained instability in the region could lead to further increases in worldwide energy costs. The market reaction reflects the strategic importance of the waterway, through which a significant portion of the world's seaborne oil trade passes.

Officials are monitoring the situation, with the full implications for global supply chains still being assessed. The price increase underscores the market's sensitivity to geopolitical tensions in key oil-producing regions.

Key points:
- Oil prices increased following an attack in Iran near a vital shipping lane.
- The Strait of Hormuz is a major conduit for global crude oil exports.
- Analysts warn extended regional conflict may drive energy prices higher.
