Scientists have released images from a recent deep-sea expedition documenting previously unexplored underwater mountain ranges near Caribbean islands. The survey captured detailed photographs of diverse marine ecosystems, including large formations of coral that exhibit a distinctive coloration. Among the findings are several marine species that had not been formally recorded by researchers before.

The expedition aimed to map the biodiversity and geological features of these remote seabed regions. The collected data is expected to contribute to broader understanding of deep-sea habitats and inform conservation strategies. Researchers noted the importance of such discoveries for assessing the health and composition of oceanic environments far from human activity.

Key points:
- The expedition mapped uncharted underwater mountains in the Caribbean.
- Images show distinctive coral structures and newly documented marine species.
- Data will support scientific understanding and conservation planning for deep-sea ecosystems.
