Former Ukrainian Energy Minister Ihor Nasalyk has been taken into custody by state security services while reportedly attempting to cross the national border. The detention follows his recent dismissal from the cabinet amid allegations of corruption within his former ministry.

Authorities confirmed the arrest but did not specify the exact location or circumstances. Nasalyk is currently involved in ongoing legal proceedings related to the corruption scandal that led to his removal from office. The case is part of broader anti-corruption efforts in Ukraine, which have seen several high-profile investigations into officials in recent years.

The Security Service of Ukraine (SBU) stated the operation was carried out in accordance with a pre-trial investigation, though further details regarding potential charges were not immediately released.

Key points:
• Former Energy Minister Ihor Nasalyk was detained by Ukrainian security services while allegedly trying to leave the country.
• His arrest follows his dismissal and is connected to ongoing corruption proceedings.
• The detention is part of wider anti-corruption measures being pursued by Ukrainian authorities.
