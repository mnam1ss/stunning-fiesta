Canadian curler Marc Kennedy has faced a second accusation of rule-breaking from an opposing team during the ongoing Winter Olympic competition. The allegation, which centers on potential violations of sweeping protocols, follows a similar complaint made against the Canadian athlete in a previous match. Officials are reviewing the incident, though no formal sanctions have been announced. The Canadian team has not issued a detailed public statement regarding the latest claim. Such disputes, while uncommon, highlight the precise nature of curling's rules and the high stakes of international play.

Key points:
- A Canadian curler has been accused of a rules violation for the second time in the tournament.
- The specific allegation involves the sport's sweeping techniques during play.
- Olympic officials are examining the incident, with no disciplinary action yet confirmed.
