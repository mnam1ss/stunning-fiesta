UK Economy Stagnates in January, Missing Growth Forecasts

Official figures released today show the UK economy recorded zero growth in January 2024. This flat performance fell short of analyst expectations, which had predicted a modest expansion for the start of the year. The data from the Office for National Statistics indicates a standstill following a slight contraction in December.

The services sector, a major component of the economy, showed minimal growth, while production output declined. The construction industry also contracted during the month. This latest snapshot suggests the broader economic landscape remains fragile as the country contends with persistent inflation and high interest rates.

Key points:
*   UK economic growth was flat in January, failing to meet analyst predictions.
*   The services sector saw negligible expansion, while production and construction output fell.
*   The data follows a recent technical recession and ongoing cost-of-living pressures.
