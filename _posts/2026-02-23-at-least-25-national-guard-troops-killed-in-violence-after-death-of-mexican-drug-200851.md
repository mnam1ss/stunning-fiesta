At least 25 members of Mexico's National Guard have been killed in widespread violence following the death of a major drug cartel leader. The unrest began after the individual, a prominent figure in a powerful criminal organization, died shortly after being detained by government special forces.

The incident has triggered a surge in attacks across multiple regions. Authorities report that armed groups have targeted security forces and engaged in blockades and arson. The government has deployed additional units in response to the escalating security situation, which represents one of the deadliest single episodes for the National Guard in recent years.

**Key points:**
*   The violence began after a captured high-profile cartel leader subsequently died.
*   Attacks have resulted in significant casualties among National Guard personnel.
*   Security forces have been reinforced in several areas to counter the unrest.
