Glasgow Central Station, the busiest railway hub in Scotland, continues to be closed to all passengers and rail services. The closure follows a significant fire that engulfed a building adjacent to the station. Emergency services were called to the scene, and the blaze has since been extinguished.

The incident has caused major disruption to national and local rail travel. Network Rail and train operators are advising passengers not to attempt to travel to or from the station until further notice. Alternative travel arrangements are being assessed, but significant delays and cancellations across the network are expected to continue throughout the day. An investigation into the cause of the fire is underway.

Key points:
- Glasgow Central Station is closed due to a fire in a neighboring building.
- Major rail disruption is ongoing, with passengers advised to avoid travel to the station.
- An investigation into the cause of the blaze is now in progress.
