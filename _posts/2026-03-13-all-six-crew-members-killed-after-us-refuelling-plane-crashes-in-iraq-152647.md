A United States military refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, resulting in the deaths of all six service members on board, according to U.S. Central Command. The incident occurred during a routine operational mission, though the specific cause of the crash remains under investigation. Officials have stated there is no initial indication of hostile activity in connection with the event. The aircraft, a KC-135 Stratotanker, is used for aerial refueling of other military planes. Recovery operations are underway at the crash site. The names of the deceased are being withheld pending notification of their families.

Key points:
• A U.S. KC-135 Stratotanker crashed during a mission in western Iraq.
• All six crew members aboard the aircraft were killed.
• The cause is under investigation, with no immediate signs of enemy fire.
