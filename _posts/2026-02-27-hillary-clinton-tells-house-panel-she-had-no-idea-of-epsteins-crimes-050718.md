In testimony before a House committee, former Secretary of State Hillary Clinton stated she was unaware of the criminal activities of the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. The closed-door session was part of a congressional investigation into Epstein’s network.

During her remarks, Clinton reportedly suggested that former President Donald Trump should also provide sworn testimony regarding his past connections to the convicted sex offender. This statement shifts focus toward other high-profile individuals who had social ties to Epstein.

The committee is examining potential failures in the justice system related to the Epstein case. Clinton’s appearance marks a notable moment in the inquiry, though no new allegations were made during her testimony.

Key points:
* Hillary Clinton testified to a House panel that she had no knowledge of Jeffrey Epstein's crimes.
* She indicated former President Donald Trump should be questioned under oath about his association with Epstein.
* The hearing is part of an ongoing congressional investigation into Epstein's network.
