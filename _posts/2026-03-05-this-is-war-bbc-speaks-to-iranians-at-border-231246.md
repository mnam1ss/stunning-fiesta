Iranians who have recently crossed the border have described to the BBC the severe conditions they fled, citing ongoing military strikes within the country. These individuals, now outside Iran, provided firsthand accounts of living under persistent aerial attacks, detailing disruptions to daily life and infrastructure.

The report, based on interviews at the border, conveys a sense of acute crisis among those departing. One interviewee characterized the domestic situation with a stark declaration of conflict. The testimonies aim to shed light on the human impact of the internal violence, though the specific locations and military actors involved were not detailed in the initial coverage.

Key points:
• Civilians fleeing Iran report living under constant bombardment.
• Interviews conducted at the border highlight widespread disruption and a humanitarian crisis.
• One source described the internal situation as a state of war.
