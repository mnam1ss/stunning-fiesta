German Paralympic Athletes Stage Silent Podium Protest

At a medal ceremony during the Winter Paralympics, German cross-country skiers conducted a silent protest against the participation of Russian athletes. The German silver and bronze medalists turned their backs while facing away from the podium as the Russian gold medalists received their awards. This action was a direct response to the International Paralympic Committee's decision to allow Russian athletes to compete under a neutral banner despite international condemnation of Russia's military actions in Ukraine.

The protest occurred during the men's cross-country skiing event. The IPC has stated that Russian and Belarusian athletes are competing as neutrals, without national flags or anthems. Several other nations had withdrawn from events against Russian athletes earlier in the Games, but this marked a prominent on-podium demonstration.

Key points:
• German Paralympians turned away from Russian gold medalists during a Winter Games ceremony.
• The protest was against the inclusion of Russian athletes at the event.
• Russian competitors are participating as neutrals under IPC rules.
