British forces stationed in Iraq successfully intercepted two unmanned aerial vehicles overnight, according to a statement from the UK's Defence Secretary. John Healey confirmed the defensive action took place at a military base hosting international coalition personnel. Officials have attributed the drones to Iranian origins, though the specific launch location and intended target of the aircraft have not been publicly detailed. The incident underscores the ongoing regional tensions and the persistent threat of aerial attacks against coalition sites in Iraq and Syria. There have been no reports of injuries or significant damage resulting from the engagement. The UK Ministry of Defence continues to monitor the situation in coordination with allied forces.

Key points:
- UK defence forces intercepted two Iranian-origin drones at a coalition base in Iraq.
- Defence Secretary John Healey confirmed the incident, reporting no casualties.
- The engagement highlights continued security threats to international troops in the region.
