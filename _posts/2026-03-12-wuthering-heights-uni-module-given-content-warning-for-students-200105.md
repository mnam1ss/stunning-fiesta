A university literature course in Wales has added a content advisory to its syllabus for a classic 19th-century novel. The module at Cardiff University, which includes Emily Brontë's *Wuthering Heights*, now informs students that the course material may contain themes some could find distressing.

The warning is intended to alert students to the novel's depictions of intense emotional turmoil, domestic conflict, and cruelty. University representatives state such notices are part of a broader framework to support student well-being by allowing for preparatory reading and informed participation. The practice of applying content guidance to established literary works has become more common in higher education institutions internationally.

Key points:
*   A Cardiff University module studying *Wuthering Heights* now carries a content warning.
*   The advisory cites potentially distressing themes within the classic novel.
*   The university states the measure is for student well-being and preparedness.
