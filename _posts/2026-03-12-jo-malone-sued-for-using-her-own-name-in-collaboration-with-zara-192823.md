Perfumer Jo Malone is facing legal action from the Estée Lauder Companies over her use of her own name in a recent collaboration with fashion retailer Zara. The dispute centers on the "Jo Loves" brand, which Malone founded after leaving the original company that bears her name.

Estée Lauder, which acquired the Jo Malone London brand in 1999, claims the Zara collaboration constitutes trademark infringement and breaches the terms of their past agreements. Malone, who has publicly expressed regret over selling the rights to her name, launched the "Jo Loves" line in 2011 to create new fragrances.

The legal proceedings highlight ongoing tensions between founders and corporate owners over brand identity and naming rights following the sale of a business.

Key points:
- Estée Lauder Companies is suing perfumer Jo Malone over her collaboration with Zara.
- The dispute involves trademark rights sold by Malone to Estée Lauder in 1999.
- Malone now operates a separate fragrance brand named "Jo Loves."
