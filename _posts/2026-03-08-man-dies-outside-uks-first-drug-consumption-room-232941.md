**Man Dies Outside Glasgow Drug Consumption Facility**

A man has died outside The Thistle, a health center in Glasgow that houses the United Kingdom's first official drug consumption room. Police were called to the Hunter Street location on Sunday morning, where they established a cordon around the entrance. Authorities have stated the death is not currently being treated as suspicious. The facility, which opened last year, allows individuals to use pre-obtained drugs under medical supervision, aiming to reduce public drug use and prevent overdose deaths. An investigation into the circumstances is ongoing, with a report to be submitted to the Procurator Fiscal, Scotland's prosecution service.

Key points:
- A man died outside the UK's first sanctioned drug consumption room in Glasgow.
- Police do not believe the death to be suspicious at this time.
- The facility is designed to provide a supervised environment for drug use to enhance safety.
