Former President Bill Clinton testified before a congressional committee this week, where he was questioned about his past association with the late financier Jeffrey Epstein. During the session, Clinton was presented with a photograph of himself and others near a hot tub at Epstein's residence. In his testimony, Clinton stated he had no knowledge of Epstein's criminal activities at the time of their association. He emphasized that he would not have traveled on Epstein's private aircraft had he been aware of the financier's actions.

The questioning is part of a broader investigation into the handling of the Epstein case. The former president's testimony sought to clarify the nature and extent of his contacts with the convicted sex offender, who died by suicide in 2019 while awaiting trial on federal charges.

Key points:
- Bill Clinton testified he was unaware of Jeffrey Epstein's crimes during their past association.
- He stated he would not have used Epstein's plane if he had known of the illegal activities.
- The testimony occurred as part of a congressional committee's ongoing investigation.
