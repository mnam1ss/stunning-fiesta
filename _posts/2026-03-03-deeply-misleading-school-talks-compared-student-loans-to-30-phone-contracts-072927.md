Former school presentations promoting student finance in England have been criticized for downplaying the nature of the loans, according to recent reports. The talks, delivered roughly a decade ago, allegedly instructed recent graduates hired as presenters to avoid terms like "debt." Instead, the financial commitment was reportedly compared to a monthly phone contract costing around £30. Critics have described the approach as minimizing the long-term financial obligation involved.

The information comes from documents and former staff involved in the outreach program. The presentations were part of a government-backed initiative to encourage university enrollment. Officials have stated that current guidance for such talks is clear and transparent, focusing on ensuring students understand the repayment terms linked to their future income.

Key points:
• School talks a decade ago reportedly avoided the word "debt" when discussing student loans.
• The financial commitment was sometimes compared to a small monthly subscription.
• Current government guidance emphasizes transparency in explaining the income-linked repayment system.
