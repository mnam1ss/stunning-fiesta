A significant number of British horse racing fans have chosen to travel to Spain's Costa Blanca region to watch the Cheltenham Festival, a major event in the National Hunt racing season. The festival, held annually in Gloucestershire, England, is traditionally attended in person by large crowds. This year, however, many spectators have opted to follow the races from abroad, combining the sporting occasion with a holiday in a warmer climate. The phenomenon has been noted by local businesses in the Spanish coastal area, which report an influx of visitors from the UK during the festival period. The trend highlights the event's international appeal and the mobility of its fanbase.

Key points:
- British racing enthusiasts are watching the Cheltenham Festival from the Costa Blanca in Spain.
- The move combines attendance at the premier jump-racing event with a holiday abroad.
- Local Spanish businesses are noting increased visitor numbers from the UK during this period.
