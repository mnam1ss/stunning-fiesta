Israeli airstrikes in the Gaza Strip have resulted in the deaths of at least 32 Palestinians, according to local rescue services. The Israeli military has acknowledged carrying out the operations. These strikes occurred amid a ceasefire period, with both Israel and Hamas trading accusations of violating the agreed-upon pause in hostilities. The recent violence marks a significant escalation in a conflict that has seen repeated cycles of fighting and truce efforts over recent months.

Key points:
- At least 32 people were reported killed in Israeli airstrikes in Gaza.
- The military actions took place during a ceasefire, which both sides accuse the other of breaking.
- The incident represents a serious breach in the temporary truce and has heightened regional tensions.
