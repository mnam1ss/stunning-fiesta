A London boutique owner is pursuing a substantial legal claim after a dog-sitting arrangement led to a complex financial dispute. Bridget Hutchcroft alleges that a woman she befriended and later allowed to stay in her home incurred debts exceeding £1.6 million in her name. The situation reportedly began when Hutchcroft agreed to care for the woman's dog, which later evolved into the acquaintance moving into her property.

According to court documents, the defendant is accused of using Hutchcroft's personal details to secure significant loans and make luxury purchases without authorization. The High Court has now issued a worldwide freezing order against the defendant's assets. The defendant has not yet filed a formal response to the claims in this ongoing case.

Key points:
*   A boutique owner claims over £1.6 million was borrowed and spent in her name by an acquaintance.
*   The dispute originated from a dog-sitting favor that led to the acquaintance residing in the owner's home.
*   A High Court judge has imposed a freezing order on the defendant's assets worldwide while the case proceeds.
