Balendra Shah, a popular rapper and independent politician, is poised to become Nepal's next prime minister following the country's recent general election. The election, held on Thursday, marks a significant political shift, being the first national vote since widespread youth-led demonstrations resulted in the collapse of the previous government in September.

Shah, who currently serves as the mayor of Kathmandu, has secured the support of a coalition of political parties, positioning him to lead the new administration. His rise from a musical career to the highest levels of government reflects a broader trend of political change in the country. The final parliamentary procedures to confirm his appointment are expected to proceed in the coming days.

Key points:
- The election was the first since protests toppled the prior administration.
- Balendra Shah is an independent politician and former rapper.
- A coalition agreement has paved his way to the premiership.
