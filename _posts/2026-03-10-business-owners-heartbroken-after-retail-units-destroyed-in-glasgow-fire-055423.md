A fire in Glasgow has severely damaged a commercial building housing multiple independent businesses, leaving owners displaced and describing themselves as devastated. The blaze occurred at Union Corner, a property adjacent to Glasgow Central Station, and resulted in a partial structural collapse. Emergency services responded to the incident, which has rendered the units unusable.

The affected retailers now face significant uncertainty, having lost their primary workspace. Local authorities and fire investigators are examining the site to determine the cause of the fire and the full extent of the damage. The incident has impacted a cluster of small enterprises that operated from the location.

Key points:
• A fire caused a partial building collapse at the Union Corner property near Glasgow Central Station.
• Several independent businesses based in the building have been displaced and their units destroyed.
• An investigation into the cause and full impact of the fire is currently underway.
