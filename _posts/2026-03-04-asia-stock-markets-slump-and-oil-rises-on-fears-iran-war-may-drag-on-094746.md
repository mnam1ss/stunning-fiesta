Asian stock markets fell sharply on Monday, while oil prices climbed, as investor concerns grew that the conflict between Israel and Iran could escalate into a prolonged regional war. The heightened geopolitical risk prompted a shift away from regional equities toward perceived safe-haven assets.

In contrast, major European markets, including those in the United Kingdom, opened with modest gains. Analysts noted that trading remained cautious and sentiment fragile, with energy markets experiencing significant price swings throughout the session. The overall financial landscape reflected a split between regions more directly exposed to Middle Eastern tensions and those currently more insulated.

Market participants are closely monitoring official statements from involved governments for any signs of de-escalation or further military action, which are expected to drive near-term volatility.

Key points:
- Asian equities declined amid fears the Iran-Israel conflict may become extended.
- Crude oil prices increased due to supply disruption concerns in the Middle East.
- European markets opened slightly higher, though energy prices stayed volatile.
