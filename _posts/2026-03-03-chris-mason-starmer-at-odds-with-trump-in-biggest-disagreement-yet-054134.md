Prime Minister Keir Starmer has publicly articulated a significant foreign policy divergence from former US President Donald Trump, marking one of the most pronounced disagreements between the two leaders. The statement was made during a parliamentary session where Starmer addressed the UK's stance on international military interventions.

In his remarks to MPs, the Prime Minister emphasized that his government's approach does not support the concept of imposing political change in other nations through aerial bombardment. This position directly contrasts with past statements and policies associated with the Trump administration, which had shown a more assertive posture regarding the use of air power in foreign conflicts. The declaration is seen as a clear effort to define the new UK government's independent strategic principles on the global stage.

Key points:
*   Prime Minister Keir Starmer has outlined a major policy difference with former US President Donald Trump.
*   Starmer stated the UK government opposes forcing regime change via aerial military campaigns.
*   The comments signal a deliberate shift in the new administration's foreign policy tone.
