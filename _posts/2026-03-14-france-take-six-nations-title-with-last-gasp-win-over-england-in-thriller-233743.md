France secured the Six Nations championship on Saturday with a dramatic victory over England in Paris. The match concluded with a final-minute penalty kick by French full-back Thomas Ramos, breaking a tie and delivering the win. The high-scoring contest saw both teams exchange leads multiple times before the decisive play in the closing seconds.

This marks France's third Six Nations title in the last five years. The team finished the tournament with four wins and one loss. The intense final round of matches determined the championship outcome, with this result in Paris proving decisive.

Key points:
* France won the Six Nations title with a last-minute penalty kick against England.
* This victory represents France's third championship in the past five tournaments.
* The decisive match was a high-scoring affair settled in the final moments in Paris.
