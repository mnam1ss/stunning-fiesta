Global stock indices declined sharply on Monday as a significant rise in oil prices fueled investor concerns over potential supply disruptions. The price surge follows heightened geopolitical tensions surrounding the Strait of Hormuz, a critical maritime passage for a substantial portion of the world's seaborne oil shipments. Analysts indicate that any major interruption to the flow of energy from the region could have widespread economic consequences.

The increased cost of crude oil threatens to raise expenses for both industries and households internationally. Higher fuel and transportation costs could contribute to inflationary pressures, impacting economic growth forecasts. Market volatility is expected to continue as the situation develops.

Key points:
*   Global equities fell amid a sharp increase in crude oil prices.
*   The price surge is linked to security concerns around the key Strait of Hormuz shipping lane.
*   Sustained higher energy costs could elevate global inflation and slow economic activity.
