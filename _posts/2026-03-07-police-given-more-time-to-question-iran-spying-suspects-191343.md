British authorities have been granted an extension to continue questioning four individuals arrested earlier this week on national security grounds. The suspects, identified as one Iranian national and three people holding dual British-Iranian citizenship, were detained by officers from the Metropolitan Police's Counter Terrorism Command. They are being held on suspicion of offenses related to assisting a foreign state's intelligence-gathering activities.

The police requested and received a judicial warrant to extend the detention period, allowing for further investigation before a decision on potential charges is made. Officials have not publicly named the foreign intelligence service allegedly involved, nor have they specified the nature of the suspected activities. The investigation remains ongoing, with searches at several residential properties also reported as part of the operation.

Key points:
- Four individuals, including three dual nationals, are detained on national security suspicions.
- Police have secured more time to question the suspects before deciding on charges.
- The investigation involves allegations of assisting a foreign intelligence service.
