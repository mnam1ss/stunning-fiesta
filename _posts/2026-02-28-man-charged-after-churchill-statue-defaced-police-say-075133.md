**Man Faces Charges Over Churchill Statue Vandalism**

A 38-year-old man has been charged in connection with the defacement of a statue of Sir Winston Churchill in central London, according to police. The individual, identified as Caspar San Giorgio, was taken into custody last Friday. Authorities state he is currently of no fixed address.

The incident involved damage to the memorial of the former prime minister, which stands in Parliament Square. The statue has been the focus of protests and acts of vandalism in recent years. Police have not released specific details regarding the nature of the recent damage or the materials used.

The case is now proceeding through the judicial system following the formal charges. The arrest underscores ongoing tensions surrounding historical monuments in public spaces.

Key points:
*   A 38-year-old man was arrested last Friday and charged with vandalizing the Winston Churchill statue.
*   The accused individual, Caspar San Giorgio, is listed as having no fixed address.
