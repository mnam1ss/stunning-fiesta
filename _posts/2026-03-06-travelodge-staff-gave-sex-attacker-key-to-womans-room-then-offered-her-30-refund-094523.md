A woman who was sexually assaulted in a Travelodge hotel room has criticized the company's response after the incident. The assault occurred after hotel staff reportedly provided a key to her room to another guest, Kyran Smith. Following the attack, the woman stated that Travelodge offered her a £30 refund, which she described as deeply inadequate.

Smith has since been convicted and sentenced for the assault. The hotel chain has acknowledged the incident and stated that it has reviewed its procedures to prevent similar occurrences in the future. The case has raised questions about hotel security protocols and corporate responses to serious crimes committed on their premises.

Key points:
- A guest was assaulted after staff gave another guest a key to her room.
- The victim called a subsequent £30 refund offer from the hotel "insulting."
- The perpetrator, Kyran Smith, has been convicted and sentenced for the attack.
