The Royal Navy is preparing the aircraft carrier HMS Prince of Wales for a potential rapid deployment, according to defense sources. The vessel's crew has reportedly been instructed to be ready to set sail within a five-day period. This accelerated readiness posture comes amid heightened regional tensions involving Iran.

The move is seen as a precautionary measure to ensure a swift response capability should the security situation deteriorate further. The HMS Prince of Wales is one of the UK's two operational aircraft carriers and serves as a central component of the nation's naval strike capability. Officials have not publicly confirmed a specific deployment order or destination, but the preparations underscore the military's alert status in the current geopolitical climate.

Key points:
- The crew of the HMS Prince of Wales has been directed to achieve readiness for deployment within five days.
- This preparation is linked to ongoing tensions with Iran in the Middle East region.
- The deployment order remains precautionary, with no final sailing command yet issued.
