**Therapy Dog Introduced at Lincolnshire School to Support Student Well-being**

A secondary school in Lincolnshire has introduced a canine companion to its pastoral care team. The dog, a puppy named Florrie, is being integrated into the school environment to provide emotional and mental health support to students.

School officials report that Florrie’s presence is intended to offer a calming influence and help pupils manage stress and anxiety. The initiative is part of a broader strategy to address youth mental health by providing non-judgmental, comforting interaction. The puppy will be gradually acclimated to the school setting under the supervision of trained staff.

Similar programs in other educational institutions have cited benefits including improved student engagement and a more positive school atmosphere.

Key points:
*   A puppy named Florrie is being used to support student mental health at a Lincolnshire secondary school.
*   The dog's role is to provide a calming presence and assist pupils with stress management.
*   The program aligns with wider efforts to bolster emotional well-being resources in educational settings.
