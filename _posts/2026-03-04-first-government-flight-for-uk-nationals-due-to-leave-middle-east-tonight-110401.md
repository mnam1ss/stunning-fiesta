The first UK government-chartered flight for British nationals in the Middle East is scheduled to depart Wednesday evening. The aircraft is set to leave from Muscat, the capital of Oman.

Officials have begun contacting eligible individuals to offer seats on the flight. The operation is part of efforts to assist citizens seeking to leave the region amid ongoing regional tensions. Further details regarding passenger eligibility and the flight's destination have not been publicly disclosed.

The Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office has advised British nationals in the area to register their presence with the authorities and follow its travel guidance.

Key points:
*   A charter flight for UK citizens is planned to depart from Muscat, Oman on Wednesday night.
*   Eligible individuals are being contacted directly to board the government-organized flight.
*   The flight forms part of broader consular assistance efforts during the current regional situation.
