Great Britain has secured its first medal at the 2026 Milan-Cortina Winter Paralympics. The medal was earned by visually impaired alpine skier Neil Simpson, who was guided by Rob Poth, in the men's alpine combined event. The pair achieved a second-place finish, claiming the silver medal.

The alpine combined event tests skiers across two different disciplines: one downhill run and one slalom run. Their combined time across these races determines the final standings. Simpson and Poth's performance delivered Great Britain's inaugural podium finish of these Games.

Key points:
*   Neil Simpson, guided by Rob Poth, won silver in the men's alpine combined.
*   This is Great Britain's first medal of the 2026 Milan-Cortina Winter Paralympics.
*   The alpine combined event consists of a downhill run and a slalom run.
