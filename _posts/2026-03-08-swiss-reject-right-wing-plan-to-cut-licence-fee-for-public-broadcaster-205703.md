Swiss voters have decisively rejected a proposal to reduce the mandatory licence fee that funds the country's public broadcaster, SRG SSR. Initial projections from the national referendum indicate that over 60 percent of participants voted against the measure, which was championed by right-wing political groups. The proposal sought to cut the annual fee from its current level of 335 Swiss francs to 200 francs.

Supporters of the fee reduction argued it would ease the financial burden on households and increase media competition. Opponents, including the government and parliament, warned that such a cut would significantly weaken public service programming and news coverage across Switzerland's multiple linguistic regions. The broadcaster stated the result ensures it can continue its mandate to provide independent information and cultural content.

Key points:
*   Swiss voters rejected a proposal to lower the public broadcasting licence fee by a margin of over 60%.
*   The initiative was backed by right-wing parties aiming to reduce the annual household charge.
*   Authorities had warned the cut would threaten the broadcaster's ability to operate across all language regions.
