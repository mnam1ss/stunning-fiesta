Former President Donald Trump has stated that U.S. military operations against Iran would not require the support of British aircraft carriers. The comment was made following a public exchange with UK Prime Minister Sir Keir Starmer regarding potential British involvement in a joint U.S. and Israeli offensive.

The discussion centers on the future of the UK's role in allied military actions in the Middle East. Mr. Starmer has recently emphasized a more independent foreign policy stance for Britain. Mr. Trump's assertion suggests a view that American capabilities are sufficient for such a conflict without key allied naval assets.

Key points:
*   Donald Trump stated UK carrier support would not be necessary for a U.S.-led conflict with Iran.
*   The remark follows a public discussion with UK Prime Minister Keir Starmer on allied military cooperation.
*   The exchange highlights ongoing dialogue about the UK's future role in joint military actions with the United States.
