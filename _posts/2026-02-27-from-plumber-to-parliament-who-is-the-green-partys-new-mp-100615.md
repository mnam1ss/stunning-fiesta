Spencer, a former plumber, has been elected as the Green Party's newest Member of Parliament. She secured her seat in the recent by-election, marking a significant gain for her party. In her initial remarks following the win, she directly addressed her previous clients, expressing regret that her new parliamentary duties in Westminster might lead to cancellations of scheduled plumbing work.

The transition from a trade profession to a national political role has drawn public attention to her background and campaign. Party officials have highlighted her election as evidence of growing support for their policies. Spencer is expected to take her seat in the House of Commons when Parliament next convenes.

Key points:
• Spencer, a plumber, has been elected as a Green Party MP.
• She apologized to former clients for potential work cancellations due to her new role.
• Her victory represents a notable parliamentary gain for the Green Party.
