Two unmanned aerial vehicles were intercepted while approaching a Royal Air Force installation in Cyprus, according to a statement from Cypriot authorities. The incident occurred at the RAF Akrotiri base, a strategic site on the island. The UK Ministry of Defence confirmed the event, stating that the attempted approach resulted in only minor physical damage to the base's infrastructure. Officials reported that no injuries were sustained by personnel stationed at the facility. The origin and intended target of the drones have not been publicly disclosed, and an investigation into the matter is ongoing. The base, which is used for operations in the Middle East, remains fully operational following the incident.

Key points:
• Two drones were intercepted near RAF Akrotiri in Cyprus.
• The UK Ministry of Defence reported minimal damage and no injuries.
• The origin and purpose of the drones are under investigation.
