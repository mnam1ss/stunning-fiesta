Ireland secured a crucial victory over Scotland in Dublin on Saturday, maintaining their contention for the Six Nations championship. The match, which also decided the Triple Crown, was a closely contested affair with Ireland ultimately prevailing.

The win ensures Ireland remains in second place in the tournament standings, one point behind England. The final round of matches next week will now determine the overall champion. Scotland, despite a competitive performance, was unable to secure the win needed to keep its own title ambitions alive.

The result sets up a decisive final weekend where Ireland will need a favorable outcome in other matches to claim the championship.

Key points:
*   Ireland defeated Scotland in a tight Six Nations match in Dublin.
*   The victory keeps Ireland's championship hopes active heading into the final round.
*   Scotland's loss eliminates them from title contention.
