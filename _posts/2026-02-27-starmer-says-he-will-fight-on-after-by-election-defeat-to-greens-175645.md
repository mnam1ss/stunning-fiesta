Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer has stated his intention to continue leading the party following a significant by-election loss to the Green Party. The defeat occurred in a parliamentary constituency historically held by Labour for almost a century.

The outcome has prompted renewed scrutiny of Sir Keir's leadership from within his own party. Some Labour Members of Parliament have voiced criticism in the wake of the result, viewing the loss as a setback.

Sir Keir acknowledged the disappointing result but emphasized his commitment to focusing on the national political landscape and the upcoming general election.

Key points:
*   Labour lost a long-held parliamentary seat to the Green Party in a by-election.
*   The loss has led to renewed internal criticism of Labour leader Sir Keir Starmer.
*   Sir Keir has stated he will continue as leader and focus on the next general election.
