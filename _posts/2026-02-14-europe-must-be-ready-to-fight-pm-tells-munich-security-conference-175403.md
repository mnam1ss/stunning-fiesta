At the Munich Security Conference on Saturday, the Prime Minister delivered a speech urging European nations to bolster their military preparedness. He stated that the continent must be ready to defend itself in an increasingly unstable geopolitical climate. The address emphasized the need for increased defense spending and stronger collective security measures among European allies.

This international appearance follows a period of significant domestic political challenges for the leader over the past week, which have drawn considerable attention at home. The speech focused on strategic autonomy and the importance of a credible deterrent, aligning with broader discussions at the conference regarding European defense capabilities.

Key points:
*   The Prime Minister called for Europe to enhance its readiness for potential conflict.
*   He advocated for greater defense investment and coordinated security policies.
*   The speech occurred amid a difficult period in the leader's domestic political situation.
