The United Kingdom will deploy the Royal Navy destroyer HMS Dragon to the eastern Mediterranean, with Cyprus as its base of operations. The announcement was made by the British government, which emphasized its ongoing commitment to regional security and the protection of its military assets in the area. The Type 45 destroyer will operate in support of existing British forces stationed at RAF Akrotiri on the island. Officials stated the deployment is part of broader, pre-planned military activity and is intended to contribute to stability in the region. No specific timeline for the deployment was provided in the initial statement.

Key points:
- The UK is deploying the HMS Dragon, a Type 45 destroyer, to be based out of Cyprus.
- The government cited a commitment to Cypriot security and the protection of British personnel at RAF Akrotiri.
- The move is described as part of planned military activity aimed at supporting regional stability.
