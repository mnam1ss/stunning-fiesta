**Inquest Opens into Holiday Park Carbon Monoxide Deaths**

An inquest has heard that two teenagers died from suspected carbon monoxide poisoning while on holiday. The hearing was told that Cherish Bean, 18, and Ethan Slater, 19, were found deceased at a holiday park in North Yorkshire earlier this year. According to testimony presented to the coroner, Ms. Bean had complained of a headache shortly before the incident. Emergency services were called to the scene, where the presence of the odorless gas was investigated. The inquest, which is examining the full circumstances of the deaths, has been adjourned to allow for further investigation. Authorities continue to look into the source of the suspected leak at the accommodation.

**Key points:**
*   An inquest heard two teenagers died from suspected carbon monoxide poisoning at a UK holiday park.
*   The hearing was told one victim reported a headache prior to the incident.
*   The proceedings have been paused for further investigation into the circumstances.
