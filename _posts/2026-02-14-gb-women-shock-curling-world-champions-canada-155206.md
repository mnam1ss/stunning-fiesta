Great Britain’s women’s curling team opened their 2026 Winter Olympic campaign with a significant victory on Tuesday, defeating reigning world champions Canada by a score of 7-6. The match, held at the Olympic venue, was closely contested throughout. The British squad secured the win in the final end, overcoming a Canadian team widely considered a favorite for the podium. This result marks a notable early upset in the women’s curling tournament at the Milano Cortina Games.

Key points:
*   Great Britain defeated Canada 7-6 in their opening match of the 2026 Winter Olympics.
*   The victory is considered an upset, as Canada entered the tournament as the reigning world champions.
*   The win provides a strong start for the British team in the Olympic curling competition.
