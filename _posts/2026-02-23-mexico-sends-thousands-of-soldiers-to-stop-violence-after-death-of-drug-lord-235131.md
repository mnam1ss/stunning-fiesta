**Mexico Deploys Military to Curb Cartel Violence Following Leader's Death**

The Mexican government has deployed thousands of soldiers and National Guard members to several states in response to a surge in violent incidents. This security operation follows the death of a high-ranking leader of a major drug trafficking organization. The individual died shortly after being apprehended by state security forces.

Officials report that the death has triggered widespread unrest attributed to cartel affiliates, including vehicle burnings and armed clashes in multiple regions. The military deployment aims to restore order and reestablish public security in the affected areas. Authorities have not provided a specific timeline for the operation but emphasize it is part of ongoing efforts to combat organized criminal groups.

Key points:
*   Military forces have been mobilized in response to escalating violence after a cartel boss died post-capture.
*   The unrest has involved acts such as arson and confrontations in various parts of the country.
*   The government states the deployment is a measure to stabilize regions impacted by the cartel's reaction.
