China has announced its lowest annual economic growth target in over three decades. The government set the goal for gross domestic product expansion at approximately 5% for the year, matching the target established in 2023. This marks the first instance in two years that the objective has not been raised, maintaining the same level as the previous year's aim.

Officials outlined the target during the opening of the National People's Congress, the country's annual parliamentary session. The figure represents a continuation of a moderated growth approach amid domestic and international economic challenges. Analysts note that achieving this goal will require sustained policy support, with a focus on technological advancement and stabilizing the property sector.

Key points:
*   China's GDP growth target for the year remains at about 5%, unchanged from 2023.
*   This is the lowest such goal set by the country since the year 1991.
*   The announcement was made during the key annual legislative gathering in Beijing.
