**Bail Conditions Lifted for Peer Following Arrest**

Lawyers representing Lord Peter Mandelson have confirmed that police have returned his passport and lifted all bail conditions. The peer had been subject to these restrictions following his arrest earlier this year on suspicion of misconduct in public office.

The arrest was connected to an investigation by the Metropolitan Police. Authorities have not provided further details on the nature of the alleged misconduct. A spokesperson for Lord Mandelson stated that he has cooperated fully with the police inquiry throughout the process.

The lifting of bail conditions does not signify the conclusion of the investigation. Police have indicated that inquiries are ongoing, and no charges have been filed at this time. Lord Mandelson, a former cabinet minister and European Commissioner, has not made a public statement regarding the latest development.

Key points:
*   Lord Peter Mandelson's bail conditions have been removed and his passport returned.
*   He was arrested earlier this year on suspicion of misconduct in public office.
*   The police investigation remains active, and no charges have been brought.
