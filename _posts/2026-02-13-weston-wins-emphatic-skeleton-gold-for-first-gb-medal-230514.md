British skeleton athlete Matt Weston secured the first gold medal for Team Great Britain at the 2026 Winter Olympics in Cortina on Tuesday. Weston’s victory marks a historic achievement in the sport for his nation.

He delivered a dominant performance across the competition’s four heats, finishing with a combined time that placed him clearly ahead of the silver medalist. The win solidifies his standing as a top competitor in international skeleton racing this season.

The medal is a significant early success for the British team at these Games. Official results and final timings are expected to be published by the International Olympic Committee later today.

Key points:
• Matt Weston won Olympic gold in the men's skeleton event.
• This is the first medal for Team GB at the 2026 Winter Games.
• The victory occurred in Cortina with a commanding performance.
