A United States military refueling aircraft crashed in western Iraq on Friday, resulting in the deaths of all four crew members on board, according to U.S. Central Command. The incident occurred during a routine operational mission. Officials have stated that the cause of the crash is under investigation and that no hostile fire was involved.

Search and recovery operations are ongoing at the crash site. The aircraft, a KC-135 Stratotanker, is used for aerial refueling of other military planes. The names of the deceased service members are being withheld pending notification of their families. The U.S. military has emphasized its commitment to supporting the families of the fallen and to conducting a thorough investigation into the accident.

Key points:
• A U.S. KC-135 refueling plane crashed in western Iraq, killing all four crew members.
• U.S. Central Command states the crash was not due to enemy action and remains under investigation.
• Recovery efforts continue, and the families of the deceased are being notified.
