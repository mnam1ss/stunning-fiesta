Several major airlines have altered flight paths and canceled services to and from the Middle East following military strikes involving Iran. The operational changes are a direct response to the heightened regional tensions and ongoing security assessments.

Carriers including British Airways, Virgin Atlantic, and Wizz Air have implemented significant schedule revisions. These adjustments primarily affect routes that would traverse airspace near the conflict zone. Some flights have been diverted to alternative airports, while others have been grounded entirely.

Aviation authorities and airlines are closely monitoring the situation, prioritizing passenger and crew safety. The disruptions are expected to continue until security conditions are deemed stable for normal flight operations.

Key points:
• Multiple airlines, including British Airways and Virgin Atlantic, have revised flight schedules.
• Cancellations and diversions are focused on routes using airspace affected by regional tensions.
• The changes are a precautionary safety measure with no specified end date.
