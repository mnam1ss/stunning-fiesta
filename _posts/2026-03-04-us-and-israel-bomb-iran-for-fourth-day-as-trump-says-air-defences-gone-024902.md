US and Israeli forces have conducted a fourth consecutive day of airstrikes on targets within Iran, according to military statements. The operation follows earlier exchanges and marks a significant escalation in regional hostilities. Former US President Donald Trump commented on the situation, stating that Iranian air defense systems appeared to be non-operational, though this claim has not been independently verified by military officials.

In retaliation, Iranian forces have initiated their own offensive measures. Reports indicate the launch of ballistic missiles and unmanned aerial vehicles directed at Israel. Additional attacks have targeted several Gulf states that host permanent US military installations, raising concerns over the widening scope of the conflict.

Key points:
- US and Israeli airstrikes in Iran have continued for a fourth day.
- Iran has retaliated with missile and drone attacks on Israel and US bases in the Gulf.
- The ongoing exchanges represent a major intensification of regional tensions.
