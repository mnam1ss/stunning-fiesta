Israeli military forces conducted an overnight raid in a southern Lebanese village, resulting in significant casualties, according to Lebanese authorities. The operation was reportedly aimed at recovering the remains of an individual missing for four decades. Lebanon's health ministry stated that at least 41 people were killed and 40 others wounded during the incident.

The Israeli military has not released an official statement regarding the specific objectives or details of the raid. The operation marks a serious escalation in cross-border tensions between Israel and Lebanon, which have seen increased exchanges of fire in recent months. The area of the operation is near the UN-drawn Blue Line, a boundary between the two countries.

Key points:
*   A nighttime Israeli military raid in southern Lebanon led to dozens of casualties, per Lebanese officials.
*   The stated objective was to retrieve remains missing since the 1980s.
*   The incident occurs amid heightened regional tensions and ongoing border clashes.
