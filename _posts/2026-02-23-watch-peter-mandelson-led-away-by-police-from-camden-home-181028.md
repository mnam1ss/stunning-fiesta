Former Labour minister Peter Mandelson was detained by police at his residence in north London on Tuesday morning. The Metropolitan Police confirmed that officers arrested a 72-year-old man at an address in the Camden area. He was taken into custody on suspicion of misconduct in a public office, a criminal offense relating to a breach of trust by a public official.

The arrest followed a pre-arranged appointment. Lord Mandelson, who served in cabinet roles under Prime Ministers Tony Blair and Gordon Brown, was later released on bail pending further inquiries. Police have not provided specific details about the allegations. A representative for Lord Mandelson stated he is cooperating fully with the investigation.

Key points:
• Former cabinet minister Peter Mandelson was arrested at his London home.
• He was detained on suspicion of misconduct in a public office.
• The Metropolitan Police investigation is ongoing; Mandelson has been released on bail.
