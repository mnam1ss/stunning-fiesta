Prime Minister Keir Starmer held a phone conversation with former U.S. President Donald Trump on Monday, with discussions focusing on international shipping security. According to a statement from the Prime Minister's office, the two leaders addressed the ongoing disruptions affecting maritime routes in the Middle East.

The conversation centered on the strategic Strait of Hormuz, a critical waterway for global oil shipments. Officials stated that both figures emphasized the necessity of restoring safe and open passage for commercial vessels in the region to ensure the stability of worldwide trade networks. The disruption to shipping lanes has raised concerns over potential impacts on energy markets and supply chains.

The readout from Downing Street did not specify any concrete policy proposals or actions arising from the discussion. The call marks one of Starmer's first publicly acknowledged diplomatic engagements with the former president since taking office.

Key points:
*   Prime Minister Keir Starmer and former President Donald Trump discussed maritime security in a phone call.
*   The talks emphasized reopening the Strait of Hormuz to end global shipping disruptions.
*   The UK government's statement highlighted the importance of stable trade routes but did not announce new policies.
