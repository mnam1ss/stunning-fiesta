Former Deputy Prime Minister Nick Clegg has described the current system for university tuition fees in England as a "mess." In an interview with the BBC, Clegg acknowledged his role in the policy's development and stated he would accept any resulting criticism.

The tuition fee system, which was significantly reformed during the coalition government from 2010 to 2015, has been a subject of ongoing political debate. Clegg, who was a key figure in that government, has previously expressed regret over a pledge to oppose fee increases, which was later abandoned. His latest comments come amid continued discussion about student debt and the funding of higher education.

Key points:
- Nick Clegg, the former deputy prime minister, has called the university tuition fee system a "mess."
- He stated he would accept criticism for his involvement in the policy's creation.
- The comments revisit a contentious political issue from the 2010-2015 coalition government.
