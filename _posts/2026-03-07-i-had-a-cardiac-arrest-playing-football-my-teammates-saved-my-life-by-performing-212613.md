**Football Match Turns to Rescue as Teammates Administer CPR**

A routine football match in Oxfordshire became a life-saving intervention last week when a player suffered a cardiac arrest on the field. The individual, identified as a local general practitioner, collapsed during the game.

According to reports, fellow players immediately initiated cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) while emergency services were called. Their prompt action is credited with sustaining the man’s life until paramedics arrived and provided advanced medical care. The patient was subsequently transported to a hospital for treatment.

Medical professionals emphasize that immediate bystander CPR can dramatically increase survival rates from cardiac arrest, which can occur suddenly even in individuals with no prior known heart conditions. The incident highlights the importance of widespread CPR training.

**Key points:**
*   A doctor experienced a cardiac arrest while playing football in Oxfordshire.
*   Teammates performed immediate CPR before emergency services arrived.
*   The incident underscores the critical role of bystander intervention in cardiac emergencies.
