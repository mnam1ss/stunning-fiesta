Bad Bunny made history at the Grammy Awards by becoming the first artist to win a major category while performing entirely in Spanish. The ceremony, however, was marked by several political statements from artists regarding U.S. immigration policy. Numerous attendees wore red pins calling for the abolition of Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE).

The evening also saw awards presented to other prominent artists. Billie Eilish, Olivia Dean, and Lady Gaga were among the musicians recognized for their work across various genres. The event highlighted a blend of musical achievement and social activism, with performers using the platform to address current political issues.

Key points:
*   Bad Bunny became the first artist to win a major Grammy with a Spanish-language performance.
*   The event featured political protests, including calls to abolish ICE.
*   Other winners included Billie Eilish, Olivia Dean, and Lady Gaga.
