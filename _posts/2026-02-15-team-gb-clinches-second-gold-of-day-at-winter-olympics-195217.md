**British Skeleton Duo Secures Historic Gold at Winter Olympics**

CORTINA, Italy â€“ The British Olympic team earned its second gold medal of the day at the Winter Games on Thursday, achieving a historic milestone in the skeleton event. Athlete Matt Weston, paired with teammate Tabitha Stoecker, secured victory in the inaugural mixed team skeleton competition.

This win marks a significant achievement for Great Britain, as Weston becomes the first athlete from the nation to win two gold medals at a single Winter Olympics. He previously topped the podium in the men's individual skeleton event earlier in the Games. The mixed team event, featuring one male and one female slider from each nation combining their times, concluded with the British pair finishing ahead of their competitors.

Key points:
*   Matt Weston and Tabitha Stoecker won gold in the new mixed team skeleton event.
*   Weston is now the first British athlete to earn two gold medals at one Winter Olympics.
*   The victory provided Team GB with its second gold medal of the day in Cortina.
