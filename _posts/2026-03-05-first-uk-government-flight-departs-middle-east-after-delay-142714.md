A UK government-chartered flight has departed from Oman after a delay of several days. The aircraft, which was scheduled to leave on Wednesday, was held on the ground due to logistical issues related to passenger boarding. The flight is part of ongoing efforts to facilitate travel from the region. Officials have not provided specific details regarding the nature of the boarding complications or the number of passengers involved. The departure marks the resumption of this specific repatriation or transport operation following the unresolved delay.

Key points:
*   A UK-chartered flight left Oman after missing its original Wednesday departure date.
*   The delay was caused by difficulties in getting passengers onto the aircraft.
*   The flight represents the first UK government-operated airlift from the Middle East to proceed after this setback.
