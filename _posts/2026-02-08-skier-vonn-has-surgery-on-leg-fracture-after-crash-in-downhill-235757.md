American alpine skier Lindsey Vonn underwent surgery for a fractured left leg following a crash during the women's downhill competition in Cortina d'Ampezzo, Italy. The incident occurred during what was widely anticipated to be the final Olympic event of her career. Medical personnel attended to Vonn immediately after the fall, and she was transported from the course for further evaluation.

The successful surgery was confirmed by her team, though a specific timeline for recovery has not been provided. Vonn, a former Olympic gold medalist and World Cup champion, has faced several significant injuries throughout her career, including previous knee surgeries. This latest setback comes during a season where she had aimed to compete at a high level ahead of the upcoming Winter Games.

Key points:
• Lindsey Vonn sustained a left-leg fracture in a crash at a downhill event in Cortina.
• She has undergone surgery for the injury, which occurred in a potential final Olympic race.
• The champion skier has a history of serious injuries, including multiple knee operations.
